## Chapter One — New Beginning
_(Year 2021)_

The attack began with precision.

Not rage.

Not spectacle.

Coordination.

A biotech complex on the edge of the city — Helix Dynamics — has been under federal contract since the Sentinel War. Publicly, it manufactured adaptive prosthetics and energy shielding.

Privately, it stored something else.

Mutant DNA archives.

Not illegal.

Not exactly.

But extensive.

And vulnerable.

### The Strike

The first tremor hit at 8:14 p.m.

Concrete rippled like disturbed water.

Glass fractured in perfect lines.

Avalanche stood across the street, fingers flexing rhythmically, sending controlled seismic pulses through the building’s foundation.

Not collapse.

Destabilization.

Across the plaza, crowds began to scatter.

From the rooftop opposite, Pyro snapped his fingers, drawing flame from ruptured gas lines and bending it into a burning perimeter wall — not wild, but sculpted.

Containment.

On the ground level, Juggernaut walked straight through reinforced security gates. Steel twisted around him like a foil.

Security teams attempted to respond.

They found themselves trapped in illusions.

Mastermind stood calmly inside the lobby, projecting chaos where there were none — phantom explosions, invisible attackers, distorted reality.

Civilians panicked.

And darting between vehicles, disrupting responders, was Toad, leaping from surface to surface with manic efficiency.

On the balcony of an adjacent high-rise stood Emma Frost.

Hands folded.

Observing.

This was not destruction.

It was a statement.

Helix Dynamics had cataloged mutant genomes under the justification of “medical preparedness.”

Emma disagreed with centralized control.

If mutants were to govern themselves, then autonomy would have to be demonstrated.

She pressed the communicator lightly.

“Phase two.”

Inside the complex, reinforced vault doors buckled.

### The Response

A shadow passed over the plaza.

The Blackbird descended between smoke columns.

It landed cleanly.

No dramatic hover.

No hesitation.

The hatch opened.

Silver hair caught the morning light first.

Pietro Maximoff stepped down.

Behind him:

- Iceman
- Colossus
- Psylocke
- Magik
- Kitty Pryde
- Jubilee
- Archangel

Cameras pivoted.

Civilians stared.

Pietro inhaled sharply.

Straightened.

“To me, my X-Men—”

Illyana glanced sideways.

“You know we are not your X-Men.”

A pause.

“…Right,” Pietro muttered. “Still calibrating.”

He turned instantly serious.

“You know the drill. Evac first. Contain. No collateral.”

He pointed.

“Bobby — freeze fire lanes. Piotr — breach control. Betsy — neutralize illusions. Illyana — flank entry. Kitty — extraction assist. Jubilee — visual suppression. Warren — air support.”

Beat.

“I’ll handle evacuation. I will be back as fast as I can.”

Another beat.

“…Wait.”

He smirked.

“I’m fast.”

And he vanished.

### Strategic Chaos

Pietro reappeared inside the lobby, relocating civilians faster than Mastermind’s illusions could adapt.

Psylocke stepped forward, psychic blade igniting violet. She cut through fabricated realities like silk. False explosions flickered and vanished. The panic in the room steadied.

“Ground them,” she said calmly.

Outside, Iceman pulled moisture from the air in a spiraling draw, flash-freezing Pyro’s flames into towering crystalline barriers. Steam roared between them.

“You still overextend,” Bobby called.

Pyro smirked. “You still hesitate.”

Bobby didn’t bite. He redirected the fire upward, sealing it into ice rather than counterattacking.

Containment over domination.

Growth.

Colossus met Juggernaut head-on.

Metal collided with unstoppable momentum.

Concrete split beneath their feet.

Piotr did not try to overpower him.

He braced.

Anchored.

Absorbed the charge.

“Portal. Now!”

Magik’s stepping disc opened beneath Juggernaut mid-stride.

He disappeared—

Reappearing inside a reinforced containment yard pre-coordinated with city response units. Magnetic clamps locked into place the second he landed.

Juggernaut roared.

But he wasn’t in the plaza anymore.

Strategic relocation.

Avalanche altered his rhythm, increasing frequency.

Kitty phased through collapsing stairwells, guiding trapped employees through walls.

Jubilee detonated controlled plasma bursts — not explosions, but blinding arcs of light that forced Toad off balance long enough for Archangel to descend in a precise dive, pinning him against reinforced concrete.

Toad struggled.

He didn’t escape.

Psylocke turned her attention toward Mastermind. His illusions twisted again — but this time they collapsed instantly under focused psychic pressure.

His knees buckled.

Neutralized.

Avalanche barely registered for Pietro’s arrival.

A blur.

Pressure strikes to nerve clusters.

Pietro relocated him behind a portable containment barrier before the tremors could re-synchronize.

“Telegraphing your rhythm,” Pietro muttered.

Outside, Iceman froze Pyro’s flame constructs into solid towers, locking him in a cage of his own element.

Within minutes—

The fire was gone.

The illusions dissolved.

The tremors ceased.

Helix vault doors remained sealed.

Damage minimal.

Civilians safe.

Only one figure remained untouched.

Emma Frost.

She stood on the balcony, immaculate.

Unharmed.

Unbothered.

Pietro appeared behind her.

“You’re done.”

Emma didn’t turn immediately.

“You’ve improved,” she said lightly.

Psylocke stepped onto the balcony beside him, psychic energy steady but controlled.

Emma finally turned.

Her skin shimmered — diamond.

Brilliant.

Unbreakable.

For a moment, tension sharpened.

Then Emma’s form softened.

Diamond faded.

She extended her hands calmly.

“I was curious how efficiently you would respond.”

Magnetic restraints secured around her wrists.

No struggle.

No spectacle.

Just compliance.

### Aftermath

Police units arrived in organized waves.

Media drones hovered but kept distance.

Juggernaut roared in containment several blocks away.

Pyro sat encased in ice.

Avalanche restrained.

Mastermind subdued.

Toad secured.

Emma Frost escorted with precision.

The plaza did not burn.

It did not collapse.

It stabilized.

Pietro stood at the center of it all, breathing slightly heavier than he wanted to admit.

He surveyed his team.

“Good containment,” he said. “Magik — tighten portal timing. Bobby — commit half a second sooner. Jubilee — flare angles near glass lines. Archangel — lower descent vector.”

He looked at Colossus.

“You held the line.”

Then to Psylocke.

“Clean sweep.”

Illyana crossed her arms.

“You nearly let Avalanche destabilize the west wing.”

Pietro rolled his eyes.

“Nearly.”

A pause.

He looked around at emergency responders stabilizing infrastructure.

Civilians nodding.

Reporters speaking words like _efficient_, _coordinated_, _heroic_.

Mutants visible.

Accepted.

Trusted.

He allowed himself a small, satisfied smile.

“Not bad.”

Emma paused before entering a transport unit.

She glanced back once — not at the police.

At Pietro.

Measured.

Assessing.

Then she stepped inside.

The doors shut.

The Blackbird cut through the morning sky.

Inside, the team finally exhaled.

Adrenaline settling into something steadier.

They had acted without Scott.

Without Jean.

Without Logan.

The city had not fallen apart.

It did not require saving by legends.

The new generation handled it.

Below them, the skyline remained intact.

And since the Sentinel War—

Mutants weren’t reacting.

They were leading.

## Chapter Two — The Quiet Morning

The estate was quiet in the early light.

Mist still clung to the lower fields beyond the North Campus, and the main grounds of the Xavier Institute lay in a kind of disciplined calm — trimmed hedges, open walkways, distant laughter from early-rising students.

Scott Summers ran the perimeter path like he always did.

Measured pace. Controlled breathing. Even stride.

Five miles. No music. No distractions.

Running wasn’t about endurance anymore. It was about clarity.

He ran the perimeter of the original estate; the mansion rising beside him as sunlight caught the school’s glass and scattered across the lawn.

He increased speed during the final stretch.

Later, on the terrace, Scott dropped into pushups. Clean sets. No wasted motion.

Then squats. Weighted carries. Core holds.

He finished by standing still in the sunlight, breathing slow and deep, letting warmth settle across his shoulders. The visor remained in place, as always. The world stayed red through it — but he had learned to see clearly enough.

Inside, the house smelled like coffee.

After a quick shower, Scott moved down the hall toward the kitchen.

Jean stood by the counter, one hand resting gently on her stomach as she stirred something in a pan.

She looked tired.

But peaceful.

Scott stepped behind her, wrapping his arms carefully around her waist.

“Good morning,” he murmured, pressing a kiss on the side of her head.

“Morning,” Jean replied, smiling faintly. “You run the earth into submission yet?”

“Almost.”

She leaned back slightly into him.

“He’s been restless,” she said softly.

Scott’s hand moved instinctively to her stomach.

“He’s just eager,” he said. “Takes after his mother.”

Jean gave him a look.

“I was calm.”

“You threw a car at someone once.”

“That was different.”

He smiled.

They stayed like that for a moment longer — quiet, grounded.

After breakfast, they moved through the main hall together.

Logan was already there, leaning against a column with coffee in hand. Ororo stood near the windows, speaking quietly with Anna (Rogue). Kevin (Morph) paced in loose circles, talking to himself about something only half-related to anything.

“Morning,” Ororo greeted.

“Morning,” Scott replied.

“Still intact?” Logan asked, nodding toward Jean.

“For now,” Jean said.

“Calvin is still on the way,” Anna added. “Traffic.”

Scott nodded.

And then—

Jean froze.

Her expression shifted — not panic, not fear.

Recognition.

Her hand tightened around Scott’s.

“Scott…”

There was a faint sound.

A rush.

Then warmth spreading down her legs.

Scott blinked.

“Jean?”

She looked at him.

“I think it’s time.”

Everything moved at once.

### The Hospital

The delivery was long — but not catastrophic.

Ororo remained calm. Anna kept Scott from pacing holes in the floor. Logan stood apart; arms crossed, silent but watchful.

When the cry finally came from inside the room, it cut through every tension in the hall.

Scott didn’t realize he’d been holding his breath until he exhaled.

A nurse stepped out.

“Mother and child are stable.”

Scott went in.

Jean lay back against the pillows, exhausted but radiant in a way no battle had ever made her.

The nurse placed the child into Scott’s arms.

Small.

Fragile.

Perfect.

Scott swallowed hard.

“Nate,” he said quietly. “Nathan Summers.”

Jean smiled faintly.

Scott lowered his forehead briefly to the baby’s head.

Outside the room, the others waited.

And in the hallway beyond them, another nurse approached.

Logan’s head tilted slightly.

The scent was wrong.

Not hostile.

Just wrong.

The nurse entered the room calmly.

“Mr. Summers,” he said politely. “We need to take the baby for further evaluation.”

Scott nodded, distracted, and began to turn—

“Scott. No.” Logan’s voice was sharp.

Scott stopped.

Jean’s head snapped up.

The nurse’s posture stiffened.

Then froze.

Jean’s eyes narrowed.

Telepathic grip.

The nurse stood rigid, unable to move.

Scott looked between them.

“What’s happening?”

And then—

A faint red mark began to bloom on the nurse’s forehead.

Diamond-shaped.

Precise.

The nurse’s lips curled into a smile that did not belong to him.

“Remember me, Scott Summers?”

Scott’s face went cold.

“…Essex.”

Logan moved instantly, grabbing the man by the collar and lifting him off the ground.

“What do you want, bub?”

Jean’s voice cut through.

“Logan, stop. He’s not in control. He’s just a conduit. Someone’s projecting from far away.”

Logan hesitated, then lowered the man roughly to his feet but kept a grip on him.

Scott stepped forward.

“Why are you here?”

The possessed nurse tilted his head slightly.

“Your family grows. Congratulations.”

Scott’s jaw tightened.

“What did you do?”

A pause.

“Do you remember your little brother, Alex?”

Scott’s body went rigid.

“What did you do to him?” he demanded.

“Nothing. Yet.”

The smile widened.

“But I would very much like to keep it that way.”

Scott handed the baby carefully to Logan without looking away and moved forward towards the man.

Logan held Nate surprisingly steady.

“If you want him safe,” the nurse continued, “you will bring me the child.”

Scott’s voice dropped.

“That’s not happening.”

“Then I suppose I will begin with your brother instead. Which would be unfortunate. One of them is far more promising.”

Scott’s fists clenched.

Jean felt his anger spike.

“If you change your mind,” Essex said calmly, “come to the old coastal refinery outside Providence. I will be waiting.”

The red diamond flared briefly.

Then vanished.

The nurse collapsed, unconscious.

Scott caught him before he hit the ground.

Ororo and Anna rushed in. Kevin stood at the doorway, stunned.

Jean closed her eyes, steadying herself.

Scott stared at the unconscious man in his arms.

He didn’t feel fear.

He felt something worse.

Calculated rage.

And beneath it—

Unease.

Because Essex had not sounded desperate.

He sounded prepared.

## Chapter Three — What Sinister Built

The lower lab was quiet except for the hum of scanners.

Jean sat upright on the diagnostic platform, Nate asleep against her chest. Scott stood close, one hand resting lightly against her shoulder. Logan leaned against the far counter, arms folded. Ororo, Anna, Kevin, and Calvin formed a loose semicircle around them.

Hank studied the readouts carefully.

“Cellular structure is normal,” he said at last. “No foreign markers. No dormant triggers. Nate’s genetic sequencing is precisely what we would expect.”

Scott didn’t realize he had been holding tension in his jaw until it eased.

“And Jean?” Storm asked.

Hank glanced at her. “Strong. Slight fatigue from delivery. Nothing abnormal.”

Jean nodded once. “He didn’t take anything.”

“No,” Hank agreed quietly. “If he wanted samples, he had an ample opportunity. The hospital confrontation was not acquisition. It was communication.”

The room absorbed that.

Upstairs, the Institute continued its rhythm. Classes were in session.

The institution was functioning.

But here, beneath it, something old had resurfaced.

Charles entered the lab slowly.

“The North HQ is on alert,” he said evenly. “The New X-Men will maintain perimeter readiness. X-Force stands by should this expand beyond containment. But for now…” His gaze settled on Scott. “This is something we must address ourselves.”

Scott nodded once.

“We don’t bring the school into this,” he said. “Not unless we have to.”

Logan shifted his weight. “That refinery’s bait.”

Hank pulled up a projection on the central screen.

An image of the old coastal refinery outside Providence filled the display.

“Decommissioned nearly fifteen years ago,” Hank said. “Multiple shell companies have owned it since. Recent activity suggests power rerouting beneath the main structure. Sublevel construction. Shielded infrastructure.”

Ororo studied the image. “He chose isolation.”

Scott’s eyes stayed fixed on the refinery.

“He chose control.”

Charles turned then — not to Scott.

To Kevin.

The shift was subtle, but deliberate.

“Morph,” Charles said gently. “It is time.”

Kevin stiffened slightly.

Scott looked between them. “Time for what?”

Kevin exhaled slowly.

“I know him,” he said.

Silence followed.

“I don’t mean I’ve read reports. I mean I know him.”

Logan’s posture changed almost imperceptibly.

“What do you mean?” Scott asked.

Kevin stepped forward.

“He took me. Years ago. Before I ever came here.”

Anna inhaled sharply.

“He didn’t like what I was,” Morph continued. “A shapeshifter with a stable form. Predictable baseline.” He swallowed. “He destabilized it.”

Ororo’s eyes narrowed. “How?”

“Accelerated cellular plasticity, stripped anchor points in my genetic structure, pushed my mutation beyond its natural tolerance.” Kevin’s voice tightened. “I don’t have a default anymore because he made sure I wouldn’t.”

Scott’s hands curled into fists.

“He experiments on himself too,” Morph said quietly. “That’s the part you need to understand.”

Jean looked up sharply. “What do you mean?”

“He’s not just a man with funding and labs,” Morph replied. “He modifies himself. His physiology doesn’t behave normally. You hurt him…” He paused. “He adapts.”

Logan tilted his head. “Healing factor?”

“More than that. Structural corrections, like his body recalibrates damage.”

Hank’s eyes sharpened. “Self-engineered cellular restructuring.”

“He can teleport short distances,” Kevin continued. “Not theatrically. Efficiently. He’s shielded telepathically — layered, engineered. Not stronger than you, Jean. But protected.”

Jean nodded slowly. “The projection felt compartmentalized. Artificial barriers.”

“He’s not human anymore,” Morph said quietly. “Maybe he never intended to stay that way.”

Scott’s voice was low.

“The orphanage.”

All eyes shifted to him.

“It was funded by Essex Corp,” Scott said. “After my parents died. Routine medical exams. Bloodwork. Psychological profiling.” His jaw tightened. “Alex was adopted young. He doesn’t even remember I exist. He never went through any of that.”

Anna frowned. “You think he flagged you?”

“I know he did,” Scott replied.

Hank brought up archived corporate records.

“Essex Corp has quietly acquired genetic databases for years. Private clinics. Orphanage networks. Research institutions. All legal. All insulated.”

Logan added, “Mutants have been disappearing. Quietly. No headlines. Clinics shutting down overnight.”

Ororo’s voice cut cleanly through the tension. “And now he invites you to his territory.”

“It’s a trap,” Anna said.

Scott met her gaze.

“Yes.”

Anna’s expression is steady now.

“He doesn’t fight impulsively. He tests. Measures. Predicts. That refinery won’t just be a lab. It’ll be layered. Industrial shell, clean interior, sublevels beneath.”

Hank nodded. “Multiple access points, reinforced steel infrastructure, environmental control systems.”

Scott looked at the image again.

“He chose metal and isolation.”

Logan’s eyes flicked briefly toward him.

Ororo spoke softly. “He expects a response.”

“He expects data,” Kevin corrected.

Scott turned back to the group.

“We don’t trade Nate.”

Immediate agreement.

Jean’s voice was calm but firm. “We bring Alex home.”

“The North HQ remains operational,” Scott continued. “New X-Men hold perimeter. X-Force stays on standby. If this spreads beyond containment, they move.”

Charles inclined his head.

Scott’s gaze swept across the room.

“Who’s coming?”

No hesitation.

“Jean. Logan. Storm. Rogue. Morph. Mimic. Me.”

Hank nodded. “I’ll monitor remotely. Signal disruption, structural mapping, thermal overlays.”

Jean shifted Nate gently into her arms.

Scott stepped closer, resting his hand briefly against his son’s head.

“He knew about Nate before tonight,” Scott said quietly.

Kevin answered without humor.

“He always knows before.”

Scott looked once more at the refinery’s rusted towers silhouetted against the ocean.

“Then let him watch,” he said.

“And let him see what happens next.”

## Chapter Four — The Shadow in Steel

The Blackbird descended without lights.

Night swallowed the coastline whole.

The old refinery stood against the dark ocean like a rusted skeleton — towers leaning into the wind, pipes cutting jagged silhouettes across the sky. Waves crashed somewhere beyond the steel labyrinth.

The jet settled quietly behind a ridge of broken storage tanks.

The hatch opened.

Scott stepped down first.

Visor glinting faintly in the moonlight.

Logan followed. Storm. Rogue. Morph. Mimic. Jean.

No one spoke.

They moved in formation toward the main industrial yard.

Wind carried salt and metal.

Storm subtly lifted her hands. The air shifted. Static thinned. External power grids flickered out in distant, dying pulses.

Hank’s voice came through Scott’s earpiece.

“Thermal scans are irregular. Multiple cold spots. No clear central heat signature.”

“Understood.”

Jean closed her eyes briefly as they advanced.

“I don’t feel him.”

“Shielded?” Rogue asked.

“Or patient.”

They approached the central concrete expanse between refinery towers.

Metal groaned somewhere overhead.

Logan slowed.

He sniffed the air once.

Twice.

Something tugged at instinct.

Not a scent.

Absence.

He turned his head slightly, scanning the dark.

Nothing.

“You good?” Scott asked without looking back.

Logan hesitated.

“…Yeah.”

They resumed.

The wind shifted.

And then—

The ground behind Jean rippled.

Not violently.

Subtly.

Like oil separating from water.

A dark shape peeled upward from the concrete itself.

No footsteps.

No breath.

No heartbeat spike.

Just a shadow detaching from shadow.

Before Logan could turn—

A pale hand clamped over Jean’s shoulder.

Another snapped a collar around her throat.

Click.

Jean gasped.

Her eyes flared — telekinetic force beginning to gather —

The collar pulsed.

A needle snapped forward against her neck.

The pulse intensified.

Her focus shattered.

The world tilted.

“Scott—”

Her knees buckled.

Mr. Sinister caught her effortlessly.

The team spun.

Logan lowered his stance—

Too late.

Storm’s eyes ignited white.

Scott’s visor locked onto the figure now standing among them.

Mr. Sinister stepped fully into the moonlight.

Immaculate.

Unhurried.

Calm.

“Her mind is formidable,” Sinister said almost conversationally. “But even formidable systems require balance.”

Logan’s claws snapped out.

“You’re dead.”

“I assure you,” Sinister replied evenly, “I am very much not.”

Scott held position, calculating angles.

“Release her.”

Sinister regarded him with clinical interest.

“You are predictable, Scott Summers. That is both your strength… and your flaw.”

Jean’s breathing slowed, consciousness slipping.

Sinister lowered her to the concrete like a specimen being placed upon a table.

“She will recover,” he said. “Eventually.”

Storm stepped forward—

And then—

**BAMF.**

Sulfur and displaced air cracked through the yard.

A blur of blue.

Kurt Wagner appeared beside Jean.

He did not speak.

He did not hesitate.

He touched her shoulder—

**BAMF.**

They vanished.

They reappeared behind a collapsed pipeline nearly fifty meters away. Kurt lowered Jean carefully against rusted steel.

His hands moved to the collar.

It hummed faintly.

“Professor,” he whispered into his comm, voice steady but urgent. “It is worse than anticipated.”

Jean’s eyes fluttered weakly.

“Kurt…”

“I have you,” he said softly. “Rest.”

He looked back toward the battlefield.

Mr. Sinister stood exactly where he had been.

Watching.

No anger.

No surprise.

Only recalculation.

“Ah,” Sinister murmured. “Redundancy.”

He turned his gaze back to Scott.

“Very wise of you.”

Scott’s voice cut sharply across the yard.

“Storm — air control. Rogue — left flank. Logan with me. Keep him boxed.”

Orders snapped into place.

Logan moved first.

Behind Sinister, the shadows thickened.

Figures stepped forward.

Humanoid.

Expressionless.

Genetically engineered constructs with reinforced musculature and hollow, unblinking eyes.

Clones.

Not identical.

Not complete.

Tools.

Rogue muttered under her breath, “Oh hell.”

Sinister stepped back slightly, hands folding behind him.

“Shall we begin?”

The clones charged.

Logan met the first with a slash that should have ended it.

The creature staggered—

Then corrected.

Rogue absorbed the second’s strength and hurled it through rusted scaffolding.

Storm unleashed a crosswind that tore two from their footing and drove them into twisted piping.

Morph shifted mid-stride, density increasing, striking with bone-jarring force.

Mimic mirrored Rogue’s borrowed power and crushed another against concrete.

Scott controlled lanes of fire with disciplined optic bursts, carving precision arcs that forced the constructs into predictable vectors.

Sinister did not move.

He watched.

Measured.

Adjusted.

And then—

From the refinery entrance, a lone figure stepped forward.

Hands glowing faintly.

Energy building.

Eyes distant.

Controlled.

A red diamond-shaped mark burned at the center of his forehead.

Alex.

Scott froze for half a heartbeat.

The mark pulsed once.

The clones pressed harder.

Alex lifted his hands.

Energy flared.

The battle had barely begun.

And they were already reacting.

Alex stepped fully into the yard.

Energy swelled around his hands — incandescent rings containing plasma.

The red diamond on his forehead pulsed faintly.

“Alex,” Scott said.

No response.

Just calculation behind unfamiliar eyes.

Alex fired.

A concentrated blast tore across the yard toward Scott.

Scott didn’t dodge.

He angled his visor forward—

The impact slammed into him like a freight train.

Concrete cracked beneath his boots.

Energy roared around him.

When the light faded, Scott was still standing.

His uniform scorched.

Unmoved.

Alex stared.

Scott lowered his chin slightly.

“You can’t hurt me,” he said quietly.

He fired back — a controlled optic beam, not full force.

It struck Alex squarely—

And dissipated against his energy field.

Alex slid back a step, unharmed.

Logan snarled mid-combat.

“Family reunion’s a little busy, Summers!”

Scott moved forward slowly.

“I’m not fighting you.”

Alex fired again.

Scott absorbed the blast through sheer stance and training, redirecting its force into the ground.

The diamond mark pulsed brighter.

Mr. Sinister watched.

“Energy symmetry,” he murmured. “Elegant, isn’t it?”

Behind them, the last clone collapsed under Rogue and Mimic’s combined force.

Storm pinned another with a violent pressure drop.

Silence fell for half a breath.

Only Alex remained between them and Mr. Sinister.

Scott moved closer.

“Alex. Listen to me.”

Alex’s eyes flickered.

For a moment.

Then the red mark flared.

Alex’s body jerked.

He attacked again.

Scott reacted instantly — not attacking but restraining.

He surged forward, locking Alex’s arms, forcing his brother’s energy outward into empty air.

The blasts scorched steel towers behind them.

Scott gritted his teeth.

“I won’t hurt you.”

Mr. Sinister stepped forward at last.

“That is precisely the problem.”

The air shifted.

Before anyone could reposition—

Mr. Sinister moved.

Not fast in the conventional sense.

Precise.

Logan lunged.

Sinister caught his wrist mid-strike.

Twisted.

Metal shrieked as adamantium claws were forced sideways.

With a flick of telekinetic force, Logan was hurled into a stack of industrial piping.

Storm summoned lightning—

Mr. Sinister redirected the charge with a subtle gesture, bending its path into a metal tower that exploded in sparks.

Rogue charged—

Sinister stepped aside with unnatural fluidity, striking her with concussive force that sent her skidding across concrete.

Morph shifted from mid-attack—

Sinister’s hand flashed forward, destabilizing him at a molecular level. Morph collapsed, skin flickering erratically.

Mimic mirrored Logan’s strength—

Sinister struck pressure points with surgical precision, dropping him before the blow fully formed.

Scott held Alex in place, watching his team fall in seconds.

No wasted motion.

No rage.

No grand display.

Just execution.

Mr. Sinister extended his hand.

Alex’s body went rigid.

Scott felt the force — invisible, absolute — wrenching his brother from his grasp.

Scott tightened his hold—

Mr. Sinister’s eyes narrowed slightly.

The ground beneath Scott buckled.

A controlled telekinetic pulse blasted him backward.

He hit concrete hard.

Alex staggered free, breathing heavily, the red diamond glowing steadily.

Mr. Sinister approached Scott.

“You are disciplined,” he said softly. “Remarkably so. But discipline cannot overcome design.”

Logan pushed himself up again.

Storm struggled to stand.

Rogue forced breath back into her lungs.

Mr. Sinister raised his other hand.

The air thickened.

Pressure descended like an invisible weight.

They were forced to their knees.

All of them.

Even Logan.

Scott tried to rise—

Sinister increased the force by a fraction.

Concrete cracked beneath Scott’s palms.

“This was never a contest,” Sinister said calmly. “It was confirmation.”

Alex stepped beside him.

Controlled.

Weaponized.

The red diamond pulsed again.

Sinister looked toward the refinery behind him.

“And now,” he said quietly, “we proceed.”

Scott lifted his head despite the crushing pressure.

His team — down.

His brother — controlled.

And Sinister — untouched.

For the first time since arriving—

Scott had no move left.

## Chapter Five — Ascension to Phoenix

Nightcrawler watched from behind the collapsed pipeline.

The battlefield below was collapsing in on itself.

Everyone was pushed to the ground, unmovable, and Mr. Sinister was untouched.

Kurt’s hand moved toward his communicator.

“Professor, we need—”

Movement beside him.

Jean.

She was trying to stand.

Her hand gripped the rusted steel, trembling.

“Kurt…” she whispered.

“You must not,” he said urgently, steadying her. “You are not strong enough.”

Her legs buckled up.

He caught her before she fell.

Tears slipped silently from the corners of her eyes.

“They need me,” she whispered.

Below, Scott strained against invisible pressure.

Jean’s breathing became shallow.

“I can’t let him win,” she murmured.

“Not terrible.”

Murmurs of impressed, surprise rippled outward.

Morph flexed his fingers.

“No glitches,” he muttered softly to himself.

### **Laura**

Logan stepped into the yard with Laura beside him.

She stayed half a step behind.

Watching everything.

Calculating.

The crowd instinctively moved closer.

Logan raised his hand.

“Hey. Back off.”

The students immediately gave them space.

“You’ll scare her.”

Laura scanned faces.

So many expressions.

Curiosity.

Excitement.

Kindness.

It was unfamiliar.

Storm approached slowly.

“You must be Laura. I am Ororo.”

Laura looked at Logan.

He gave Laura a subtle nod.

Storm crouched slightly to meet her eye level.

“Would you like to fly?”

Laura blinked.

“Fly?”

Storm extended her hand.

The wind stirred gently.

Laura hesitated.

Then glanced back at Logan again.

He gave a small, approving nod.

“Go on.”

Laura stepped forward.

Storm lifted her carefully.

The air gathered beneath them.

They rose slowly.

Not fast.

Not dramatic.

Just a steady ascent.

Laura’s hands gripped Storm’s arm tightly at first.

The ground dropped away.

The campus widened beneath her.

Wind moved through her hair.

Her breathing changed.

Fear.

Then confusion.

Then—

A laugh.

Small at first.

Then louder.

Storm smiled.

Other airborne mutants followed behind them, Wanda, Archangel, some kids, rising into the sky — a loose formation across the morning horizon.

Below, students pointed out.

Some cheered.

Logan watched with arms crossed.

Scott stepped beside him.

“She’s going to be alright.”

Logan didn’t look away.

“Yeah.”

Above, Laura leaned forward slightly as Storm increased speed.

The campus stretched toward the ocean.

North HQ is alive beneath them.

The memorial stone is small now from this height.

The future is larger.

Despite Sinister’s true motives—

Laura wasn’t a weapon.

She was just a child in the sky.
