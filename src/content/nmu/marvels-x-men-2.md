## Chapter One — New Beginning (2021)

The attack began with precision.

Not rage.

Not spectacle.

Coordination.

A biotech complex on the edge of the city, **Helix Dynamics** has been under federal contract since the Sentinel War. Publicly, it manufactured adaptive prosthetics and energy shielding.

Privately, it stored something else.

Mutant DNA archives.

Not illegal.

But extensive.

And vulnerable.

### The Strike

The first tremor hit at 8:14 p.m.

Concrete rippled like disturbed water.

Glass fractured in perfect lines.

Avalanche stood across the street, fingers flexing rhythmically, sending controlled seismic pulses through the building's foundation.

Not collapse.

Destabilization.

Across the plaza, crowds began to scatter.

From the rooftop opposite, Pyro snapped his fingers, drawing flame from ruptured gas lines and bending it into a burning perimeter wall — not wild, but sculpted.

Containment.

On the ground level, Juggernaut walked straight through reinforced security gates. Steel twisted around him like a foil.

Security teams attempted to respond.

They found themselves trapped in illusions.

Mastermind stood calmly inside the lobby, projecting chaos where there were none — phantom explosions, invisible attackers, distorted reality.

Civilians panicked.

And darting between vehicles, disrupting responders, was Toad, leaping from surface to surface with manic efficiency.

On the balcony of an adjacent high-rise stood Emma Frost.

Hands folded.

Observing.

This was not destruction.

It was a statement.

Helix Dynamics had cataloged mutant genomes under the justification of "medical preparedness."

Emma disagreed with centralized control.

If mutants were to govern themselves, then autonomy would have to be demonstrated.

She pressed the communicator lightly.

"Phase two."

Inside the complex, reinforced vault doors buckled.

### The Response

A shadow passed over the plaza.

The Blackbird descended between smoke columns.

It landed cleanly.

No dramatic hover.

No hesitation.

The hatch opened.

Silver hair caught the morning light first.

Pietro Maximoff stepped down.

Behind him:

Iceman, Colossus, Psylocke, Magik, Kitty Pryde, Jubilee and Archangel.

Cameras pivoted.

Civilians stared.

Pietro inhaled sharply.

"You know the drill. Evac first. Contain. No collateral."

He pointed.

"Iceman — freeze fire lanes, Colossus — breach control, Psylocke — neutralize illusions, Magik — flank entry. Kitty Pryde — extraction assist. Jubilee — visual suppression. Archangel — air support."

Beat.

"I'll handle evacuation. I will be back as fast as I can."

Another beat.

"...Wait."

He smirked.

"I'm fast."

And he vanished.

### Strategic Chaos

Pietro reappeared inside the lobby, relocating civilians faster than Mastermind's illusions could adapt.

Psylocke stepped forward, psychic blade igniting violet. She cut through fabricated realities like silk. False explosions flickered and vanished. The panic in the room steadied.

"Ground them," she said calmly.

Outside, Iceman pulled moisture from the air in a spiraling draw, flash-freezing Pyro's flames into towering crystalline barriers. Steam roared between them.

"You still overextend," Bobby called.

Pyro smirked. "You still hesitate."

Bobby didn't bite. He redirected the fire upward, sealing it into ice rather than counterattacking.

Containment over domination.

Growth.

Colossus met Juggernaut head-on.

Metal collided with unstoppable momentum.

Concrete split beneath their feet.

Colossus did not try to overpower him.

He braced.

Anchored.

Absorbed the charge.

"Portal. Now!"

Magik's stepping disc opened beneath Juggernaut mid-stride.

He disappeared—

Reappearing inside a reinforced containment yard pre-coordinated with city response units. Magnetic clamps locked into place the second he landed.

Juggernaut roared.

But he wasn't in the plaza anymore.

Strategic relocation.

Avalanche altered his rhythm, increasing frequency.

Kitty phased through collapsing stairwells, guiding trapped employees through walls.

Jubilee detonated controlled plasma bursts — not explosions, but blinding arcs of light that forced Toad off balance long enough for Archangel to descend in a precise dive, pinning him against reinforced concrete.

Toad struggled.

He didn't escape.

Psylocke turned her attention toward Mastermind. His illusions twisted again — but this time they collapsed instantly under focused psychic pressure.

His knees buckled.

Neutralized.

Avalanche barely registered for Pietro's arrival.

A blur.

Pressure strikes to nerve clusters.

Pietro relocated him behind a portable containment barrier before the tremors could re-synchronize.

"Telegraphing your rhythm," Pietro muttered.

Outside, Iceman froze Pyro's flame constructs into solid towers, locking him in a cage of his own element.

Within minutes—

The fire was gone.

The illusions dissolved.

The tremors ceased.

Helix vault doors remained sealed.

Damage minimal.

Civilians safe.

Only one figure remained untouched.

Emma Frost.

She stood on the balcony, immaculate.

Unharmed.

Unbothered.

Pietro appeared behind her.

"You're done."

Emma didn't turn immediately.

"You've improved," she said lightly.

Psylocke stepped onto the balcony beside him, psychic energy steady but controlled.

Emma finally turned.

Her skin shimmered — diamond.

Brilliant.

Unbreakable.

For a moment, tension sharpened.

Then Emma's form softened.

Diamond faded.

She extended her hands calmly.

"I was curious how efficiently you would respond."

Magnetic restraints secured around her wrists.

No struggle.

No spectacle.

Just compliance.

### Aftermath

Police units arrived in organized waves.

Media drones hovered but kept distance.

Juggernaut roared in containment several blocks away.

Pyro sat encased in ice.

Avalanche restrained.

Mastermind subdued.

Toad secured.

Emma Frost escorted with precision.

The plaza did not burn.

It did not collapse.

It stabilized.

Pietro stood at the center of it all, breathing slightly heavier than he wanted to admit.

He surveyed his team.

"Good containment," he said. "Magik — tighten portal timing. Bobby — commit half a second sooner. Jubilee — flare angles near glass lines. Archangel — lower descent vector."

He looked at Colossus.

"You held the line."

Then to Psylocke.

"Clean sweep."

Illyana crossed her arms.

"You nearly let Avalanche destabilize the west wing."

Pietro rolled his eyes.

"Nearly."

A pause.

He looked around at emergency responders stabilizing infrastructure.

Civilians nodding.

Reporters speaking words like efficient, coordinated, heroic.

Mutants visible.

Accepted.

Trusted.

He allowed himself a small, satisfied smile.

"Not bad."

Emma paused before entering a transport unit.

She glanced back once — not at the police.

At Pietro.

Measured.

Assessing.

Then she stepped inside.

The doors shut.

The Blackbird cut through the morning sky.

Inside, the team finally exhaled.

Adrenaline settling into something steadier.

They had acted without Scott.

Without Jean.

Without Logan.

The city had not fallen apart.

It did not require saving by legends.

The new generation handled it.

Below them, the skyline remained intact.

And since the Sentinel War—

Mutants weren't reacting.

They were leading.

## Chapter Two — The Quiet Morning

The estate was quiet in the early light.

Mist drifted low across the fields as Scott Summers ran the perimeter path, boots striking gravel in steady rhythm.

Measured pace. Controlled breathing. Even stride.

Five miles. No music. No distractions.

Running wasn't about endurance anymore.

It was about clarity.

To his left, the mansion rose in pale stone — still the heart of the Institute, its tall windows catching the first break of sunlight.

To his right, across a manicured stretch of lawn and old oak trees, the academic building stood separate but connected — classrooms already illuminated, faculty arriving early.

The two structures faced one another across open ground.

Residence.

Education.

Balance.

Further beyond the tree line, almost softened by distance, the North Campus towered in steel and glass — larger, deliberate, modern. Training domes shimmered faintly in the morning haze.

Behind that — barely visible from this angle — the silhouette of North HQ cut into the horizon.

Growth built behind legacy.

As Scott rounded the eastern edge of the property, faint blue lines flickered briefly along the outer boundary fence — the security grid cycling its morning calibration.

Invisible to most.

Constantly watching.

Unauthorized mutant signatures triggered immediate alerts. Parents seeking admissions require clearance at the gatehouse. Every entry was logged and monitored.

The world trusts them now.

That did not mean the world was safe.

Scott increased his speed during the final stretch.

Later, on the rooftop, he finished with efficient sets — pushups, weighted carries, controlled squats. No wasted motion.

He stood still in the sunlight when he was done, breathing slow and deep. The visor remained in place, as always. The world stayed red — but steady.

Inside, the house smelled like coffee.

After a quick shower, Scott moved down the hall toward the kitchen.

Jean stood at the counter, one hand resting gently on her stomach as she stirred something in a pan.

She looked tired.

But peaceful.

Scott stepped behind her, wrapping his arms carefully around her waist.

"Good morning," he murmured, pressing a kiss to the side of her head.

"Morning," Jean replied, smiling faintly. "You run the earth into submission yet?"

"Almost."

She leaned back slightly into him.

"He's been restless," she said softly.

Scott's hand moved instinctively to her stomach.

"He's just eager," he said. "Takes after his mother."

Jean gave him a look.

"I was calm."

"You threw a car at someone once."

"That was different."

He smiled.

They stayed like that for a moment longer — quiet, grounded.

After breakfast, they moved through the main hall together.

Logan leaned against a column with coffee in hand. Storm stood near the windows, reviewing something on a tablet. Rogue leaned across a side table scanning reports. Morph paced loosely, mid-thought as usual.

"Morning," Storm greeted.

"Morning," Scott replied.

"Still intact?" Logan asked, nodding toward Jean.

"For now," Jean said.

"Mimics still on the way," Rogue added. "Traffic."

Scott nodded.

Down the corridor, a bell chimed softly.

Classroom doors opened.

Younger students filed past in small groups, laughing, arguing, comparing assignments. A faculty member redirected a telekinetically floating backpack before it collided with a doorway.

Order.

Routine.

Across the grounds, visible through tall windows, the North Campus was already active — structured drills beginning on the training fields.

Storm glanced northward.

"They started assessment rotations early."

Scott followed her gaze.

In the distance, silhouettes moved in coordinated patterns across the training grounds.

Measured.

Disciplined.

Jean smiled faintly.

"They're eager."

"They're impatient," Logan corrected.

The estate no longer felt like a refuge.

It felt like an institution.

And institutions required more than power.

They required judgment.

### Administrative Reality (Revised) 

A soft chime sounded through the original school's reception corridor. 

Not loud.

Not urgent.

Controlled.

On the main console, the perimeter system updated automatically:

**Visitor clearance confirmed — Admissions Appointment.**

Hank's voice filtered through the internal channel.

"Charles, the admissions family from Boston has cleared the gate. Escorting them to the school building now."

Upstairs, inside his office overlooking the courtyard between the mansion and the school, Charles adjusted slightly in his chair.

"Thank you, Hank."

Below, security personnel guided a small family through the central walk.

No weapons scans visible.

No intimidation.

Just procedure.

The Institute no longer hid behind secrecy.

It operated like any respected institution.

Only smarter.

Charles rolled toward the balcony railing connecting the administrative wing to the academic building.

He paused there for a moment.

Watching.

There was something different in the air now.

Not secrecy.

Not survival.

Expectation.

### Admissions Room — Original School 

The admissions office sat on the right wing of the property — part of the original academic building.

Sunlight filtered through tall windows lined with student artwork and controlled telekinetic practice diagrams.

A teenage boy sat stiffly across from Charles.

His parents beside him — cautious, protective.

Nervous.

The boy's fingers vibrated faintly against the armrest — kinetic energy building in subtle pulses he struggled to contain.

The room's dampening field absorbed the excess safely. 

"You understand," Charles said gently, "that the North Campus is not a training ground for combat."

The father nodded carefully.

"We understand." 

Scott entered quietly from the side hallway, taking a seat beside Charles.

He didn't loom.

He grounded the room.

"It is a school first," Scott added evenly. "Powers are part of you. Not a performance."

The boy looked between them.

"I don't want to hurt anyone."

Storm stood near the doorway, reviewing the intake file.

"You won't," she said calmly. "Not here."

Charles brushed the surface of the boy's mind — not intrusion, only assessment. 

Fear. 

Guilt. 

Restraint.

Good signs.

Outside the window, younger students crossed the courtyard between classes. 

Structured. 

Guided.

Safe. 

"We will begin you here," Charles said, gesturing lightly to the academic wing. "Foundation before specialization."

The mother exhaled in visible relief.

"North Campus comes later," Scott added. "When control becomes confidence."

Paperwork slid across the table.

Biometric markers confirmed.

Admissions approved.

### Teacher Interviews — Academic Wing

Further down the corridor, Jean sat in a modest interview room overlooking the east gardens.

Across from her sat a prospective literature instructor — non-mutant.

"We integrate human history and mutant history," Jean explained gently. "Not as separate narratives. As one timeline."

The woman nodded thoughtfully.

"You're not preparing them to hide."

Jean's hand rested briefly against her stomach before she answered.

"No. We're preparing them to lead."

In the adjacent classroom, Storm conducted interviews for environmental sciences.

She reviewed credentials carefully.

No fear-based rhetoric.

No sensationalism.

No savior complex.

Educators first.

Outside, a class of younger students practiced controlled elemental manipulation beneath supervision — a light breeze redirected safely through calibrated field generators.

This building was not a battlefield.

It was foundation.

Beyond the tree line, the North Campus stood waiting.

But here —

They learned who they were first.

### North HQ — Hero Registration Review 

The upper briefing chamber of North HQ was quiet when Charles, Scott, and Logan entered.

Pietro was already there, replaying footage from the Helix operation.

Bishop stood near the far wall, reviewing a separate tactical overlay.

The holographic projection rotated slowly above the circular table.

Civilian extractions.

Containment maneuvers.

Environmental damage control.

No applause.

No criticism.

Just examination.

Scott watched as Iceman redirected flame upward instead of counterattacking.

"He chose containment over retaliation."

Pietro nodded.

"He's thinking about aftermath now."

Bishop added calmly, "He held the structural line. That prevented escalation."

Logan's eyes narrowed at a moment where one of the graduates hesitated during evacuation — civilians shouting, alarms blaring.

"He paused."

Pietro didn't deny it.

"He didn't want to move someone into a worse position."

Scott leaned forward slightly.

"That's not weakness."

The footage slowed.

The graduate corrected course within two seconds and cleared the hallway safely.

Charles folded his hands.

"Empathy without discipline slows response," he said gently. "Discipline without empathy endangers lives. They are learning the balance."

Silence followed.

Not heavy.

Thoughtful.

### Candidate Discussion — Public Authorization 

A graduate file expanded.

Flight capability.

Kinetic shielding.

Urban response simulations complete.

Pietro spoke first this time.

"He's improved his stress tolerance."

Bishop overlaid biometric data.

"Still spikes under auditory overload."

Logan didn't look away from the screen.

"That's where crowds break you."

Scott studied the pattern carefully.

"He doesn't break," Scott said evenly. "He absorbs too much."

Charles nodded.

"He feels the room before acting."

Another pause.

Scott made the call — not final, not absolute.

"Supervised deployments. Secondary response tier. Let him build confidence in controlled spaces."

Pietro agreed without hesitation.

"He'll grow into it."

Bishop inclined his head once.

"X-Force remains within escalation range."

No rejection.

No labeling.

Just direction.

Decision logged.

### North Campus Advancement 

Another file replaced the previous.

Fourteen years old. Advanced telekinetic aptitude. Stable baseline. Controlled emotional response.

Scott watched the simulation replay.

"She's plateaued here."

Pietro folded his arms.

"She needs larger space."

Logan added quietly, "She's cautious."

"That's why she's ready," Scott replied.

Charles smiled faintly.

"North Campus."

Placement updated.

No ceremony.

Just trust.

### Balcony — North Campus 

The council stepped onto the North HQ balcony.

Below them, the North Campus moved with steady purpose.

Students sparred inside calibrated containment grids.

Aerial trainees navigated circular flight rings.

In one formation, Wanda corrected a student's unstable projection — red energy stabilizing the field, not overpowering it.

She spoke calmly. The student recalibrated. The construct held.

Bishop watched the discipline on the field.

"They're better than last year."

Pietro answered softly,

"They're steadier."

Scott rested his hands on the railing.

"This is what we were fighting for."

Charles moved beside him.

"Yes."

A brief pause.

"And this," Charles added quietly, "is what we are responsible for."

Below them, another timed extraction drill concluded.

No panic.

No chaos.

Measured.

Learning.

The Institute wasn't deciding who was worthy.

It was shaping who they would become.

### Mansion 

By the time Scott returned to the mansion, the campus lights had softened.

Even North Campus, visible beyond the trees, had dimmed to a steady glow.

The day's decisions lingered in his mind — names, placements, futures.

Inside, the house was quieter.

Dinner sat on the table, covered neatly.

Beside it, a small, folded note.

Scott opened it.

_Too tired to wait up. I ate. Don't skip yours. — J_

A faint smile curved beneath the visor.

Even exhausted, she made sure he wouldn't forget.

The meal wasn't elaborate.

Simple.

Warm.

Enough.

After a shower, Scott moved through the hallway with practiced quiet.

The bedroom door opened without sound.

Jean was already asleep, turned slightly toward his side of the bed.

One hand rested lightly against her stomach.

He paused for a moment.

Just looking.

He pulled out his specially designed sleep patch from the drawer beside the bed (reinforced lenses calibrated to prevent accidental discharge), and wore it after carefully removing his visor before sleep.

No risk.

No fear.

Only precaution.

Scott slipped beneath the sheets slowly, adjusting them over Jean's shoulders.

She stirred slightly.

Not waking.

Just enough to find him.

Her hand reached instinctively.

Their fingers tangled.

Then held.

Breathing slowed.

The weight of the day dissolved into shared stillness. 

Outside, the estate remained silent.

Protected.

Earned.

### Elsewhere

Far from the estate, beyond the protection grids and plasma shields, an old coastal refinery stood against the dark ocean.

Rust.

Steel.

Isolation.

Inside, cold light flickered over stainless surfaces.

Nathaniel Essex moved calmly between containment units.

One bed held a young man — restrained but alive.

Alex Summers.

Unconscious.

Monitored.

Across the chamber, a sealed hibernation pod hummed softly.

**Hibernation Unit: X-23.**

Fluid suspension stable.

Genetic adaptation complete.

Essex adjusted a control panel with clinical precision.

On another screen, genetic templates rotated — variations, enhancements, refinements.

Clones in development.

Evolution curated.

He paused.

Observed his work.

Satisfied.

"Almost," he murmured.

The refinery lights dimmed as backup generators took over.

Outside, waves struck metal foundations without rhythm.

Inside, plans had already begun.

## Chapter Three

Mansion

The next morning, the team gathered again in the hallway, moving toward their respective duties.

Jean had insisted on continuing light oversight work despite nearing term. The Institute's medical wing was more than capable — but she had chosen a public hospital for the delivery.

Normal.

Human.

A choice not defined by power.

And then suddenly—

Jean froze.

Her expression shifted — not panic, not fear.

Recognition.

Her hand tightened around Scott's.

"Scott..."

There was a faint sound.

A rush.

Then warmth spreading down her legs.

Scott blinked.

"Jean?"

She looked at him.

"I think it's time."

Everything moved at once.

### Hospital 

The delivery was long — but not catastrophic.

Storm remained calm. Rogue kept Scott from pacing holes in the floor. Logan stood apart; arms crossed, silent but watchful.

When the cry finally came from inside the room, it cut through every tension in the hall.

Scott didn't realize he'd been holding his breath until he exhaled.

A nurse stepped out.

"Mother and child are stable."

Scott went in.

Jean lay back against the pillows, exhausted but radiant in a way no battle had ever made her.

The nurse placed the child into Scott's arms.

"Nate," he said quietly. "Nathan Summers."

Jean smiled faintly.

Scott lowered his forehead briefly to the baby's head.

Outside the room, the others waited.

And in the hallway beyond them, another nurse approached.

Logan's head tilted slightly.

The scent was clean.

Too clean.

Not hostile.

Not wrong.

Just absent of everything human.

The nurse entered the room calmly.

"Mr. Summers," he said politely. "We need to take the baby for further evaluation."

Scott nodded, distracted, and began to turn—

"Scott. No."

Logan's voice was sharp.

Scott stopped.

Jean's head snapped up.

The nurse's posture stiffened.

Then froze.

Jean's eyes narrowed.

Telepathic grip.

The nurse stood rigid, unable to move.

Scott looked between them.

"What's happening?"

And then—

A faint red mark began to bloom on the nurse's forehead.

Diamond-shaped.

Precise.

The nurse's lips curled into a smile that did not belong to him.

"Remember me, Scott Summers?"

Scott's face went cold.

"...Essex."

Logan moved instantly, grabbing the man by the collar and lifting him off the ground.

"What do you want, bub?"

Jean's voice cut through.

"Logan, stop. He's just a conduit. Someone's projecting from far away."

A beat.

"He couldn't get near us."

Logan hesitated, then lowered the man roughly to his feet but kept a grip on him.

Scott stepped forward.

"Why are you here?"

The possessed nurse tilted his head slightly.

"Your family grows. Congratulations."

Scott's jaw tightened.

"What did you do?"

A pause.

"Do you remember your little brother, Alex?"

Scott's body went rigid.

"What did you do to him?" he demanded.

"Nothing. Yet."

The smile widened.

"But I would very much like to keep it that way."

Scott handed the baby instinctively to Logan without breaking eye contact.

Logan adjusted Nate carefully, steady and certain.

"If you want him safe," the nurse continued, voice clinical and detached, "you will bring me the child."

Scott's voice dropped.

"That's not happening."

"Then I suppose I will begin with your brother instead. Which would be unfortunate. One of them is far more promising."

Scott's fists clenched.

Jean felt his anger spike like heat through glass.

"If you change your mind," Essex said calmly, "come to the old coastal refinery outside Providence. I will be waiting."

The red diamond flared briefly.

Then vanished.

The nurse collapsed, unconscious.

Scott caught him before he hit the ground.

Storm and Rogue rushed in. Morph stood at the doorway, stunned.

Jean closed her eyes, steadying herself.

Scott stared at the unconscious man in his arms.

He didn't feel fear.

He felt something worse.

Calculated rage.

And beneath it—

Unease.

Because Essex had not sounded desperate.

He sounded prepared.

## Chapter Four

The lower lab was quiet except for the hum of scanners.

Jean sat in a medical support chair beside the diagnostic platform, Nate asleep against her chest. A mobility brace rested near her feet — precautionary, temporary. The strain of delivery still lingered in her posture.

Scott stood close, one hand resting lightly against her shoulder.

Logan leaned against the far counter, arms folded. Storm, Rogue, Morph, and Mimic formed a loose semicircle around them.

Hank studied the readouts carefully.

"Cellular structure is normal," he said at last. "No foreign markers. No dormant triggers. Nate's genetic sequencing is precisely what we would expect."

Scott didn't realize he had been holding tension in his jaw until it eased.

"And Jean?" Storm asked.

Hank glanced toward her.

"Fatigue is significant. Muscular strain, elevated neural activity from the confrontation. You shouldn't be upright this long."

Jean gave him a faint look.

"I'm not fragile, Hank."

"No," he agreed gently. "But you are recovering."

She didn't argue further.

"He didn't take anything," Jean said quietly.

"No," Hank confirmed. "If he wanted samples, he had opportunity. The hospital incident was not acquisition."

"A message," Logan muttered.

Hank nodded.

"Communication."

Upstairs, the Institute continued its rhythm.

Classes in session.

Admissions underway.

Students crossing the courtyard.

The institution functioning.

But beneath it, something old had resurfaced.

Charles entered the lab slowly. 

"The North HQ is on alert," he said evenly. "New X-Men maintain perimeter readiness. X-Force stands by should this escalate beyond containment." 

His gaze shifted — not only to Scott.

But to Jean.

"For now, this matter requires careful response."

Scott nodded once.

"We don't bring the school into this," he said. "Not unless we have to."

Logan shifted his weight, "That refinery's bait."

Hank projected the image onto the central display.

The old coastal refinery outside Providence filled the room.

"Decommissioned fifteen years ago," Hank said. "Recently reactivated power beneath the primary structure. Sublevels. Shielded infrastructure. Environmental control systems."

Storm studied it.

"He chose isolation."

Scott's voice was quiet.

"He chose control."

Charles turned — not to Scott.

To Morph.

"Morph," he said gently. "It is time."

Morph stiffened slightly.

Scott looked between them. "Time for what?"

Morph exhaled slowly.

"I know him," he said.

Silence followed.

"I don't mean I've read reports. I mean I know him."

Logan's posture changed almost imperceptibly.

"What do you mean?" Scott asked. 

Morph stepped forward.

"He took me. Years ago. Before I ever came here." 

Rogue inhaled sharply.

"He didn't like what I was," Morph continued. "A shapeshifter with a stable form. Predictable baseline." He swallowed. "He destabilized it."

Storm's eyes narrowed. "How?"

"Accelerated cellular plasticity, stripped anchor points in my genetic structure, pushed my mutation beyond its natural tolerance." Morph's voice tightened. "I don't have a default anymore because he made sure I wouldn't."

Scott's hands curled into fists.

"He experiments on himself too," Morph said quietly. "That's the part you need to understand."

Jean looked up sharply. "What do you mean?"

"He's not just a man with funding and labs," Morph replied. "He modifies himself. His physiology doesn't behave normally. You hurt him..." He paused. "He adapts."

Logan tilted his head. "Healing factor?"

"More than that. Structural corrections, like his body recalibrates damage."

Hank's eyes sharpened. "Self-engineered cellular restructuring."

"He can teleport short distances," Morph continued. "Not theatrically. Efficiently. He's shielded telepathically — layered, engineered. Not stronger than you, Jean. But protected."

Jean nodded slowly. "The projection felt compartmentalized. Artificial barriers."

"He's not human anymore," Morph said quietly. "Maybe he never intended to stay that way."

Scott's voice was low.

"The orphanage."

All eyes shifted to him.

"It was funded by Essex Corp," Scott said. "After my parents died. Routine medical exams. Bloodwork. Psychological profiling." His jaw tightened. "Alex was adopted young. He doesn't even remember I exist. He never went through any of that."

Rogue frowned. "You think he flagged you?"

"I know he did," Scott replied.

Hank brought up archived corporate records.

"Essex Corp has quietly acquired genetic databases for years. Private clinics. Orphanage networks. Research institutions. All legal. All insulated."

Logan added, "Mutants have been disappearing. Quietly. No headlines. Clinics shutting down overnight."

Storm's voice cut cleanly through the tension. "And now he invites you to his territory."

"It's a trap," Rogue said.

Scott met her gaze.

"Yes."

Rogue's expression steadied.

"He doesn't fight impulsively. He tests. Measures. Predicts. That refinery won't just be a lab. It'll be layered. Industrial shell, clean interior, sublevels beneath."

Hank nodded. "Multiple access points, reinforced steel infrastructure, environmental control systems."

Scott looked at the image again.

"He chose metal and isolation."

Logan's eyes flicked briefly toward him.

Storm spoke softly. "He expects a response."

"He expects data," Morph corrected.

Scott turned back to the group.

"We don't trade Nate."

Immediate agreement.

Jean's voice was calm but firm. "We bring Alex home."

Scott stepped closer to Jean.

"You're not coming."

It wasn't harsh.

It was final.

Jean met his gaze evenly.

"I'm not helpless."

"I know," Scott replied. "That's not the point."

The room grew still.

Storm watched them carefully.

Scott continued — now as leader.

"You just went through delivery. Your neural output spiked during projection. If he's shielded and layered like Morph says, he'll push for telepathic strain. I won't let him use that."

Jean's jaw tightened slightly.

"He expects me."

"Yes," Scott said. "And that's exactly why you stay."

Silence.

Not marital tension.

Strategic calculation.

Logan finally spoke.
