## Prelude: Queens 2016

_"My name is Peter Parker. I'm just a regular high school kid from Queens... who also happens to be your friendly neighborhood Spider-Man._

_It's been a year since I was bitten by the genetically enhanced spider that changed everything. It gave me the abilities of a spider:_ _wall-crawling, super strength, enhanced senses, agility, and somehow, the responsibility that came with it._

_At first, I didn't know what to do with any of it. I used to sneak into underground fights, testing how far my powers could go. One night, after a match, a thief ran past me. I could've stopped him, but I didn't. I thought it wasn't my problem._

_Later that night, Uncle Ben was murdered by that same thief._

_That was the moment my life changed forever._

_Since then, I've tried to honor his words, "With great power comes great responsibility." I became Spider-Man, fighting for the people who can't fight for themselves, stopping crime, saving lives, and trying every day to be the person my uncle wanted me to be._

_In just one year, a lot has happened. I built my own web-shooters, designed a few prototype suits before perfecting one, and took down villains like Batroc, Trap-Master, and even Aleksei Sytsevich in his Rhino armor. But the real problem was the crime lords hiding behind it all, Hammerhead. He and his allies control everything from the shadows... and Spider-Man keeps getting in their way unknowingly._

_I live with my Aunt May, the strongest and smartest woman I know. She works multiple jobs just to afford my education, never once complaining, never once giving up. And somehow, she still finds time to worry about me being late for dinner._

_My friends are my world:_

_Harry Osborn — the confident one who believes in me even when I don't._

_Mary Jane Watson — my childhood friend with dreams bigger than this city._

_Gwen Stacy — my lab partner, sharp as lightning and always two steps ahead._

_And Flash Thompson — the school quarterback who's made it his mission to remind me I'm not cool._

_So yeah... this is my story._

_A high school student, a part-time scientist, and a superhero._

_And this is the story of how I became the first real hero of this age —_

_the story of Spider-Man."_

## Chapter 1: A Year in Red and Blue

### **Weekend in the Webs**

Saturday afternoon in Queens had a different rhythm.

The sun hung high over brick rooftops and fire escapes, washing the city in heat and noise. Car horns, music spilling from open windows, kids shouting in the distance, all of it blurred together as a red-and-blue streak arced above the streets.

Spider-Man moved easily in daylight.

He timed his swings between buses and delivery trucks, adjusting web length on instinct, feet brushing walls as he redirected momentum. People pointed out. Some waved. Others pulled out their phones.

He didn't slow.

The police scanner crackled in his ear.

"—possible purse snatching, Queensboro Plaza—"

"Copy," Spider-Man muttered, already banking left.

A quick stop. A webbed wrist. A purse returned to a shaken woman who barely had time to say thank you before Spider-Man was gone again.

He didn't wait for it.

By evening, the sun dipped low, and the city softened, long shadows stretching across rooftops as Spider-Man perched atop a billboard, adjusting a web-shooter clogged with grit.

Saturday nights were louder. Messier.

A hit-and-run near the avenue.

A fight spilling out of a convenience store.

A fire escape bending under too much weight.

He worked fast, efficiently, saving strength where he could, listening, choosing which sirens to follow and which to leave to the police.

Between calls, he checked the time.

No homework due until Monday. No alarms are waiting in the morning.

Just one more swing.

Sunday morning came with sore muscles and a bruised rib that protested every deep breath.

Peter finished his breakfast quickly, forcing himself to eat more than he wanted to. Aunt May stood near the sink, already halfway through her morning routine.

"I'm heading out," Peter said, grabbing his bag. "Studying at Gwen's place. Might be a long one."

Aunt May glanced over her shoulder, smiling. "Don't stay out too late. And come home for dinner if you can."

"I will," Peter said easily. "Okay."

She nodded, satisfied, and Peter slipped out the door.

Spider-Man moved slower now, perched on a church steeple as the city below woke up, bakery doors opening, joggers passing, church bells ringing.

He stretched carefully, testing what still hurt.

"Okay," he muttered. "We're not broken. That's a win."

The scanner chimed again.

A lost kid near the park.

A break-in gone wrong.

A dog trapped on a roof.

Not all heroics made headlines.

By afternoon, the sun was harsh again, sweat soaking into the suit beneath the mask. Spider-Man stopped long enough to re-web a torn seam and drink water from a rooftop hose, head tilted back, breathing hard.

He thought about going home.

He didn't.

By Sunday night, the city finally slowed.

Neon lights flickered on. The river reflected the moon in broken lines. Spider-Man landed on a rusted water tower overlooking the East River, chest heaving, hands braced on his knees.

Below, two figures smashed a jewelry store window and scrambled out, bags clinking.

Spider-Man sighed.

"Guys," he said quietly, tightening his gloves, "you picked the worst possible night."

He leapt.

Web. Flip. Impact. Two quick strikes and a lamppost later, the thieves hung upside down, wrapped tight.

Sirens approached.

Spider-Man was already swinging away.

Hours later, back in his small Queens bedroom, Spider-Man peeled off the mask and let it drop onto his desk. He caught his reflection in the mirror, exhaustion etched into his face, bruises darkening, eyes heavy but alive.

Two days.

No breaks. No applause.

Just... work.

On his desk sat an old photograph, Uncle Ben, smiling wide, hand resting on Peter's shoulder.

Peter picked it up, thumb brushing the corner.

"Still trying," he whispered. "Every weekend."

Outside, a siren wailed once, then faded.

Peter lay back on his bed, muscles screaming, eyes closing.

Sunday night gave him that much.

Monday would ask for more.

## Chapter 2: The Web of Friends

Morning light spilled through the narrow windows of Midtown High, catching dust motes in soft, golden streaks. The bell rang. Lockers slammed. Sneakers squeaked across the linoleum.

It was chaos with a heartbeat, the kind Peter Parker had learned to love.

He moved through the crowded hallway clutching a half-eaten sandwich and a stack of homework. Beneath his shirt, a bruise near his collarbone pulsed faintly, a quiet reminder of last night's patrol.

"Morning, tiger."

Peter looked up and met Mary Jane Watson's grin, auburn hair tied back, camera slung over her shoulder, eyes bright with the same mischief she'd carried since they were kids climbing fences and chasing fireflies.

"You look like you lost a fight with gravity," she said.

Peter smiled. "More like chemistry homework. Gravity was kinder."

She laughed, nudging his arm. "One day you'll learn to sleep like a normal person, Parker."

"Sleep's overrated." Peter said in response, voice low.

"Tell that to your eye bags," a voice cut in.

They turned the corner to find Harry Osborn leaning against a locker, tie loosened, phone in hand, looking far too awake for the hour.

"There's my favorite nerd duo," Harry said, flashing Peter a grin. "MJ, you're looking suspiciously alive for first period."

"That's because I actually sleep," she shot back.

Harry clapped Peter on the shoulder. "Party at my place this weekend. You're coming. No excuses. No homework. No mysterious disappearances. No volunteer work. I'm not taking no for an answer."

Peter hesitated. "I'll... try."

"Try means yes," Harry said. That effortless Osborn confidence is already sealing the deal.

A football thudded loudly against a locker behind them.

Flash Thompson strolled down the hall, quarterback confidence radiating from every step. He smirked when he saw Peter.

"Hey Parker. Forget your lunch money again, or are you saving it for a microscope fund?"

MJ rolled her eyes. "Grow up, Flash."

Flash shrugged. "Just saying hi to my favorite nerd."

Before Peter could respond, a calm voice cut through the tension.

"Ignore him."

Gwen Stacy stood by her locker, arms full of science journals. Her blonde hair was tied neatly back, her expression composed but alert.

Flash scoffed once, then moved on.

Gwen handed Peter a small vial wrapped carefully in tissue. "You left this in the lab last day. Your formula ratios are off, again."

Peter blinked. "Oh. Thanks, Gwen. I didn't mean to—"

"You never do," she said with a faint smile. "But you're welcome."

Harry watched the exchange, amused. "So this is the genius lab partner I keep hearing about."

Gwen tilted her head slightly. "And you must be the distraction."

MJ laughed. Peter rubbed the back of his neck, smiling despite himself.

For a moment, everything felt normal, a fragile balance of classes, friendships, crushes, and the thousand secrets Peter carried with him every day.

### **Cafeteria**

At lunch, the four of them gathered at their usual table near the window overlooking the football field.

MJ scribbled notes for the school paper. Gwen sketched a circuit diagram in the margins of her notebook. Harry scrolled through messages. Flash sat a few tables away, finishing a post-practice snack.

Peter was finishing his notes, until MJ stopped typing and turned on the news.

**DAILY BUGLE: "SPIDER-MENACE STRIKES AGAIN"**

She scoffed. "They make him sound like a criminal. He's saving people."

Flash leaned over from his table. "Hey, at least he's doing something. Guy has guts. Unlike those mutants who only fight for themselves."

Peter froze, sandwich halfway to his mouth.

Harry smirked. "What about you, Parker? You think he's a hero or a menace?"

Peter swallowed and forced a shrug. "I think... sometimes people just do what they can. Even if no one gets it."

MJ looked up at him, curious at the tone but said nothing.

### Lab (Extra classes for Science Olympiad)

After the final bell, the lab was quiet, bathed in late-afternoon sunlight. Beakers glowed amber beneath the fading day.

Gwen tapped her pen against her notebook. "You ever feel like people only see a fraction of who you are?"

Peter glanced up from his microscope. "All the time."

She smiled faintly. "Then maybe that's a good thing. Keeps life interesting."

Peter smiled back, though the thought he didn't voice sat heavy behind it.

_If only you knew._

### **Outside Campus**

As they stepped out onto the ground, Gwen nudged him lightly.

"So," she said, "when are you asking MJ to Homecoming?"

Peter groaned. "I haven't thought about it."

"Oh, come on," Gwen said. "I know you like her. What could possibly go wrong?"

"I'm afraid I'd jeopardize our friendship."

"And that," Harry said from behind them, "is why you need to have some faith in yourself."

Peter jumped slightly. "Harry, what are you still doing here?"

"My dad's busy," Harry shrugged. "Figured I'd stay and do homework."

Gwen raised an eyebrow. "You? Homework? Are you okay today?"

Harry smirked. "Very funny."

Suddenly, Peter's Spider-Sense flared.

He stiffened, then forced a smile. "Hey, I... I gotta go, I just remembered I have some work to do at Queens Community Foundation. See you guys."

Before they could respond, he was already moving away.

Harry and Gwen exchanged glances.

"What's going on with him lately?" Gwen murmured, watching Peter disappear down the path.

Harry shrugged, slipping his hands into his pockets. "Who knows. He doesn't really share much."

They walked in silence for a few steps before Gwen glanced at him again.

"How long have you two known each other?" She asked.

Harry smiled faintly. "Since middle school. First week actually."

Gwen raised an eyebrow. "Really?"

"Yeah," Harry said. "I was stuck on an algebra problem, couldn't figure it out. Out of nowhere, he just... leaned over and fixed it. Didn't even say much. Just pointed, explained it once, and just disappeared."

Gwen smiled. "That sounds like him."

"A few days later," Harry continued, "it was pouring. Like heavy rain. I was heading home in my dad's car and saw him on the side of the road, fixing the chain on his bike. Completely soaked. Clothes, bag... I was pretty sure his notebooks were ruined."

He shook his head slightly in the memory.

"I made my dad stop. Peter tried to say no, but we dragged him into the car anyway. Took him to our place. My dad liked him instantly. Said he was smart. That night we got him new notebooks, clothes... whatever he needed."

Gwen listened quietly.

"After that," Harry said, "we were inseparable. He introduced me to MJ too. It didn't take long to figure out he was already into her. He wasn't exactly subtle."

Gwen laughed softly. "Figures."

She hesitated. "Is he always like this? Running off at the last second? Disappearing?"

Harry shook his head. "Not really. It started happening more after his uncle died."

Gwen slowed. "I remember, Benjamin Parker?"

"Yeah," Harry said. "His parents left when he was young. Not sure why. He grew up with his aunt and uncle. They were basically his parents."

He swallowed. "The night his uncle was murdered... I've never seen anyone that broken. MJ and I stayed with him the whole day after the funeral. He barely spoke."

Gwen nodded slowly. "I remember, Peter didn't came to the extra lectures for weeks."

They reached the edge of the campus.

"I can tell you one thing," Harry added quietly. "If he ever needs me, I'm there. No questions. And I know he'd do the same for any of us."

Gwen smiled, wiping her eyes before the tears could fall.

"He's lucky," she said. "To have friends like you."

Harry smiled back. "Thanks."

They stood for a moment, the evening breeze moving through the trees lining the campus road.

Harry glanced at Gwen. "Hey... you want a lift?"

She blinked. "I thought your dad was picking you up."

Harry smirked. "I said he is busy. I never said anything about picking me up."

Before she could respond, he reached into his pocket and casually pressed a button on a sleek key fob.

A soft electronic chime echoed down the street.

From farther up the road, a low hum grew louder, smoother, controlled. A dark, polished car rolled into view; headlights flashed once as it slowed near the curb.

Gwen stopped walking.

"...Okay," she said, genuinely impressed. "You're full of surprises."

Harry shrugged, trying not to look too pleased. "Comes in handy sometimes."

She shook her head, smiling as they headed toward the car. "You really don't act like someone who owns _that_."

Harry glanced back at the school, then at the empty path Peter had taken earlier.

"Yeah," he said quietly. "Guess not."

The car door opened smoothly. Gwen slid in, still glancing around, curiosity flickering behind her calm expression.

Harry followed, the door closing behind them as the car pulled away from the curb.

The school disappeared in the rearview mirror.

That night, the city called again.

A police chase downtown, Spider-Man webbed the lead car's axle, sending it spinning harmlessly into a stack of trash bags as officers closed in.

A mugging near the bridge, two quick webs, a sharp kick, and the thief was left dangling beneath the streetlight, cursing as Spider-Man vanished into the dark.

A child trapped on a fire escape, smoke curling from the window, small hands shaking as Spider-Man lifted them into his arms and carried them to safety just as flames licked the railing.

Peter reached home before dinner, exhaustion settling into his muscles as he quietly closed the front door behind him.

"Peter?" May's voice came from the kitchen.

"I'm home."

She stepped into the hallway, drying her hands with a towel before smiling warmly.

"There you are." Her eyes lingered on him for a moment. "Long day at the Queens Foundation?"

Peter rubbed the back of his neck.

"Yeah... pretty much. Lots of running around. Helped a few people. Carried some heavy stuff.."

May nodded knowingly.

"I can see that... You look exhausted."

"I'm okay."

She smiled.

"Go get changed. Dinner's almost ready."

Peter managed a small smile.

"Sure."

About ten minutes later, they sat across from each other at the small dining table. The house was quiet except for the clinking of silverware and the evening news playing softly from the television in the living room.

May served another spoonful onto Peter's plate.

"You've been eating like you're trying to feed two people lately."

Peter laughed awkwardly.

"I guess I'm just growing."

"So they keep telling me."

For a few moments they ate in comfortable silence.

"How's school?" May asked.

"It's good."

"Making friends?"

Peter smiled.

"Yeah. Harry's in a couple of my classes. MJ and Gwen too."

"And Flash?"

Peter chuckled.

"Believe it or not... he's actually trying this year."

May raised an eyebrow.

"Really?"

"I know. I was shocked too."

She laughed softly.

"Well, people can surprise you."

Peter nodded.

"I guess they can."

May looked at him for a moment before speaking again.

"I'm proud of you, Peter."

He looked up.

"You've been balancing school, volunteering at the Queens Foundation..." She smiled. "Growing up isn't easy, but you're handling it better than you think."

Peter lowered his eyes, guilt briefly flashing across his face.

"I'm trying."

"I know you are."

She reached across the table and gently squeezed his hand.

"That's all anyone can ask."

Peter smiled, grateful.

"Thanks, May."

Peter hesitated before standing.

"What about you?" he asked. "How was work?"

May smiled, pleasantly surprised that he'd asked.

"Oh, you know. The usual."

Peter raised an eyebrow.

"That really doesn't answers the question."

She laughed softly.

"We were short-staffed today. Mrs. Alvarez called in sick, so I ended up covering two departments." She shook her head with an amused sigh. "My feet are definitely going to complain tomorrow."

Peter frowned slightly.

"May... you don't have to push yourself so hard."

She reached over and gently rested a hand on his.

"Peter."

He looked up.

"I'm alright."

"You've been working extra shifts."

"Only because I want to."

He wasn't convinced.

"You've already done so much for me."

May smiled warmly.

"And I'll keep doing it."

She gave his hand a reassuring squeeze.

"I'm doing this for us, Peter. So you can focus on school, your volunteering, and being sixteen for a little while longer."

Peter smiled faintly.

"I just don't want you overworking yourself."

"I appreciate that." She chuckled. "But right now, you don't have to worry about me."

She stood and began collecting the empty plates.

"I've got this."

Peter watched her for a moment before getting up and taking his plate anyway.

"Maybe," he said with a grin, "but I'm still helping with the dishes."

May laughed.

"Now that is something I won't argue with."

Together, they carried the dishes into the kitchen.

After washing it, Peter went upstairs to finish his homework.

He had barely opened his notebook when a message notification sound popped up.

MJ: "Hey, Peter."

Peter checked his phone, a smile appeared on his face seeing her message as he typed.

Peter: "Hi, MJ."

MJ: "Survived another day at the Queens Foundation?"

Peter: "Barely."

MJ: "Tough day?"

Peter: "Lots of filing. Lots of moving boxes. Nothing exciting."

MJ: "Sounds thrilling."

Peter: 😄

Peter: "What about you? How's the school paper coming along?"

MJ: "Still collecting stories for the school paper. Mr. Harrington wants something people actually want to read."

Peter: "You'll figure it out."

MJ: "I hope so."

A comfortable silence settled between them before Peter could type another word.

MJ: "Just don't work yourself too hard, okay?"

Peter smiled.

Peter: "I could say the same to you."

She grinned.

MJ: "Fair enough. See you tomorrow."

Peter: "See you."

She went offline.

Peter watched the light in her window for a second before returning to his desk.

An hour later, Peter closed his textbook and stretched.

His eyes wandered to the framed photograph beside the lamp.

Uncle Ben stood smiling, a much younger Peter perched proudly on his shoulders.

Peter picked up the frame, his thumb brushing the edge as a smile appeared, remembering the old days when Ben taught him to repair his bicycle.

He set it back carefully, switched off the light, and climbed into bed.

Within minutes, sleep found him.

But the city never really slept.

Somewhere across the city, a man named Hammerhead watched the footage with cold amusement inside his base.

"So," he muttered, lifting his glass of bourbon, "the bug's still buzzing."

He picked up his phone.

"Find me Aleksei Sytsevich," he said. "Tell him I've got an offer."

## Chapter 3: The City That Never Sleeps

Night had fallen over **Midtown**, but the city's pulse never slowed. Neon lights flickered against rain-slicked streets; car horns blared, and sirens threaded through the concrete canyons like anxious whispers. Somewhere in the shadows, a different kind of storm was brewing.

In a dimly lit warehouse near the docks, **Hammerhead** sat behind a scarred steel desk, a half-empty glass of bourbon in one hand. His eyes followed the grainy footage on a flickering monitor: Spider-Man swinging through Midtown, saving lives, stopping small-time criminals with ease.

"The kid's a problem," Hammerhead muttered, voice rough like gravel. He slammed the glass down, the amber liquid spilling onto the desk. "I've tried the usual muscle. He's smarter than anyone I've sent. Stronger. Faster. But everyone has a weakness."

From the shadows, a figure stepped forward, **Aleksei Sytsevich**, nervous but desperate. "Mr. Hammerhead... you said you had a way. A way to... fix this. To end it."

Hammerhead smiled, the faintest glint of steel in his eyes. "I've got something better. Power, Aleksei. Enough to crush him. Enough to make sure no one calls him hero again."

Aleksei's hands trembled. "What... what do I have to do?"

Hammerhead leaned forward, voice low and dangerous. "Take this." He placed a small vial on the desk, glowing faintly in the dim light. "From Oscorp. One shot. Inject it. I promise you'll have strength like you've never dreamed. And Spider-Man... will regret the day he stepped into this city."

Aleksei swallowed hard, staring at the liquid as if it burned. "And if it goes wrong?"

Hammerhead's smile widened. "It won't. Or... if it does, it'll make Spider-Man pay twice."

The words lingered in the stale air. Aleksei picked up the vial, the weight of choice pressing down on him like a hammer. He closed his eyes. He thought about the ridicule, the failures, the endless shadow of Spider-Man's victories. And then, with a shuddering hand, he injected the serum into his veins.

Pain flared instantly. Heat, strength, something monstrous swelling inside him. His bones shifted, muscles tore and rebuilt, sinew stretching. The mirror across the room reflected the transformation: humans replaced by beast, Aleksei replaced by **the Rhino**.

He roared, a sound that split the concrete walls and rattled Hammerhead's whiskey glass. Fists clenched, the knuckles cracking like firecrackers. "I... I feel... unstoppable," he growled.

Hammerhead nodded, leaning back in his chair. Satisfaction etched in every line of his face. "Go. Show him what happens when you bite more than you can chew."

### Midtown High

Meanwhile, the next morning at Midtown High, the science lab was unusually quiet.

Sunlight filtered through the tall windows, reflecting off glass beakers and scattered circuit boards. Peter Parker sat at his workstation beside Gwen Stacy, carefully soldering a tiny circuit into place.

His hands moved with practiced precision.

His mind was somewhere else.

*Chemistry quiz... finish history homework... volunteer work after school... patrol tonight if things stay quiet...*

A sigh escaped him before he realized it.

Across the table, Gwen looked up from her notebook.

"You've been staring at that circuit for five minutes."

Peter blinked.

"What?"

"You haven't soldered anything."

He glanced down.

She was right.

Peter smiled sheepishly.

"Sorry."

Gwen leaned back in her chair.

"What's going on?"

"Nothing, just thinking about work after school"

"They've been keeping me pretty busy."

Gwen rested her chin on her hand.

"You know..."

He looked up.

"...you're allowed to take a break once in a while."

Peter laughed quietly.

"I wish."

"I'm serious."

"I miss when you'd spend lunch arguing with me over crazy experiments instead of looking like you're planning tomorrow before today's even over."

Peter smiled.

"I miss that too."

"So skip a day."

He shook his head.

"I can't."

Gwen frowned.

"How important is this volunteer work? Nothing's going to happen if you take one day off."

Peter opened his mouth to answer—

His chest tightened.

A sharp pulse shot through his body.

His Spider-Sense.

His eyes snapped toward the windows.

Outside...

A deafening crash shook the building.

Students screamed.

Metal twisted.

Concrete exploded into the air as a massive gray figure burst through the front gates, swinging a massive steel pipe like it weighed nothing.

Rhino.

Peter's heart sank.

"...Not today."

The streets groaned beneath the first blows of Rhino's rampage.

Outside Midtown High, students who had been leaving early scattered in panic. Teachers shouted for everyone to get back inside as windows rattled from the impact. Rhino charged through the front gates, roaring as he tore through parked cars and hurled debris across the courtyard.

Gwen turned toward the window.

"What was—"

Before she could finish, Peter had already grabbed his backpack.

He sprinted into the hallway, weaving through frightened students as alarms began to blare throughout the school.

Reaching into his bag, he pulled out his web-shooters and mask. In one swift motion, he peeled off his T-shirt, revealing the red-and-blue suit hidden beneath.

It was time for Spider-Man to go to work.

## Chapter 4: Chaos at Midtown High

Outside Midtown High, panic spread like wildfire.

Teachers rushed students back toward the building as alarms echoed through the campus.

"Everybody inside!"

"Move! Move!"

Most students didn't need convincing.

They sprinted toward the entrances, some crying, others pulling terrified friends by the arm.

Rhino strode through the front courtyard, each step cracking the pavement beneath his feet.

His breathing was heavy.

His eyes scanned the school.

"Come on..." he growled.

"I know you're here."

With a roar, he drove his steel pipe into the ground.

Concrete burst upward.

Windows shattered across the front of the building.

Inside, students screamed.

---

Flash Thompson pushed against the crowd gathering near the entrance.

"I want to see."

A teacher blocked his path.

"Absolutely not!"

Flash leaned around him, trying to catch a glimpse of the destruction outside.

Harry grabbed his shoulder.

"Flash, forget it."

"That's Spider-Man out there."

"We don't even know that."

"We will."

Flash slipped past the teacher.

"Flash!" Harry shouted.

With a frustrated sigh, Harry ran after him.

MJ saw them disappear through the doors.

"Oh, you've got to be kidding me..."

She hurried after Harry.

Gwen caught up beside her.

"We're seriously doing this?"

"We're making sure those idiots don't get themselves killed."

The four of them emerged onto the front steps of Midtown High.

The courtyard was almost empty now.

Most students had already fled inside.

Only a handful of teachers remained, desperately directing the last few people away from the gates.

Rhino stood alone in the middle of the shattered street.

Waiting.

---

The air grew unnaturally still.

Only the distant wail of approaching sirens broke the silence.

Rhino stood in the middle of the shattered courtyard, his massive chest rising and falling with each breath.

His eyes slowly scanned the school building.

"Come on..." he growled.

"I know you're here."

With a roar, he wrapped both hands around a concrete bench bolted into the courtyard.

The bolts snapped.

Teachers instinctively shielded the students closest to them.

"Everybody back!"

Flash, however, couldn't pull his eyes away.

"Come on..." he whispered.

Harry grabbed his arm.

"Flash, we need to go."

"This is insane..."

"What happened?"

**THWIP!**

Broken windows.

Terrified teachers.

Students still trying to get inside.

He paused, swallowing hard. "Uncle Ben... his death... it was my fault. I didn't stop the man who killed him. I... I thought if I could make it right somehow, I could prevent it from happening to anyone else. That's why I've been keeping this a secret. I didn't want May to overreact. I didn't want anyone to stop me. I thought I was protecting everyone."

Harry's expression shifted from disbelief to overwhelming admiration. "Peter, I thought we were best friends but now I feel like you didn't trust me enough to share this part of your life."

Gwen's voice trembled slightly, touched with a mix of awe and chagrin. "All this time, I thought you were discussing wild theories... but you were actually... doing this. You didn't have to hide it from us."

Flash crossed his arms, face conflicted, finally letting out the words he needed to say. "And all this time you pretended to be weak... so I could best you, tease you. I... I get it now."

MJ: "You know I always supported Spider-Man, supported you. We spent our entire childhood together; you could have told me."

Peter's eyes met theirs, exhausted but sincere. "I'm sorry. Truly. I thought I was doing the right thing. I was afraid... afraid I'd change everything between us. I didn't want anyone to worry."

May's eyes filled with tears; her voice shook, choked with relief and fear.

Peter reached out to her, voice soft. "I'm okay, Aunt May. I'm... still me."

May shook her head, tears streaking her face. "It's not about you being okay. It's about trust, Peter. You can't face this alone. You must let us in... we only have each other. Ben would be proud of you for the boy you turned into, and you feel the blame for what happened to him, but that doesn't give you the right to hide all of this from me or the people who loves you."

Peter nodded, swallowing the lump in his throat. "I understand... I am sorry."

Silence fell over the room for a heartbeat. Then Harry spoke, breaking it with warmth in his voice: "You won't be fighting alone. We've talked about it. We'll stand with you."

Flash nodded, a spark of newfound admiration in his eyes. "Yeah. Anything you need."

Gwen's gaze was steady and resolute.

Peter at first in disbelief, "Guys, you don't have to do this."

MJ's hand squeezed Peter's. "You need us, just as we need you. Even heroes need help."

Peter smiled faintly, a sense of weight being lifted from his shoulders, "Thanks guys."

Harry gets up, "Okay, we are going to celebrate this, our new team."

Everyone disagrees with him, Gwen talks about schoolwork, MJ talks about Peter getting rest, May talks about eating home cooked food, there is buzz all over the room.

Outside the window, Midtown continued its chaotic rhythm. But inside the house, a new era had begun. Spider-Man was no longer alone.

## Chapter 8: Web of Trust

The morning sunlight spilled across Midtown, illuminating Peter's bedroom as the five friends gathered around the small table, strewn with notebooks, gadgets, and half-finished projects. Peter leaned against the desk, still tender from the fight, but the spark in his eyes was unmistakable.

"So, what's the plan?" Flash asked, his voice carrying a mix of excitement and newfound respect. "We can't just sit around and wait for the next Rhino."

MJ smiled, rolling up her sleeves. "We've been talking. If Peter's going to be out there, we need to make sure he's never alone. We can gather information, analyze threats, and help him with tech, media coverage, even research."

Gwen adjusted her glasses, scanning the blueprints Peter had sketched for his latest web-shooter upgrades. "I can help with the science side. Spider-Man tech is still primitive compared to what he could achieve with proper support. And I want to make sure we understand what we're dealing with before it hits the streets."

Harry leaned back, a grin breaking across his face. "I'll handle logistics. Cars, routes, equipment, keeping an eye on the city's underworld. If anyone's going to help Peter without blowing his cover, it's us."

Peter shook his head slightly, smiling. "I never thought I'd see the day where all of you would willingly get involved. It's... reassuring. More than I can say."

Flash nudged him playfully. "You better believe it, Parker. After what I saw, there's no way I'm standing on the sidelines. Not again."

MJ reached over. "We've always believed in Spider-Man. And now we know why. But more than that... we believe in _you_, Peter."

The room was silent for a beat, each of them letting the weight of that moment sink in. Outside, the city continued its relentless rhythm, but here, in this small residence, a team had formed, five friends bound together by trust, courage, and loyalty.

A few hours later, as the sun dipped below the skyline, Peter suited up. Swinging through Midtown, the city's lights reflecting off his suit, he felt a **renewed sense of purpose**. People cheered from the streets, kids pointed upward in admiration, and somewhere, a camera from the local news captured his figure as he soared above the chaos.

Returning home, Peter found his friends already huddled in his living room, sketching, planning, and preparing for what was next. MJ spread out the first mock-up of a communication network. Gwen had drawn diagrams of enhanced web-shooters and defensive gear. Harry had a map of Midtown, pinpointing potential crime hotspots. Flash leaned back in his chair, smirking, clearly enjoying his new role in the group.

Peter smiled, leaning against the doorframe. "Looks like I've got a whole network now. Not just me. Us."

MJ grinned, looping her arm through his. "Exactly. Heroes need help. And we've got your back."

Gwen looked at Flash and Harry. "Think of it as... Spider-Man support staff. Only cooler."

Harry laughed. "Team Spider. Catchy, huh?"

Flash rolled his eyes but couldn't hide his smile. "As long as we keep Peter alive, I don't care what we call it."

They all laughed, the tension of the past week dissolving in camaraderie and shared purpose. Outside, the city thrummed with life, a world that would continue testing them, but inside that room, **friendship had become armor, trust had become a weapon, and loyalty had become the foundation of something far greater**.

Peter is attending school regularly, and to everyone's surprise, Flash was showing interest in studies, and the teacher was impressed. The school day ended, and the students left with copies of the **school newspaper**, front page emblazoned with:

**"Spider-Man and Wolverine Save Midtown High — Heroes Among Us!"**

Peter held the paper, smiling as his friends crowded around. Cheers, laughter, and playful teasing filled the air. Flash ruffled Peter's hair. Gwen and Harry exchanged quiet smiles. MJ looks at him, chuckling at his reaction.

### Homecoming Night

The gymnasium at Midtown High looked almost unrecognizable.

Soft lights hung from the ceiling, reflecting silver streamers and balloons swaying gently to the music. The chatter of students blended with laughter and the low hum of anticipation a rare night where homework, expectations, and secrets were momentarily forgotten.

Peter Parker stood awkwardly near the entrance, tugging at the collar of a borrowed suit jacket.

"I hate this already," he muttered.

Flash clapped a hand on his shoulder. "Relax Parker, you survived Rhino. You can survive a dance."

Harry adjusted his tie in the reflection of a trophy case, grinning. "Besides, you don't get out of this one. We _forced_ you to prepare for a reason."

Peter exhaled, scanning the room until he saw her.

Across the gym, Mary Jane Watson stood beside Gwen near the refreshment table. MJ wore a simple but elegant dress, confident without trying, her smile effortless. Gwen leaned in, whispering something that made MJ laugh softly, the kind of laugh Peter had known since childhood.

For a moment, the noise faded.

MJ looked up.

Their eyes met.

She excused herself from Gwen and walked toward Peter, every step deliberate, calm. Peter felt his heart hammer louder than any fight he'd ever been in.

"Hey," she said gently.

"Hey," he replied, suddenly forgetting every sentence he'd rehearsed in his head.

MJ tilted her head, studying him, the nerves, the posture, the boy she'd known forever trying not to look terrified. Her smile softened.

"I know," she said.

Peter blinked. "You... know-"

She nodded. "About your feelings? Yeah. It wasn't exactly a secret."

He swallowed. "MJ, I—"

She stepped closer, extending her hand. "You don't have to worry about it. Just... dance with me."

For a second, Peter hesitated; then he took her hand.

They stepped onto the dance floor together as the music swelled, slow and steady. The world narrowed to movement and rhythm. Peter wasn't Spider-Man here. He wasn't carrying guilt or responsibility or bruises under his skin.

He was just Peter Parker.

Flash leaned against the bleachers nearby, arms folded, watching the dance floor with a half-smile that didn't quite reach his eyes.

Gwen stepped up beside him, mirroring his stance. "You look... thoughtful. That's new."

Flash scoffed softly. "Don't spread it around. I've got a reputation."

She glanced at him. "You ever feel like people already decided who you are before you even open your mouth?"

Flash tilted his head, surprised. "Yeah."

A beat. "More than they should."

Gwen studied him for a moment. "You're not who you pretend to be."

He let out a short laugh. "Funny. I was about to say the same about you."

They watched Peter and MJ on the dance floor, laughing awkwardly, completely unaware of anything beyond each other.

"My dad's a cop," Gwen said casually, like it wasn't a big thing. "Teaches you to notice patterns. People, I mean."

Flash shifted his weight. "Yeah. I get that."

She glanced at him. "You don't talk about your family much."

He hesitated, just long enough to be noticeable, then shrugged. "Some things aren't great party conversation."

Gwen nodded. No pressure. No follow-up.

Instead, she smiled. "Well... for what it's worth, you're doing okay tonight."

Flash smirked. "High praise."

They stood there a moment longer, the music drifting over them, the night warm and forgiving.

"Hey," Flash said finally, pushing off the bleachers. "Wanna grab something to drink? Before someone starts a slow song and makes it weird."

Gwen smiled. "Lead the way."

They walked off together, not a couple, just two people choosing not to be alone for the moment.

Harry stood off to the side, drink in hand, eyes on Peter and MJ, smiling quietly, satisfied. Things were finally falling into place.

Then, a subtle vibration.

One by one, their watches lit up.

Red.

Peter felt it instantly. The shift. The call.

MJ noticed his expression change before he even spoke. She glanced down at his wrist, then back at him.

"Time?" she asked.

Peter nodded, apologetic. "I'm sorry..."

MJ squeezed his hand, smiling not sad, not afraid. Just understanding.

"Go get them, tiger."

Peter's breath caught. He smiled back, nodding at Flash, Gwen, and Harry. They moved instinctively, blocking sightlines, creating distractions, laughter rising as Flash deliberately knocked over a stack of chairs.

Peter slipped away.

By the time anyone noticed, Peter was already gone.

High above the city, Spider-Man swung from web to web, the wind in his face, the cheers below him, and the knowledge that **he was never truly alone**.

Somewhere in the distance, the city's lights glimmered like stars, hinting at the challenges to come, the threats yet unseen, and the friends who would stand beside him, ready for the next swing.
