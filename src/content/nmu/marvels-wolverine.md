## Chapter 1

### **Opening Scene – North HQ – Danger Room**

Steel doors seal.

The chamber expands into a simulated collapsing city block.

Dust. Fire. Sirens.

A voice announces:

"Urban crisis simulation. Level Seven."

On one side—

The **New X-Men**:

Iceman

Colossus

Jubilee

Kitty Pryde

Archangel

Magik

Psylocke

On the other—

**X-Force**:

Bishop

Warpath

Gambit

Sunspot

At the center—

Wolverine

Still. Watching.

### Phase 1 – Two Philosophies

Simulation begins.

A skyscraper fractures.

Hostile drones swarm.

Civilians scatter.

**Bishop** immediately calls formation.

X-Force moves aggressively.

Warpath smashes through advancing units.

Gambit charges debris and detonates it mid-air.

Sunspot ignites, blasting drone clusters.

Efficient.

Lethal.

But civilians remain exposed.

On the other flank—

Iceman freezes collapsing staircases.

Colossus shields a group of trapped projections.

Kitty phases through rubble to extract hostages.

Jubilee disorients enemies with light bursts.

Archangel sweeps civilians upward to safety.

Magik opens stepping discs for evacuation routes.

Psylocke neutralizes incoming threats with psychic precision.

Careful.

Coordinated.

But slower.

Logan observes.

Then moves.

### Phase 2 – Correction

A drone targets civilians.

Logan slices it mid-flight.

"Priority," he growls. "People first."

Bishop glances at him but doesn't argue.

Another explosion destabilizes the structure.

Colossus braces a collapsing support beam—

Too much weight.

Logan leaps, claws digging into concrete.

He reinforces the beam alongside him.

Metal and flesh holding back a digital catastrophe.

"Move!" he commands.

They move.

### Phase 3 – Teaching Moment

A younger mutant hesitates; Jubilee flinches at crossfire.

Logan lands beside her.

"Focus."

She steadies.

Blasts incoming drones precisely.

He nods once.

Approval.

Across the room, Gambit smirks.

"Y'know, old man, some of us like to end fights quick."

Logan turns.

"And leave bodies behind?"

Silence.

Warpath folds his arms.

Bishop watches carefully.

This isn't about power.

It's about philosophy.

### Simulation Ends

The city dissolves.

Silence returns.

Logan walks between both teams.

"You," he nods toward X-Force, "hit hard."

Then toward New X-Men.

"You hesitate."

He looks at all of them.

"World ain't clean. But it ain't target practice either."

A beat.

"Figure out the line."

No applause.

No pep talk.

Just expectation.

### Private Beat

The teams disperse.

Magik opens a portal and disappears.

Bishop exchanges a quiet look with Logan before leaving.

Gambit flicks a card lazily and follows.

Logan remains alone.

The room resets to sterile steel.

### Locker room

Logan finish taking a shower and wears his normal clothes, black T-Shirt and jeans.

Suddenly he hears Professor's voice in his head, he is being called to the principal's office.

### Xavier's School – Principal's Office

Logan arrived near the principal's office.

He pushes open the office door without knocking.

Inside—

Professor X

Cyclops

Jean Grey

Storm

And three unfamiliar figures stand with quiet discipline.

Japanese.

Composed.

The moment Logan enters—

All three bow.

Logan pauses.

"...Do I owe somebody money?"

Scott suppresses a reaction.

The woman steps forward.

Yukio

Sharp eyes. Confident.

"Master Mariko sent me."

Logan stares at her blankly.

"She is in grave danger. You are the only person she trusts."

Logan's brow tightens slightly.

"I don't know who this Mariko is."

A second figure steps forward—

Shiro Yoshida

Calm. Controlled. Dangerous beneath the surface.

"You made a promise."

Logan's jaw tightens.

"What promise?"

The third man steps forward — mid 40s, composed, dressed traditionally but with modern tactical accents. A blade rests at his side. Not theatrical. Practical.

Takeda Ren

"You cannot betray her."

Logan steps closer.

"You're talkin' like you know me."

Yukio's voice softens slightly.

"We do."

Silence fills the room.

Logan looks at Charles.

"What's going on?"

### Charles' Decision

Charles' expression is grave.

"There is something I have withheld from you."

Logan's eyes harden.

"You said you only restored what I needed."

"I did."

A pause.

"But some memories were too unstable to return at once."

Logan takes a step closer.

"What did you keep from me?"

Charles moves forward slowly.

"Not kept."

"Protected."

Logan doesn't look convinced.

Charles' voice lowers.

"It is time."

Scott shifts slightly.

Jean watches carefully.

Storm remains still.

Logan exhales sharply.

"Do it."

### The Restoration

Charles raises a hand gently to Logan's temple.

No dramatic glowing effects.

Just concentration.

The room fades to white—

### Memory Surge

Rain.

A Japanese courtyard.

Cherry blossoms against gray sky.

A woman's laughter.

Mariko Yashida

Her hand in his.

Training under falling leaves.

Her voice:

"You will return?"

Logan, younger in spirit:

"I will."

Cut—

Snow.

Gunfire.

Black vans.

Stryker's men.

Metal restraints.

Lab lights.

Screaming.

Adamantium bonding.

Memory collapsing.

Everything erased.

### Back to Present

Logan jerks back violently.

Stumbles.

Breathing heavy.

"Mariko..."

The name leaves his mouth like it's been buried for decades.

Scott steps forward instinctively.

"Logan—"

"Don't."

Logan holds up a hand.

Too much.

Japan.

Love.

Promise.

Betrayal.

Abduction.

It crashes into him all at once.

He looks at Yukio.

Now recognition flickers.

He looks at Shiro and Ren.

Then at Charles.

Logan's breathing steadies — but his eyes are different now.

Haunted.

"I promised her."

Silence.

He turns abruptly and walks toward the door.

Scott moves again.

"Logan—"

Logan doesn't stop.

"Leave me alone."

The door shuts.

### Aftermath – Office

Yukio stands motionless.

Shiro's expression unreadable.

Ren bows slightly.

Charles speaks quietly.

"When Logan left Japan, he intended to return."

He looks at Scott and Jean.

"He never had the chance."

Storm's voice is soft.

"Stryker."

Charles nods.

"He was taken before he could fulfill that promise."

Jean exhales slowly.

"And she waited."

Charles closes his eyes briefly.

"Yes."

Logan standing alone in a hallway.

Hands trembling slightly.

Not from fear.

From memory.

For the first time in decades—

He remembers love.

### Mansion – Terrace

The city lights below flicker quietly.

Wind moves across the rooftop.

Logan sits on the ledge, elbows on knees, staring at the sky.

No cigar.

No drink.

Just silence.

Footsteps behind him.

He doesn't turn.

"I figured you'd come up here," Scott says quietly.

Cyclops

Jean Grey

Storm

They stand a few steps back.

Giving him space.

Jean speaks first.

"You remember everything?"

Logan exhales slowly.

"Enough."

A beat.

"She was... different."

Scott folds his arms.

"Mariko?"

Logan nods.

"She wasn't afraid of me."

Wind shifts.

"She trained harder than most men I fought beside. Learned fast. Didn't complain."

Storm watches him carefully.

"You loved her."

Logan doesn't answer immediately.

"Yeah."

It's quiet.

Jean steps closer.

"What happened?"

Logan's eyes stay on the skyline.

"I left to take care of something back home. Told her I'd come back."

His jaw tightens.

"I meant to."

Scott's voice is steady.

"But you were taken."

Logan nods once.

"Stryker's men grabbed me before I got the chance."

A long silence.

"I don't know what she thought," Logan says. "Probably that I walked."

Jean's voice softens.

"She waited."

That hits.

Logan swallows.

"I broke a promise."

Storm steps beside him.

"No. It was taken from you."

Logan shakes his head.

"Intent don't change absence."

He stares at his hands.

"I don't know what I'm supposed to do now."

A calm voice interrupts.

"You go."

They all turn.

Professor X rolls forward from the doorway.

"She still trusts you."

Logan looks at him.

"How can I face her?"

Charles answers gently.

"She sent the only people she trusts to retrieve you. That speaks louder than resentment."

Logan's voice lowers.

"How do I face them?"

Before Charles can respond—

Footsteps approach from the stairwell.

Yukio steps onto the terrace first.

Behind her, Shiro.

And Ren.

Yukio bows slightly.

"Professor told us what was done to you."

Ren steps forward.

If he speaks, it should be measured.

"If we had known sooner what you endured..."

He bows his head slightly.

"I would not have judged you so harshly... Kuzuri-san."

Logan lets out a low chuckle.

"Been a long time since I heard that."

Shiro crosses his arms.

"I thought we could resolve this ourselves."

His tone is controlled, not arrogant.

"But this is... sensitive."

Logan studies him.

"What do you mean?"

Yukio's eyes darken.

"It was a coup."

The word lands heavy.

Shiro finishes:

"Kenuchio Harada."

Logan's expression changes immediately.

Not confusion.

Recognition.

He knew that name.

Even before the memory returned.

"He wouldn't."

Ren's voice is firm.

"He did."

Yukio adds quietly:

"Mariko was taken by her own blood."

Wind moves harder across the terrace.

Logan stands slowly.

Claws do not extend.

But something sharper settles in his eyes.

"Then we go."

No dramatic speech.

Just decision.

Scott steps closer.

"You're not going alone."

Logan looks at him.

For a second, the old instinct flashes—

Handle it alone.

Then he shakes his head.

"This one... I gotta face."

Jean steps forward.

"We'll be here."

Storm nods.

"You are not the man who left Japan."

Logan looks toward the sky again.

"No."

A beat.

"I ain't."

Cut to black.

## Chapter 2 – Arrival

### **Exterior – Tokyo – Night**

Rain.

Not heavy. Just enough to make the neon bleed across asphalt.

The jet touches down quietly at a private airstrip outside the city.

No ceremony.

No press.

Just waiting black vehicles.

Logan steps out first.

Black formal suit.

No tie.

Hair pulled back slightly.

Hands in pockets.

He doesn't look like a superhero.

He looks like a man attending something unfinished.

Behind him:

Yukio.

Shiro.

Ren.

They don't speak much.

Tokyo hums in the distance.

### The Taxi Ride

Instead of armored convoy, they choose anonymity.

A simple taxi.

Logan in the back seat.

Yukio beside him.

Shiro and Ren in the vehicle behind.

City lights pass across Logan's face.

He closes his eyes.

Inhales.

Then—

A subtle shift.

His eyes open again.

"Three vehicles," he says quietly.

Yukio doesn't react.

"How far?"

"Close enough."

Ren's voice crackles through comm discreetly.

"We see them."

Logan nods slightly.

"They've been with us since the airstrip."

Shiro's voice is calm.

"Harada does not waste time."

Logan almost smiles.

"Neither do I."

### The Setup

They allow it.

The taxi drives out of the city.

Industrial outskirts.

Fewer lights.

More open space.

Logan taps the glass.

"Here."

Taxi stops.

Yukio pays.

Ren bows politely to the driver.

"Thank you. Please leave."

The taxi pulls away quickly.

Silence settles.

Rain continues.

Headlights appear behind them.

Three black SUVs.

Engines cut.

Doors open.

Figures step out.

Not theatrical.

Not exaggerated.

Modernized shinobi gear.

Light armor.

Night optics.

Carbon blades.

Disciplined.

Harada doesn't send amateurs.

### The Confrontation

They spread out.

Silent.

Logan stands still in the rain.

Hands still in pockets.

Ren unsheathes his blade quietly.

Shiro's hands glow faintly beneath his sleeves.

Yukio adjusts her stance.

One masked leader steps forward.

Voice distorted through mask.

"You should have stayed away."

Logan tilts his head slightly.

"So what's the protocol here?"

A beat.

"Do I bow first and say ‘konnichiwa'?"

It's dry.

No smile.

Just faint irritation.

The leader signals.

The ninjas move.

### The Fight

They're fast.

Not reckless.

Coordinated.

Two break toward Shiro — mistake.

Sunfire steps into them without raising his voice. Heat flares in tight, disciplined bursts — not wild firestorms. The air distorts. One attacker's weapon glows red, forcing him to drop it. The other is thrown back by a focused blast against his chest armor.

No fatalities.

Just efficiency.

Ren moves before the next trio finishes their arc.

His blade is quiet.

Economical.

He disarms one with a twist, sweeps another to the pavement, and halts the third's strike inches from his throat — not killing. Just deciding.

Yukio doesn't posture.

She moves.

Low.

Fast.

A shuriken flashes from her hand — not theatrical, not spinning endlessly — just enough to sever a strap on a weapon harness mid-swing. She pivots, drives an elbow into a masked jaw, sweeps a leg, and rolls back to her feet before the body fully hits the ground.

Her movements are disciplined Japanese martial arts — sharp, balanced, economical.

No wasted motion.

Logan still hasn't drawn claws.

He lets one come close.

Close enough to smell the metal.

The blade cuts rain before it cuts air.

He sidesteps.

Grabs the wrist.

Snaps it clean.

Then—

SNIKT.

Claws extend.

Not dramatic.

Just necessary.

He disables tendons. Slashes armor straps. Cuts weapons from hands.

Surgical.

One attacker lunges from behind.

Logan doesn't turn.

"Left."

Yukio is already there.

She pivots mid-step, drives her heel into the attacker's knee, follows with a blade-hand strike to the throat. The man collapses, unconscious.

Now the leader steps forward.

He's better.

Not louder.

Not emotional.

His stance is disciplined. Blade angled properly. Distance controlled.

He tests Logan with short strikes — not wild swings.

Steel meets adamantium.

Sparks jump in the rain.

For a moment—

It's quiet precision.

Not rage.

Just skill.

Logan blocks. Redirects. Closes distance. Hooks the blade, twists, disarms.

He pins the man to the pavement with a claw at his collar.

"Where is she?"

The leader's voice is calm behind the mask.

"You are already late."

No taunting.

Just statement.

Ren steps beside Logan.

"Answer."

The leader remains silent.

Then—

A distant explosion ripples across the industrial skyline.

Low.

Heavy.

Not random.

Shiro looks toward the smoke rising faintly against neon glow.

"That was coordinated."

Logan releases the man.

He doesn't gut him.

Doesn't finish him.

He stands slowly.

Rain runs down his face.

He retracts his claws.

"Harada's movin' pieces."

No anger.

Just assessment.

Yukio retrieves one of her thrown blades without comment.

Ren wipes his steel clean.

Shiro's flames die down.

Logan looks toward the smoke.

"Let's move."

They leave the fallen where they are.

Alive.

Beaten.

Message delivered both ways.
