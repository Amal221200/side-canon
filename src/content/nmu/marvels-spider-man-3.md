## Chapter One: Back To Campus

**Massachusetts Institute of Technology**

**Cambridge, Massachusetts**

**September, 2022**

Summer had quietly surrendered to autumn.

A gentle breeze drifted across the Charles River, carrying with it the unmistakable scent of fresh leaves and coffee from the cafés scattered around Kendall Square. Students crossed the MIT campus in every direction, some dragging overpacked suitcases behind them, others balancing cardboard boxes filled with books, laptops and the pieces of another year of their lives.

The campus felt louder than it had in months.

Excited conversations echoed between the Infinite Corridor and the Great Dome. Clubs had already claimed every available patch of grass with banners promising robotics competitions, hackathons, rowing teams and impossible career opportunities. Professors hurried between buildings while first-year students stared at maps, trying to understand why every building seemed to be identified by a number instead of a name.

For most people, it was simply another semester.

For Peter Parker...

...it was the beginning of the last one.

He adjusted the strap of his backpack as he stepped through Killian Court, slowing almost unconsciously.

Three years.

Three years since he'd first walked onto this campus with his friends, wondering what it looks like coming to a very prestigious institue.

Back then everything had been unfamiliar.

The classrooms.

The professors.

The impossible workload.

The people.

Now...

MIT felt like home.

He smiled as he passed Building 10.

He remembered getting hopelessly lost during his first week.

He remembered staying awake for thirty-six hours before his first engineering project submission.

He remembered meeting Harry outside the dorms.

Meeting Gwen.

Meeting Cindy.

Flash nearly running him over with a bicycle because he insisted brakes were "optional weight."

Meeting MJ.

His smile grew a little.

Somehow...

...all those memories already felt like another lifetime.

"Hey, Parker!"

Peter looked up.

A student from one of his machine architecture classes waved while walking in the opposite direction.

"Welcome back!"

Peter waved back.

"You too."

A few more familiar faces nodded as they passed.

Some recognised him from engineering labs.

Others from robotics competitions.

A couple simply knew him as the quiet guy who somehow managed to answer impossible exam questions without ever sounding like he knew he was the smartest person in the room.

Peter still wasn't used to that.

He doubted he ever would be.

His phone buzzed.

**MJ**

> *Where are you?*

Peter smiled.

> *Campus.*

Three dots appeared almost immediately.

> *Helpful.*

Before he could answer again—

"PARKER!"

That voice.

Peter didn't even have to turn around.

He laughed first.

Then spun just in time to catch Harry Osborn barreling straight into him.

The hug nearly knocked the air from his lungs.

"Okay—"

Peter stumbled backwards.

"—still stronger than you look."

Harry stepped back with a grin that hadn't changed in four years.

"You've been impossible to get hold of."

Peter adjusted his backpack.

"I was working."

"I know."

Harry folded his arms.

"At Stark Industries."

Peter scratched the back of his neck.

"...Yeah."

"And?"

"And what?"

Harry stared at him.

"You interned at Stark Industries."

"...Yeah."

"And that's all you're giving me?"

Peter shrugged.

"It was good."

Harry burst out laughing.

"'It was good.'"

"You spent three months at one of the greatest engineering companies on Earth and your review is..."

Harry put on an exaggerated serious voice.

"'Yeah... it was good.'"

Peter couldn't help laughing.

"It was really busy."

"I'll bet."

Harry looked him up and down.

"You look different."

Peter frowned.

"I do?"

"A little."

Harry tilted his head.

"I don't know..."

"You look..."

"...older."

Peter chuckled.

"I was hoping for 'more handsome.'"

"I wasn't going to lie."

Peter shoved him lightly.

Harry shoved him back.

It was effortless.

Comfortable.

Like no time had passed at all.

A loud crashing sound interrupted them.

Both turned.

Flash Thompson stood twenty feet away surrounded by two suitcases, a duffel bag, an overstuffed backpack and what looked suspiciously like a car tyre.

Peter blinked.

"...Why do you have a tyre?"

Flash looked offended.

"It's not a tyre."

Harry raised an eyebrow.

"It is literally a tyre."

"It's an experimental performance wheel assembly."

"...For your dorm room?"

Flash sighed dramatically.

"You people have absolutely no appreciation for engineering."

He grabbed one suitcase.

It refused to move.

Flash looked down.

"...Who zipped my bag into the wheel?"

Peter and Harry exchanged a glance.

Then simultaneously burst out laughing.

"Oh, laugh it up."

Flash yanked harder.

Nothing.

Harry walked over without saying a word, crouched down and quietly untangled the wheel from the suitcase handle.

"There."

Flash looked genuinely embarrassed.

"...Thanks."

"No problem."

Peter noticed something.

Three years ago...

Flash probably would've pretended he didn't need help.

Now...

He simply accepted it.

It was a small change.

But somehow...

...it said everything.

Flash slung the bag over his shoulder.

"So..."

He looked at Peter.

"Stark Industries."

Peter groaned.

"You too?"

"What?"

Flash smirked.

"Word gets around."

Before Peter could answer—

"Still bullying Parker?"

The three turned together.

Walking across the courtyard was Gwen Stacy.

A notebook tucked beneath one arm.

A messenger bag hanging from one shoulder.

Her blonde hair moved gently with the breeze as she smiled at the group.

Flash immediately straightened.

"I don't bully anybody."

Gwen raised an eyebrow.

"No?"

"No."

She looked at Peter.

Peter shrugged.

"He only bullies inanimate objects now."

Flash pointed at the wheel.

"It started it."

Gwen laughed.

"I missed you guys."

Gwen reached them with an easy smile, setting her bag down beside Flash's mountain of luggage.

"So," she said, looking around the group, "we survived another summer."

"Barely," Flash muttered.

"You've only been back five minutes."

"And I've already carried forty kilos across campus."

Peter looked down at the wheel still hanging from Flash's bag.

"Forty-five."

Flash sighed dramatically.

"This is why engineers don't have friends."

Harry looked around.

"I count four."

"...Five if MJ still likes you."

Flash frowned.

"That's... actually reassuring."

Before anyone could respond, another familiar voice drifted across the courtyard.

"I heard that."

Everyone turned.

Mary Jane Watson walked toward them with the confidence that only came from finally feeling like she belonged somewhere. A laptop bag rested comfortably over one shoulder, her auburn hair swaying gently as she crossed the courtyard.

Peter's face softened almost instantly.

She noticed.

"So..."

She folded her arms.

"You ignored my message."

"I replied."

"You replied 'Campus.'"

"...Technically accurate."

MJ tried to maintain a serious expression.

She lasted exactly two seconds.

"You've been spending too much time with engineers."

Peter smiled.

"I *am* an engineer."

"You weren't this bad before Stark."

Harry quietly leaned toward Gwen.

"They've started speaking in processor architectures."

"I heard that."

MJ shot back without looking.

Harry raised both hands.

"I surrender."

Peter stepped closer.

For a brief moment the noise of the campus seemed to disappear.

"No hug?" he asked.

MJ smiled warmly.

"I was waiting to see if you'd offer."

Peter laughed before wrapping his arms around her.

Nothing about it was awkward.

Nothing felt uncertain.

It was simply two people who had spent months apart finally finding each other again.

When they stepped back, MJ looked him over carefully.

"You've lost weight."

"I have?"

"You forget to eat when you're working."

Peter opened his mouth.

Closed it again.

"...Maybe."

"I knew it."

She reached into her bag and pulled out an energy bar.

"I bought extras."

Flash stared.

"...Do you carry emergency food specifically for him?"

MJ handed it to Peter.

"Yes."

Peter accepted it without protest.

Harry shook his head.

"You two are adorable."

"We're efficient," MJ corrected.

"That's somehow even nerdier."

Peter unwrapped the bar.

"You should've seen her this summer."

MJ immediately looked at him.

"...Peter."

"What?"

"You promised."

Harry's eyes lit up.

"Oh, now I definitely want to know."

Peter grinned.

"She rewrote an entire graphics optimization pipeline because she said the engineers 'weren't thinking at the hardware level.'"

MJ sighed.

"They weren't."

Flash blinked.

"They weren't?"

"No."

Harry looked between them.

"I understood maybe three words."

Peter nodded.

"That's about how my first week at Stark went."

MJ smiled.

"You survived."

"Barely."

"And yet somehow..."

She reached up and straightened his slightly crooked collar.

"...you still can't dress yourself."

Peter looked down.

"I did this on purpose."

"No."

She fixed it properly.

"There."

Flash looked at Harry.

"I don't know whether that's romantic or terrifying."

Harry smiled.

"Both."

A few footsteps approached from behind them.

Unlike everyone else...

These were almost silent.

Cindy Moon stopped beside Gwen, her hands tucked casually into the pockets of her jacket.

"So..."

She looked around.

"Did I miss everyone making fun of Peter?"

Peter looked over.

"Only the first round."

"Good."

She smiled.

"I hate missing traditions."

Without another word she lightly bumped her shoulder against Harry's.

He instinctively reached down, lacing his fingers with hers.

It happened so naturally that no one even commented on it.

Peter noticed.

He smiled quietly.

Harry caught his eye.

Peter simply nodded.

Harry smiled back.

No words needed.

Flash looked around at everyone gathered together.

"...Well."

"This is weird."

Gwen tilted her head.

"What is?"

"We're seniors."

Silence.

Just for a second.

Everyone instinctively looked around the campus.

Freshmen hurried between buildings carrying maps.

Parents took photographs.

Clubs competed for attention.

Everything looked exactly like it had four years ago.

Except...

...they weren't the new students anymore.

Harry exhaled.

"Feels strange."

"A little."

Peter looked toward the Great Dome.

"I still remember getting lost on the first day."

"You *were* lost."

MJ laughed.

"You walked into Building 13 looking for Building 31."

Peter frowned.

"They're numbers."

"They're completely different numbers."

"I was under pressure."

"You were following Google Maps."

"It betrayed me."

Cindy laughed.

"I remember Flash trying to convince everyone he already knew the campus."

Flash immediately pointed at her.

"I did."

"You asked a janitor where your own dorm was."

"I was verifying."

Harry burst out laughing.

"Verifying?"

"It was a confidence check."

"For who?"

"...The janitor."

Even Gwen couldn't keep a straight face anymore.

Flash looked around.

"You know..."

"I've missed this."

Nobody answered.

Because nobody needed to.

They all had.

Harry adjusted the strap of his backpack.

"So..."

"Our table?"

Peter smiled.

"The cafeteria."

Flash nodded confidently.

"If somebody stole it..."

Harry finished the sentence.

"...We're taking it back."

Together, the six of them began walking across the MIT campus.

Students flowed around them in every direction.

Conversations came and went.

Bicycles passed.

The bells from a distant clock tower echoed across the courtyard.

Peter walked in the middle of the group.

MJ beside him.

Harry and Cindy just ahead.

Flash arguing with Gwen over whether electric cars had "souls."

For just a moment...

He allowed himself to stop thinking about deadlines.

About Stark.

About Spider-Man.

About the future.

He simply watched his friends laugh.

And realized...

This wasn't just where he'd studied for four years.

This was home.

---

### Cafeteria

The familiar chatter of the cafeteria washed over them long before they reached the entrance.

Nothing had changed.

Students still fought over the best tables near the windows.

Someone had already occupied the piano in the corner.

The smell of coffee mixed with pizza and fresh pastries filled the air.

Flash stopped in the doorway.

"...Moment of truth."

"What?" Peter asked.

"Our table."

Harry looked toward the far corner of the cafeteria.

Empty.

Flash raised both fists triumphantly.

"YES!"

Several nearby students looked over.

Flash lowered his voice.

"...I meant... nice."

Peter laughed.

"Subtle."

"I've matured."

"You yelled."

"I yelled responsibly."

They claimed the large corner table they'd occupied for almost four years.

It had started almost by accident during freshman year.

Then it became tradition.

Now...

No one questioned it.

Everyone instinctively sat in the same places.

Peter and MJ beside each other.

Harry opposite Peter.

Cindy beside Harry.

Flash dropped into the chair across from Gwen.

Nobody consciously chose.

It simply happened.

Harry leaned back.

"...Feels good."

"It does."

For a few moments, nobody spoke.

They simply watched the organized chaos around them.

Freshmen trying to understand meal plans.

Upperclassmen already complaining about assignments.

Graduate students looking like they'd forgotten sleep existed.

Peter smiled.

"It's weird."

"What is?" Gwen asked.

"We used to be those freshmen."

Flash laughed.

"I wasn't."

"You literally asked where the cafeteria was."

"I was testing campus security."

MJ didn't even look up from setting her laptop bag beside her chair.

"You've used that excuse three times today."

"It's versatile."

A waitress walked over with a smile.

"Welcome back."

Harry looked surprised.

"You remember us?"

She laughed.

"You've practically lived here for four years."

Flash looked proud.

"I've built a legacy."

"You've built a tab."

Everyone laughed.

After ordering, the conversation naturally drifted to the question everyone had been waiting to ask.

Harry looked at Peter.

"So..."

"How was Stark?"

Five pairs of eyes turned toward him.

Peter rubbed the back of his neck.

"It was..."

He searched for the right word.

"...everything I imagined."

"That's saying something," Gwen replied.

Peter nodded.

"I spent the first week convinced somebody had accidentally let me into the wrong building."

Flash grinned.

"And then?"

"And then I realized everyone there was smarter than me."

MJ smiled knowingly.

"And?"

"And it was awesome."

That answer surprised everyone.

Peter continued.

"They don't care how old you are."

"They don't care where you came from."

"If your idea works..."

"...they'll listen."

He looked down at his hands for a moment.

"I learned more in three months than I thought was possible."

Flash leaned forward.

"So..."

"What did you actually build?"

Peter smiled.

"I can't tell you."

"Oh, come on."

"Seriously."

"I signed enough paperwork to fill your suitcase."

Flash looked disappointed.

"...Fair."

Peter reached into his backpack.

"I *can* show you something."

He placed a familiar metallic device on the table.

Everyone immediately recognised it.

His web shooter.

Except...

It wasn't quite the same.

It was noticeably slimmer.

Cleaner.

The mechanisms looked almost seamless.

Flash picked it up carefully.

"...This is new."

Peter nodded.

"Version nine."

Cindy smiled immediately.

"I knew it."

"You noticed?"

"You reduced the profile."

She turned it over in her hand.

"...And the cartridge housing is different."

Peter looked impressed.

"I thought it'd take you longer."

She smirked.

"It would've."

"I cheated."

"How?"

"I saw it sticking out of your backpack."

Peter laughed.

"Still counts."

Flash examined it closely.

"It weighs less."

"About twenty percent."

"And the storage?"

"Larger."

Flash blinked.

"How?"

Peter tapped the redesigned cartridge.

"Better internal layout."

Cindy added,

"And stronger materials."

Peter nodded.

"Exactly."

Harry looked between them.

"You two really spent your summer doing this."

Peter shrugged.

"...Sometimes."

Cindy shrugged as well.

"We were curious."

MJ reached over.

"Can I?"

Peter handed it to her.

She inspected it differently.

Not mechanically.

Electronically.

She studied the small processor housing before looking back at Peter.

"You changed the controller."

Peter smiled.

"I knew you'd notice."

"You finally replaced the old microcontroller."

"It was becoming a bottleneck."

MJ nodded approvingly.

"And the firmware?"

Peter hesitated.

"...Needs work."

"I knew it."

She handed it back.

"The hardware's excellent."

"But you're wasting cycles."

Peter sighed dramatically.

"I've been back ten minutes."

"And I'm already getting code reviews."

"You should be grateful."

Flash looked completely lost.

"I understood maybe..."

He counted silently.

"...three words."

Harry raised a finger.

"I got 'hardware.'"

Gwen smiled.

"I recognized 'firmware.'"

Cindy leaned back.

"I understood all of it."

Flash pointed dramatically.

"Traitor."

She laughed.

"I'm literally a scientist."

"Wrong kind."

"I'm dating a businessman."

Harry looked offended.

"I have feelings."

Peter slipped the web shooter back into his bag.

"I've got a few more ideas."

MJ immediately smiled.

"I know that look."

"What look?"

"The 'I've thought about this for three months and definitely didn't sleep enough' look."

Peter couldn't argue.

Because she was absolutely right.

The waitress returned with their drinks, carefully balancing a tray that had become familiar over the years.

Two coffees.

A hot chocolate.

An iced tea.

One protein shake.

And...

She looked at Flash.

"...Three sandwiches?"

Flash shrugged.

"What?"

"You've been back for twenty minutes."

"I skipped breakfast."

"You always skip breakfast."

"I was packing."

"You packed a wheel."

"It was important."

Harry leaned back, smiling.

"I've genuinely missed this."

"So..." Gwen said, stirring her coffee. "Who's next?"

Flash immediately pointed at her.

"Nope."

She looked up.

"Nope?"

"You've successfully avoided the internship question."

"I was waiting."

"Your turn."

Gwen smiled.

"I spent the summer at Westchester."

Peter looked up immediately.

"With Dr. McCoy?"

She nodded.

"The entire summer."

Harry whistled softly.

"That's impressive."

"It was terrifying."

Everyone laughed.

"I'm serious."

"The first day he handed me three research papers..."

"...then asked me what was wrong with them."

Flash frowned.

"That's normal, isn't it?"

"They were his."

"...Oh."

Peter grinned.

"Sounds like Hank."

Gwen nodded.

"He never tells you the answer."

"He just keeps asking questions until you find it yourself."

MJ smiled.

"So..."

"What were you working on?"

"Biomaterials."

She paused for a moment.

"Mostly trying to understand how artificial tissues respond under different chemical environments."

Flash blinked.

"...English?"

Gwen laughed.

"We're trying to make replacement tissues that behave more like real ones."

"Oh."

"That actually makes sense."

Peter nodded.

"Hank always said biology isn't just chemistry."

"It's engineering."

Gwen smiled.

"He says that almost every day."

"And?"

Peter asked.

"Did you enjoy it?"

She didn't even hesitate.

"I loved every second."

Peter smiled.

"Good."

Flash watched her for a moment.

"You've got that look."

"What look?"

"The one Parker gets whenever somebody mentions new technology."

Peter looked offended.

"I don't have a look."

Everyone around the table looked at him.

"You absolutely do."

Peter sighed.

"...I hate that you're all right."

Harry laughed.

"Cindy."

He turned toward her.

"Empire State Research Institute."

"How was it?"

Cindy rested her chin on one hand.

"It was..."

"...quiet."

"Quiet?" MJ asked.

She nodded.

"Lots of cell cultures."

"Lots of microscopy."

"Lots of waiting."

Flash smiled.

"So basically..."

"...watching things grow?"

"Pretty much."

"And honestly..."

"I liked it."

She glanced at Peter.

"I think I finally understand why you enjoy spending six hours trying to fix one tiny problem."

Peter smiled knowingly.

"It gets addictive."

"It really does."

She laughed.

"There was one week where our experiment kept failing."

"I couldn't stop thinking about it."

"What was wrong?"

"The incubator."

"Temperature?"

She shook her head.

"Humidity."

Peter nodded immediately.

"That'll do it."

The rest of the table looked between them.

Harry finally sighed.

"You two have become impossible."

"What?"

"You just diagnosed a laboratory experiment in three seconds."

Peter shrugged.

"It was the obvious answer."

"It wasn't."

"It was."

"It wasn't."

MJ took a sip of her coffee.

Cindy, "It actually was."

Harry pointed dramatically.

"See?"

"This is exactly what I mean."

"I have no idea how your conversations work anymore."

"They're efficient."

Peter and Cindy answered together.

The table burst into laughter.

Flash leaned back in his chair.

"...Okay."

"My turn."

Everyone looked at him.

"I know what you're all expecting."

Peter raised an eyebrow.

"Were we?"

"You think I spent the whole summer changing tyres."

Harry nodded.

"...Little bit."

Flash looked offended.

"I'll have you know..."

"...I changed transmissions too."

More laughter.

He waited until it settled.

"I interned with Emerson Automotive."

"Prototype engineering."

Peter's expression immediately became more interested.

"Really?"

Flash nodded.

"We were testing new suspension systems for electric performance vehicles."

Gwen smiled.

"So that's why you brought a wheel."

Flash proudly pointed at her.

"Exactly."

"It wasn't random."

"I spent three months staring at these things."

Harry leaned forward.

"Did you actually get to build anything?"

Flash's face lit up.

"A little."

"They let me help redesign a steering linkage."

Peter smiled.

"That's huge."

"It was."

Flash nodded.

"The senior engineer said..."

He paused, almost embarrassed.

"...he said I had good instincts."

Nobody joked.

Nobody interrupted.

Peter simply smiled.

"Congratulations."

Flash looked down at his drink.

"Thanks."

For just a second...

The old Flash Thompson, the teenager who always needed to prove he was the toughest person in the room was gone.

In his place sat a young engineer who was quietly proud that someone had believed in his abilities.

Gwen was the first to break the silence.

"I always knew you'd be good at building things."

Flash looked up.

"You did?"

"You've always liked understanding how things work."

"You just used to express it..."

She smiled.

"...by taking them apart before asking permission."

Peter laughed.

"That explains my bicycle."

"It was an accident."

"You removed the brakes."

"They were slowing you down."

"They were keeping me alive."

"There are trade-offs."

The laughter lingered around the table.

It wasn't loud.

It didn't need to be.

It was the kind that only existed between people who had spent years collecting inside jokes no one else would understand.

Harry looked toward MJ.

"Alright."

"Your turn."

MJ took a sip of her coffee before setting the cup down.

"I interned at Nova Silicon."

Flash nodded slowly.

"I've heard of them."

"You've heard of them?"

Harry looked surprised.

"My car's autonomous driving unit uses one of their processors."

MJ smiled.

"They're everywhere."

"Phones."

"Cars."

"Medical equipment."

"Supercomputers."

"Research labs."

Peter added quietly,

"They're probably the biggest semiconductor company in the world."

MJ nodded.

"They're obsessed with efficiency."

"I spent most of the summer working with the graphics systems team."

Flash frowned.

"So..."

"You made video games?"

Peter nearly laughed into his coffee.

MJ looked horrified.

"No."

Harry grinned.

"I think you offended her."

"I absolutely did."

MJ folded her arms.

"I worked on GPU scheduling."

Flash blinked.

"...You definitely made video games."

"No."

Peter smiled.

"She's the reason games *run*."

MJ pointed at him.

"Thank you."

She looked back at Flash.

"Think of it this way."

"You design the engine."

She nodded toward Peter.

"He designs the vehicle."

"Then somebody has to make sure every part works together without wasting energy."

Flash thought for a moment.

"...Okay."

"I actually understand that."

Harry leaned forward.

"So how was it?"

MJ's eyes lit up.

"I loved it."

"There wasn't a single day where I wasn't learning something."

She laughed softly.

"I also learned I'm nowhere near as smart as I thought I was."

Peter smiled.

"I know that feeling."

"The first week I sat beside an engineer who spent twenty minutes explaining cache coherency."

Harry looked confused.

"...Should I know what that is?"

"No."

"Good."

"Neither did I."

The table laughed again.

MJ looked around at everyone.

"I think that's what surprised me the most."

"What?"

Gwen asked.

"No matter where we interned..."

"...everyone there still had more to teach us."

Peter nodded slowly.

"I liked that."

"It reminded me..."

"...we've still got a long way to go."

For a brief moment...

The conversation settled.

Not awkwardly.

Comfortably.

Six students.

Six different careers beginning to take shape.

Harry finally broke the silence.

"You know what's funny?"

"What?"

Peter asked.

Harry looked around the table.

"When we first got here..."

"I couldn't imagine any of us outside college."

Flash nodded.

"I honestly thought we'd be students forever."

"I wish."

Gwen muttered.

"But now..."

Harry continued.

"I can actually picture it."

He pointed around the table.

"Peter designing technology that changes the world."

Peter looked away awkwardly.

"Harry..."

"What?"

"It's true."

He looked at MJ.

"You'll probably end up designing the processors everybody else uses."

MJ smiled politely.

Then Gwen.

"You'll publish research papers nobody else can understand."

"I hope somebody understands them."

Harry laughed.

"Cindy."

"You'll probably discover something that changes medicine."

Cindy looked down with a small smile.

"And Flash..."

Harry paused dramatically.

"...will somehow convince an entire automotive company to redesign an engine because he doesn't like the sound it makes."

Flash grinned.

"...I might."

Peter laughed.

"I believe him."

"So do I."

Gwen added.

The smiles slowly faded into thoughtful expressions.

Harry noticed.

"...What?"

Peter looked around the cafeteria.

"One year."

Nobody answered.

"That's all we've got left."

The words hung quietly over the table.

Around them...

The cafeteria continued as normal.

Freshmen laughed.

Professors discussed research.

Someone dropped a tray.

Life moved forward.

But at their table...

Time seemed to slow.

Flash leaned back.

"...Never really thought about it."

"Graduation."

He stared down at his drink.

"It's weird."

"It is."

Gwen admitted.

Peter looked out through the cafeteria window.

"Feels like yesterday we were trying to find our classrooms."

"And next year..."

He stopped.

"...who knows where we'll all be."

Nobody had an answer.

For the first time that afternoon...

The future felt real.

Not as an assignment.

Not as an internship.

As life.

Life beyond MIT.

Peter's eyes remained on the window.

Students crossed the courtyard in groups.

Some laughed.

Some hurried toward their first lectures.

Some walked alone, headphones on, completely unaware that their lives were about to change over the next four years.

He remembered being one of them.

Wondering where he'd fit in.

Wondering if he'd made the right choice.

Now...

He couldn't imagine being anywhere else.

A vibration against the table broke the silence.

Flash's phone.

He glanced down.

The smile he'd been wearing faded almost imperceptibly.

Harry noticed first.

Flash picked up the phone.

Read the message.

Locked the screen.

Placed it face down.

Nobody said anything.

The conversation tried to recover.

"So..."

Harry looked toward Peter.

"Still swinging around New York?"

Peter smiled faintly.

"When I can."

Cindy shrugged.

"He's getting lazy."

Peter looked at her in mock disbelief.

"Lazy?"

"You took last Thursday off."

"I had a systems design report due Friday."

"You also took Saturday."

"I was asleep."

Flash looked between them.

"You two actually keep track?"

Peter nodded.

Harry.

## Chapter Two: Beyond Tomorrow

Morning sunlight reflected across Manhattan's skyline as a sleek silver town car crossed the Queensboro Bridge.

The city slowly unfolded before them.

Glass towers reached toward the clouds.

Construction cranes stood alongside buildings that had defined New York's skyline for generations.

Traffic crawled beneath elevated railways while ferries carved white trails across the East River.

Flash looked out the window.

"I don't think I've ever seen the city this early."

Peter smiled.

"It looks different."

"It feels..."

He searched for the word.

"...awake."

Harry sat across from him, arms folded comfortably.

"My dad always says New York never really sleeps."

"It just changes shifts."

MJ looked away from the city long enough to smile.

"That sounds exactly like something he'd say."

Harry shrugged.

"He's been saying it since I was a kid."

As the car entered Midtown, something enormous slowly came into view beyond the surrounding buildings.

Flash leaned forward.

"...There it is."

Oscorp Tower.

It didn't dominate the skyline because it was the tallest.

It dominated because it looked...

Different.

Its sweeping glass façade reflected the morning sun like polished crystal, while living terraces wrapped around sections of the building, filled with trees and gardens that seemed impossible at that height.

The upper floors appeared to twist gently as they climbed into the sky, each level offset just enough to catch natural sunlight.

Solar arrays blended almost invisibly into the glass.

Wind turbines rotated silently between structural supports.

Nothing about the building felt wasteful.

Every surface seemed to serve a purpose.

Peter stared through the window.

"...It's beautiful."

"It was designed that way."

Harry answered quietly.

"My dad always hated buildings that only looked impressive."

"He wanted people to understand what Oscorp believes before they even walk inside."

Flash looked confused.

"What's that?"

Harry smiled.

"That technology should work with people."

"...Not replace them."

The car rolled beneath a wide entrance canopy where employees were already arriving for work.

Scientists carrying coffee hurried toward the main doors.

Engineers discussed something over holographic tablets.

Medical researchers in white coats greeted security staff by name.

Nobody looked rushed.

Nobody looked intimidated.

They simply looked...

Purposeful.

The doors opened automatically.

The group stepped into the main atrium.

Nobody spoke.

The lobby stretched nearly six stories high.

Natural sunlight poured through the glass ceiling overhead, illuminating indoor trees that reached toward the upper balconies.

Water flowed gently beneath suspended walkways before disappearing beneath polished stone floors.

Digital displays lined the walls.

Not advertisements.

Research.

Live energy production.

Global humanitarian projects.

Medical outreach statistics.

Environmental restoration programs.

Oscorp's newest publications.

Gwen slowly turned in place.

"...This isn't a corporate headquarters."

Harry smiled.

"No."

"It isn't."

Peter looked around at the hundreds of people moving through the atrium.

Researchers.

Doctors.

Programmers.

Engineers.

Biologists.

Mathematicians.

None of them looked like extras in someone else's story.

Every one of them looked like they were trying to solve a problem.

Flash quietly exhaled.

"I suddenly feel underdressed."

MJ laughed.

"You are."

"I knew I should've worn the other shirt."

Before anyone could answer—

"Harry."

A familiar voice echoed through the atrium.

Norman Osborn descended the broad staircase overlooking the lobby with calm, measured confidence.

He wasn't surrounded by security.

He wasn't flanked by assistants.

He carried a tablet under one arm and wore the same warm smile Peter remembered from three years earlier.

Harry smiled.

"Morning, Dad."

Norman embraced his son before looking toward the rest of the group.

"It's good to see all of you again."

His eyes briefly settled on Peter.

Then Cindy.

There was a quiet understanding there.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

He remembered.

And he had kept their confidence.

Peter stepped forward first.

"It's good to see you again, Mr. Osborn."

Norman offered his hand.

"Norman."

Peter smiled.

"I don't think I'm quite there yet."

Norman laughed.

"Fair enough."

One by one, he greeted each of them.

Not as Harry's classmates.

As individuals.

"Gwen."

"I heard Dr. McCoy kept you busy this summer."

Gwen blinked.

"...You know Dr. McCoy?"

Norman smiled.

"We've disagreed at scientific conferences for nearly fifteen years."

She laughed.

"I can't imagine that."

"Oh, you should."

"He usually wins."

Then...

"Mary Jane."

"Nova Silicon."

"I've heard good things."

MJ looked surprised.

"I'm just an intern."

Norman shook his head.

"No engineer is 'just' an intern."

"If they gave you meaningful work..."

"...it's because someone believed you were capable of it."

Peter noticed the slight smile that appeared on MJ's face.

Norman turned toward Flash.

"And you."

"Automotive engineering."

Flash nodded.

"Yes, sir."

"I've always believed transportation is one of civilization's greatest inventions."

Flash looked genuinely surprised.

"Really?"

Norman nodded.

"Every bridge."

"Every road."

"Every engine."

"Every vehicle."

"They all began with someone who wanted to make travel just a little easier."

He smiled.

"Never underestimate what you're building."

Flash stood a little taller.

Finally...

Norman looked toward Cindy.

"Empire State Research Institute."

"I hope they asked difficult questions."

Cindy smiled.

"They did."

"Good."

"The best science usually begins with one."

Norman looked around at the six students before gesturing toward the elevators.

"Shall we?"

The doors opened silently.

As they stepped inside, Peter looked back one last time at the atrium.

For the first time...

He understood why Harry never described Oscorp as a company.

It wasn't.

It was a place where thousands of people had gathered to imagine tomorrow.

The elevator rose in near silence.

Not because nobody had anything to say.

Because everyone was too busy looking.

The glass walls revealed more of the tower with every passing floor.

Laboratories.

Open collaboration spaces.

Conference rooms where engineers covered entire walls with equations.

Prototype workshops alive with robotic arms assembling components too small to see clearly from this distance.

"How many people work here?"

"Across all divisions?"

"...People?"

"People."

"Scientists."

"Engineers."

"Doctors."

"Researchers."

"Manufacturing specialists."

Only glass.

People could see one another.

Talk to one another.

Learn from one another.

Or hoodies.

Or jeans.

"I thought companies were..."

"I've found innovation rarely wears a tie."

"I've been telling him that for years."

Every machine.

Every prototype.

"You're trying to figure out how everything works."

"I've never seen her this quiet."

"Give it another minute."

Then smiled.

"We estimated twenty-six."

"You've run simulations already?"

Peter laughed softly.

"...There she goes."

"What language are they speaking?"

Peter smiled.

"...Computer."

Not interrupting.

Simply observing.

"You chose Stark."

"I did."

"I learned engineering isn't about building impressive things."

"What is it about?"

"It's about solving problems."

Norman smiled.

"The right answer."

"The public usually remembers inventions."

Everywhere he looked...

Someone was asking another question.

Someone was testing another idea.

Someone was trying again after another failure.

None of them looked discouraged.

They looked...

Curious.

Norman continued walking.

"This way."

"We've only just begun."

Brighter.

Completely.

Warmly.

Norman continued.

"We now know..."

A small section illuminated.

Then branched.

Not replacing the rest of the genome.

Growing from it.

Ancient microorganisms.

Fish.

Amphibians.

Mammals.

Early hominids.

Modern humans.

Marked only with a single word.

**Mutation.**

Scientists and mutants.

Working side by side.

Only whether the research was sound.

Norman replied.

"Science rarely rewards certainty."

## Chapter Three: The Unknown

The containment wing of Oscorp was quieter than the rest of the tower.

Its own pressure.

"He makes us all look bad."

Peter smiled politely.

But only for a moment.

Hank gestured toward the couch.

"Sit."

Peter slowly lowered himself onto it.

For the next several minutes...

No one interrupted.

They simply told him everything.

The invasion.

The animals.

The evacuation.

The battle.

X-Force.

Tony.

Harry.

Silk.

Flash.

The serum.

The separation.

Venom.

Agent Venom.

Peter listened.

Quietly.

Without saying a word.

When they finished...

The room fell silent.

Peter stared at the floor.

His hands slowly tightened.

Tony finally spoke.

"Kid..."

He leaned forward.

"You fought your way back."

"You gave us the chance to save you."

Peter never looked up.

Tony continued.

"You didn't choose what happened."

Peter slowly stood.

The room watched him.

He looked toward the doorway.

His voice was barely above a whisper.

"...But I let it happen."

Silence.

"I was supposed to stop it."

"I should've been stronger."

He finally looked back at everyone.

Pain filled his eyes.

"People got hurt..."

"...because I wasn't."

No one answered.

Peter quietly walked toward the door.

"Peter—"

Harry began.

Peter didn't stop.

He simply opened the door.

And walked out into the night.

The door quietly closed behind him.

Silence returned.

No one moved.

Charles Xavier looked toward the doorway for a long moment.

Then quietly spoke.

"Let him go."

Everyone turned toward him.

"He isn't running away from us."

Charles' expression remained calm.

"He's trying to understand what happened."

"He needs a little time."

Across the room...

Logan continued staring at the closed door.

After everything he'd seen that night...

He understood that look.

Better than most.

---

The rest of the night passed quietly.

Peter never returned to the mansion.

He simply walked.

Westchester was still awake.

Emergency crews cleared fallen trees.

Firefighters sprayed the last burning buildings.

Construction crews erected temporary barriers around damaged streets.

Television crews reported live from every intersection.

Peter kept his head down.

Nobody recognized him.

He wasn't Spider-Man.

He was just another young man walking through a city trying to recover.

He stopped beside a collapsed storefront.

Without saying a word...

He helped a construction worker lift a fallen steel beam.

A few blocks later...

An elderly woman struggled to move boxes back into her damaged shop.

Peter quietly carried them inside.

She smiled.

"Thank you."

Peter nodded politely.

Then kept walking.

No webs.

No mask.

No applause.

Just...

Helping.

Every television he passed told the same story.

*"Authorities confirm the situation has been contained..."*

*"Emergency responders continue rescue operations..."*

*"Officials credit the X-Men and several unidentified heroes..."*

Peter never stopped walking.

Each report only made the weight inside him heavier.

Eventually...

The city grew quiet.

---

The next morning.

Around nine o'clock.

A cool breeze drifted through the cemetery, weather wasn't exactly sunny.

Peter stood silently before a familiar gravestone.

**Benjamin Parker**

He looked at the worn inscription.

His fingers rested lightly against the cold stone.

His mind returned to another night.

Another promise.

Another moment when he believed he had failed someone he loved.

"I'll do better."

He had meant every word.

Now...

He wasn't sure he had.

The sound of footsteps approached through the grass.

Heavy.

Unhurried.

Peter didn't turn.

He assumed whoever it was would simply continue walking.

Instead...

The footsteps stopped beside him.

A familiar voice broke the silence.

"Hey, kid."

Peter finally looked up.

Logan stood with his hands in his jacket pockets.

The morning wind moved gently through his hair.

Peter managed a tired smile.

"...Didn't expect to see you here."

Logan looked toward Ben Parker's grave.

"Figured you might be."

Silence settled between them.

Neither man seemed interested in breaking it.

Not yet.

---

Peter remained silent.

The morning breeze gently moved through the cemetery.

For a while...

Neither he nor Logan spoke.

Finally...

Peter broke the silence.

"I remember..."

He looked toward Logan.

"You saved my friends from Rhino."

Logan glanced at him.

"I was still recovering."

"I never got the chance to thank you."

A faint smile crossed Logan's face.

"You don't need to."

"I was just passing through."

Peter couldn't help but smile.

Only a little.

Then his eyes returned to Uncle Ben's grave.

"I guess..."

He took a slow breath.

"...things haven't really changed."

Logan listened.

"The night I lost him..."

Peter gently rested his hand against the gravestone.

"I lost someone important because of my own mistake."

His voice became quieter.

"And now..."

"I let it happen again."

Silence.

Logan looked toward the name engraved in the stone.

Then finally spoke.

"You're missing something."

Peter looked at him.

"You changed how people see us."

"Powered people."

"Mutants."

Logan let out a quiet breath.

"You didn't make us heroes."

"We already were."

"But you reminded the world what we were fighting for."

He smiled faintly.

"And trust me..."

"I was never supposed to be one."

Peter stayed quiet.

Logan continued.

"Kid..."

"I've spent more years than you've been alive wishing I could change things I'd done."

His eyes drifted toward the horizon.

"You think you're the first person to wake up wondering if people got hurt because you weren't strong enough?"

He shook his head.

"You're not."

A long silence followed.

"Difference is..."

"Someone believed I could still do some good."

"Charles."

"My team."

"They never stopped believing in me."

Logan turned toward Peter.

"Looks to me..."

"...your people are trying to do the same."

---

Peter looked back at Uncle Ben's grave.

His eyes slowly filled with tears.

"I don't know how to live with this."

His voice cracked.

"And..."

He hesitated.

"Something much worse is coming."

Logan's expression changed.

"What do you mean?"

Peter looked into the distance.

"I saw it."

"While I was trapped."

"There were..."

He searched for the words.

"...more."

"So many more."

"They weren't like anything I've ever seen."

"They were connected."

"Following someone."

"I don't know who..."

"...but whatever I saw..."

"It wanted everything."

"The whole world."

Peter lowered his head.

"I don't think we're ready."

The sound of wheels rolling gently across the stone pathway interrupted the silence.

"Then..."

Charles Xavier's voice answered calmly.

"...we'll be ready together."

Peter and Logan turned.

Charles approached slowly.

Behind him...

Harry.

MJ.

Cindy.

Flash.

Gwen.

Finally...

May.

Peter looked at all of them.

Charles stopped beside Logan.

"As long as we stand together..."

"We won't let that darkness take this world."

He smiled gently.

"And this time..."

"We know more about our enemy."

Peter's eyes shifted toward Flash.

Standing quietly among the others.

The black organism remained hidden.

But Peter knew it was there.

Charles followed his gaze.

"And..."

"We have an ally who understands it better than anyone."

Flash gave Peter a reassuring smile.

Peter remained quiet for a long moment.

Then finally asked...

"...What if we're still not enough?"

Nobody answered immediately.

Instead...

Charles simply smiled.

"Then we ask for help."

---

As Charles finished speaking...

The world seemed to pause.

Then—

A series of images.

Across the country...

People were already preparing.

---

Malibu.

Tony Stark stood inside one of the lower laboratories beneath his mansion.

The recovered symbiote research floated as holograms around him.

He adjusted wave frequencies.

Ran simulation after simulation.

Testing stronger sonic emitters.

Designing better countermeasures.

"If there's a next time..."

"...we'll be ready."

---

The Baxter Building.

Reed Richards projected a three-dimensional model of the symbiote into the center of the room.

Sue watched silently.

Johnny leaned against the wall.

Ben folded his arms.

"It adapted faster than any organism I've ever studied."

Reed looked at his family.

"We're dealing with something unlike anything on Earth."

Johnny's usual smile disappeared.

"So..."

"Immediately."

---

The Helicarrier.

Director Fury silently watched the classified footage.

Spider-Man's transformation.

The invasion.

The separation.

Agent Venom.

He picked up the secure phone.

"Increase extraterrestrial surveillance."

"We're not waiting for the next one."

Nearby...

Steve Rogers continued his morning training.

Every strike.

Every movement.

More focused than before.

He had already read the report.

---

The University of Virginia.

Bruce Banner quietly finished reading Charles Xavier's encrypted message.

His expression grew serious.

He looked toward one of the research monitors.

"An extraterrestrial organism..."

He whispered.

---

Back at Xavier's School...

Hank and Jean continued studying every piece of recovered data.

The molecular structure rotated above the laboratory table.

Jean looked toward Hank.

"Do you think we'll see another one?"

Hank didn't answer immediately.

Finally...

"I hope not."

He saved the latest report.

"...isn't a strategy."

The research continued.

---

The montage ended.

Back at the cemetery...

Charles looked toward Peter.

"Believe me."

"Everyone is preparing."

"We won't face the next wave alone."

Peter looked at each of them.

Charles.

May.

His friends.

For the first time since waking...

The weight on his shoulders felt...

Just a little lighter.

He stepped forward.

Stopping in front of Charles.

Without saying a word...

Peter extended his hand.

Charles smiled warmly.

Then gently shook it.

"Welcome back, Peter."

A small smile appeared on Peter's face.

Behind him...

Harry stepped forward first.

Wrapping Peter into a quick hug.

"Told you we'd bring you home."

MJ smiled and hugged him next.

"We're just glad you're back."

Flash grinned.

"You still owe me for saving your life."

Peter laughed quietly.

"I'll think of something."

Gwen smiled.

"He's joking."

Flash looked offended.

"I think."

Everyone laughed.

Cindy stepped forward last.

She didn't say anything.

She simply hugged him.

Tightly.

Peter smiled.

"...Thank you."

Finally...

He turned toward May.

She had been watching quietly the entire time.

Almost as though she had always known this was where Peter would end up.

Peter walked over.

Wrapped his arms around her.

May held him close.

A gentle smile crossed her face.

"I had a feeling..."

"...I'd find you here."

Peter closed his eyes.

Smiling through the last of his tears.

"Yeah..."

"So did I."

## Chapter Nine: Home

### The Thompson residence.

Late afternoon.

The front door quietly opened.

Flash stepped inside.

The house was unusually quiet.

The television was still on.

Every news channel carried the same story.

**"Recovery efforts continue following the Westchester Incident..."**

"...authorities have confirmed that several young heroes assisted during the crisis..."

Flash quietly slipped off his shoes.

He hoped he could make it upstairs unnoticed.

"...Flash."

He stopped.

Slowly turning around.

His father sat in the living room.

Still wearing part of his police uniform.

His eyes never left the television.

For a long moment...

Neither of them spoke.

Flash finally broke the silence.

"...Dad."

His father picked up the remote.

Muted the television.

The room became silent.

"I saw what you did."

Flash looked down.

His father continued.

"You stepped into danger."

"You knew you could've died."

"And you still went."

Another pause.

"But..."

"This time..."

"It wasn't for yourself."

"It was for someone else."

Flash remained silent.

His father slowly stood.

For years...

Every conversation between them had ended with anger.

This one didn't.

"I'm proud of you."

Flash looked up.

Genuinely surprised.

His father nodded once.

"I don't think I've said that enough."

Flash searched for something to say.

Nothing came.

He simply nodded.

"...Thanks."

He turned toward the stairs.

"...Flash."

He stopped once more.

His father looked at him.

"I spent a long time trying to decide what your life should be."

"I thought if I pushed hard enough..."

"...you'd become the man I wanted."

A faint smile appeared.

"I was wrong."

Another pause.

"You already figured it out."

Flash's eyes softened.

His father took a slow breath.

"I'm done standing in your way."

"If this is the path you've chosen..."

"...I'm not going to stop you anymore."

Flash smiled.

Not the confident grin everyone else knew.

A quiet one.

One he'd waited years to have.

"...Thanks, Dad."

His father gave a small nod.

"Go get some rest."

Flash turned.

Walking upstairs.

For the first time in years...

Neither of them left the conversation angry.

The relationship wasn't fixed.

Not yet.

But it had finally begun to heal.

"I know."

Her mother stood quietly beside the counter.

Watching.

For a long moment...

Only silence.

"...Mom."

Searching.

Making sure she was truly standing there.

Cindy took a slow breath.

"I know..."

"...you won't agree with what I've decided."

"But I have."

"I know what I want to do with my life."

She looked back up.

"And..."

"I don't regret it."

Silence.

Her mother didn't answer.

Cindy gave a small nod.

"I understand."

She quietly picked up her bag.

Then walked toward the staircase.

Halfway up...

A voice stopped her.

"...Cindy."

Her mother was still standing exactly where she had been.

Her expression hadn't changed.

"I still don't like it."

Another pause.

"I probably never will."

"I know."

Her mother continued.

"But..."

"I saw the news."

"I saw what happened."

Her voice became quieter.

"You saved people."

A long silence followed.

"I'm still afraid."

"I'm always going to be afraid."

A tear slowly rolled down her cheek.

"...But I'm proud of the woman you've become."

Cindy's eyes immediately filled with tears.

Her mother smiled softly.

Cindy smiled through the tears.

"I promise."

This time...

Cindy was the one who walked forward.

The two embraced.

Not because every disagreement had disappeared.

But because love had proven stronger than fear.

### The Stacy residence.

Early evening.

Captain George Stacy stood there.

For a brief moment...

Neither of them spoke.

Then...

A warm smile spread across his face.

"Welcome home."

Not because she needed comforting.

Because he was simply glad she was home.

A second later...

Her mother hurried over.

"Oh, Gwen."

"I was so worried."

Gwen laughed softly.

"I'm okay."

For the first time in days...

She wasn't thinking about battles.

Or symbiotes.

She was simply...

Home.

### Parkers Residence (few days later)

The backyard hadn't changed much.

The old oak tree still stood proudly in the corner, its branches stretching across the yard as though protecting the house beneath it.

Time had weathered it.

So had life.

Only a few worn wooden planks remained where a treehouse once stood.

Nature had slowly reclaimed the rest.

Peter sat beneath the porch roof, elbows resting on his knees.

The afternoon sky hung low over Queens.

Gray clouds drifted lazily overhead.

The wind carried the scent of rain.

Inside the house...

Silence.

May had taken an extra shift at the hospital.

The house felt strangely empty.

Peaceful.

Peter stared quietly toward the tree.

A faint smile found its way onto his face, staring at the tree, remembering when he made the tree house.

The porch door creaked open behind him.

Peter didn't turn.

He already knew who it was.

Mary Jane stepped outside carrying two mugs of coffee.

She placed one beside him before quietly sitting down.

Neither spoke.

They had learned long since that silence wasn't awkward.

Sometimes...

It said everything.

She gently slipped her hand beneath his.

Her fingers intertwined with his.

Then she rested her head against his shoulder.

Peter closed his eyes.

"...I miss him."

His voice cracked.

"More than I ever have."

Mary Jane squeezed his hand.

"I know."

Another quiet moment passed.

Peter looked toward the old oak tree again.

"...Remember the treehouse?"

Mary Jane smiled immediately.

"The one that leaned to one side?"

Peter laughed softly.

"It wasn't that bad."

"It absolutely was."

"You made me believe it was architecturally advanced."

"It was architecturally dangerous."

Peter couldn't help laughing.

"I bent every nail."

"You bent every single one."

"I blamed the hammer."

"You did."

"And Ben blamed the carpenter."

They both laughed.

The sound echoed gently across the backyard.

Peter looked down at his hands.

"I guess..."

"...We've come a long way since then."

Mary Jane glanced toward him.

"I'd say building web-shooters is a slight improvement."

Peter smiled.

"...Slight."

His eyes drifted back toward the tree.

"Then it started raining."

Mary Jane nodded.

"You looked so disappointed."

"I thought we were going to lose the whole afternoon."

"And then I ran outside."

Peter laughed.

"You didn't even hesitate."

"You chased after me."

"I wasn't going to let you have all the fun."

Silence settled once more.

Neither needed to finish the memory.

They both remembered.

Every second of it.

Peter took a slow breath.

"It feels..."

He searched for the words.

"...surreal."

"...Like that afternoon never really ended."

Almost on cue—

A distant roll of thunder echoed across Queens.

Both of them looked toward the sky.

The first raindrop landed softly on the porch railing.

Another.

Then another.

Mary Jane smiled.

"...Looks familiar."

Peter smiled back.

"It does."

Without another word—

She stood.

Walked beyond the shelter of the porch.

Held out one hand.

Tiny drops gathered across her fingertips.

Then more.

The rain slowly became steady.

Mary Jane closed her eyes.

Lifted her face toward the sky.

Spread her arms.

Exactly...

As she had all those years ago.

Peter remained beneath the roof for a moment.

Watching her.

The years quietly faded away.

He didn't see the woman standing before him.

He saw the little girl who had abandoned an unfinished treehouse because playing in the rain had seemed far more important.

He smiled.

Somehow...

She still made the world feel that simple.

Peter stepped into the rain.

Cold water soaked his shirt within seconds.

Neither of them cared.

Mary Jane opened her eyes.

She looked at him.

Really looked.

The weight he'd carried for so long...

The guilt.

The anger.

The fear of never being enough.

For the first time since ages...

Peter was smiling.

Not the smile he wore to reassure other people.

Not the smile he forced behind the mask.

A real one.

Mary Jane smiled too.

Without speaking...

She extended her hand.

Peter looked at it.

Then gently took it.

Instead of simply holding it...

He gave the smallest pull.

She stepped closer.

Closer still.

Until only a few inches remained between them.

Rain fell steadily around them.

The world beyond the backyard seemed to disappear.

No sirens.

No villains.

No Spider-Man.

No expectations.

Just...

Peter.

And Mary Jane.

They looked into each other's eyes.

Neither knew who moved first.

It didn't matter.

Their lips met gently.

Neither rushed the moment.

There was no urgency.

No desperation.

Only years of unspoken affection finally finding its way into the open.

When they finally pulled apart...

Neither moved very far.

Peter rested his forehead against hers.

His eyes shimmered with tears.

Not tears of grief.

Of gratitude.

He smiled softly.

"...Thank you."

Mary Jane looked at him.

"For what?"

Peter's voice was barely a whisper.

"For coming into my life."

For a heartbeat...

She couldn't answer.

Every memory flashed through her mind.

A handshake.

A crooked treehouse.

School mornings.

Homework.

Rain.

Laughter.

Heartbreak.

Loss.

Growing up together.

She wrapped both arms around him.

Holding him tighter than she ever had before.

As though afraid that if she let go...

The moment might disappear forever.

Peter held her just as tightly.

Rain continued to fall around them.

Gentle.

Steady.

Peaceful.

From the kitchen window...

The old oak tree swayed quietly in the wind.

Its branches stretched over the backyard...

Just as they had on that summer afternoon long ago.

The rain washed over everything.

Not to erase the past.

But to remind them that after every storm...

Life still found a way to begin again.

Above them...

The clouds slowly drifted apart.

And somewhere, in the quiet that followed...

It almost felt as though Ben Parker was smiling too.
