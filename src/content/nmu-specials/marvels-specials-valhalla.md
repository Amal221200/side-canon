### **The Threshold**

There was no pain.

No cold.

No weight.

Loki stood.

Alone.

Before a gate he did not remember walking toward.

It was vast.

Not imposing—

But absolute.

He looked at his hands.

No frost.

No blood.

No illusion to hide behind.

Just himself.

A figure stood beside the gate.

**Aldrymir**

Old.

Unmoving.

Eyes glow like distant stars.

Watching.

“You have come far,” the guardian said.

Loki frowned slightly.

“I did not choose to come here.”

“No one does.”

A pause.

“But all are brought… where they must be.”

The gates opened.

### **The Hall**

It was not silence inside.

It was life.

Warriors stood.

Sat.

Trained.

Spoke.

Not as ghosts—

But as something continued.

The greatest of Asgard.

Those who had fallen—

But not ended.

Loki walked through them.

Unseen.

Unstopped.

Until—

He reached the center.

### **The One Who Remains**

A throne stood—not elevated, not distant.

Present.

Seated upon it—

**Borr**

Ancient.

Not in age—

In presence.

His gaze met Loki’s.

And did not waver.

“Welcome,” he said.

Loki didn’t bow.

Didn’t speak immediately.

“I do not understand,” he said finally.

“Why am I here?”

A pause.

“I betrayed them.”

“My family.”

“My realm.”

No anger in his voice.

No defense.

Just fact.

### **The Judgment**

Borr studied him.

Not weighing.

Not deciding.

Recognizing.

“And yet…”

He said quietly.

“You returned.”

Loki’s eyes flickered.

“You chose them.”

A step forward.

“When it mattered.”

Another pause.

“And you died… for that choice.”

Loki looked away.

“That does not erase what I did.”

“No.”

Borr agreed.

“It does not.”

Silence.

Then—

“It completes it.”

Loki looked back.

For the first time—

Uncertain.

### **The Truth**

“You are not here because you were perfect.”

Bor continued.

“None who stand here were.”

“You are here…”

“Because in the end—”

“You chose to protect.”

A breath.

“And that… is what we remember.”

Loki didn’t respond.

Not immediately.

Because for the first time—

He wasn’t being judged.

He was being… understood.

### **Acceptance**

Borr gestured lightly.

Around them—

Warriors continued.

Training.

Laughing.

Existing.

“This realm,” Bor said,

“is not for those who lived without fault.”

“It is for those who faced themselves…”

“And chose… something greater.”

A pause.

“Rest, if you wish.”

“Train, if you must.”

“Or simply… be.”

### **End Beat**

Loki stood still.

No throne.

No title.

No burden.

For the first time—

Nothing to prove.

He looked around.

Then—

Took a step forward.

And the hall did not reject him.
