# Marvel Specials – Captain America

## Chapter 1 – Stubborn Kid From Brooklyn
### Brooklyn, New York – 1943

The morning streets of Brooklyn bustled with life.

Vendors shouted from their stalls, children raced between sidewalks, and the sounds of jazz drifted through the air from an open café. It was a city preparing for war, yet desperately trying to live as though tomorrow was guaranteed.

Near an alleyway, the laughter stopped.

Three older boys had cornered a young newspaper vendor, no older than fourteen.

"Come on," one sneered as he snatched the boy's earnings. "You don't need all this."

The frightened boy reached for the money, only to be shoved back against the brick wall.

"Please... that's all I've got."

A quiet voice interrupted.

"Give it back."

The boys turned.

Standing behind them was a thin young man in worn-out clothes. His jacket looked two sizes too large for his frail body, and every visible bone beneath his skin hinted at years of illness and malnutrition.

One of the bullies laughed.

"Seriously? You?"

Steve Rogers stepped closer.

"I said... give it back."

The tallest bully walked toward him until they stood face to face.

"And what are you gonna do about it?"

Steve looked him directly in the eyes.

"What I can."

The punch came fast.

Steve hit the pavement before he even realized he'd been struck.

The boys burst into laughter.

"Stay down."

Steve coughed, wiping blood from the corner of his mouth.

Slowly...

He pushed himself back onto his feet.

The bully sighed.

"You really don't know when to quit."

"No."

Steve answered without hesitation.

"I just don't like bullies."

Another punch.

Another fall.

Again...

Steve stood.

Again...

He fell.

Again...

He rose.

The boys' amusement slowly turned into irritation.

"What is wrong with you?"

Steve steadied himself, barely able to keep his balance.

"If nobody stands up to people like you..."

He caught his breath.

"...then you keep thinking you're stronger than everyone else."

Before another punch could land—

"HEY!"

A voice echoed down the street.

The bullies turned just as James Buchanan Barnes sprinted toward them.

Unlike Steve, Bucky carried confidence with every stride.

"You got a problem?"

The boys exchanged uneasy glances.

"There're three of us."

Bucky smirked.

"Then it's a fair fight."

Not wanting any trouble, the boys tossed the stolen money onto the ground before walking away with a few muttered insults.

Steve picked up the scattered coins and handed them back to the young vendor.

"You alright?"

The boy nodded repeatedly.

"Thank you."

Steve smiled.

"No problem."

The boy hurried away.

Bucky looked at Steve's bruised face and sighed.

"One of these days, you're gonna learn not to start fights you can't win."

Steve dusted off his jacket.

"I didn't start it."

"You literally walked up to three guys."

"They were picking on someone who couldn't fight back."

"And?"

Steve looked at him as though the answer was obvious.

"So somebody had to."

Bucky shook his head with a smile.

"You know... most people would've kept walking."

"Most people shouldn't."

The two friends continued down the street.

### United States Army Recruitment Office

Steve sat across from an army physician.

The doctor barely glanced up from the paperwork.

"Height."

"Five foot four."

"Weight."

"Ninety pounds."

The doctor sighed before even finishing the examination.

"Mr. Rogers..."

He stamped the document.

**4-F**

Rejected.

Steve looked at the paper in silence.

"There's always next time," the doctor offered politely.

Steve stood.

"There will be."

A montage followed.

Steve entering another recruitment office.

Rejected.

Another city.

Rejected.

Another medical examination.

Rejected.

Each application listed a slightly different place of birth...

A slightly different address...

A different middle initial...

But always the same determined face.

Rejected.

Rejected.

Rejected.

No matter how many times the government told him he wasn't fit to serve...

Steve refused to quit trying.

### Stark Expo – New York

The World's Fair was alive with innovation.

Towering exhibits showcased America's greatest achievements.

Families admired experimental automobiles.

Children pointed excitedly at futuristic machines.

Military demonstrations drew enormous crowds.

Steve wandered through the exhibition with Bucky, his eyes filled with admiration rather than excitement.

"I wish I could do something that mattered."

Bucky nudged him.

"You matter."

Steve smiled faintly.

"I appreciate the lie."

"It's not a lie."

Steve smiled and continued, "So, you will be deployed tomorrow?"

Bucky, "Yes, and that's why I'm am worried if you keep doing stupid things like this, no one will be there to save you."

Steve, "How can I, you are taking all the stupid with you, just don't die before I join."

Bucky laughed, before he could continue, Steve noticed a military recruitment booth.

His smile disappeared.

"I'm gonna check something."

Bucky sighed.

"Steve..."

"I'll be right back."

Bucky, "I will be waiting at Stark presentation."

Steve nodded, then stepped toward the enlistment station.

A camera photographed each applicant before displaying their image on a verification screen.

Steve frowned.

His reflection wasn't aligning with the marked silhouette.

The attendant adjusted the machine.

Still nothing.

"It must be malfunctioning," the attendant muttered.

"It isn't."

A calm German voice spoke from behind.

Dr. Abraham Erskine stepped forward, studying Steve with quiet curiosity.

"I've seen that face many times."

Steve turned.

"I'm sorry?"

Erskine smiled.

"Not in person."

He opened a folder.

"I've seen dozens of enlistment applications."

Steve's expression shifted.

"Different names..."

Erskine continued.

"...different addresses..."

He flipped another page.

"...different birthplaces."

Then another.

"But always the same man."

Steve lowered his eyes.

"I know."

"You understand impersonating official records is a federal offense?"

"I do."

"And yet..."

Steve nodded.

"I kept trying."

Erskine studied him for a long moment.

"Why?"

Steve answered without hesitation.

"Because people are dying."

"You wish to kill Nazis?"

Steve shook his head.

"No."

"Revenge?"

"No."

"Glory?"

Steve almost laughed.

"I don't think anyone looking like me dreams about glory."

Erskine folded his arms.

"Then why become a soldier?"

Steve looked across the Expo toward a massive American flag waving above the crowd.

"I don't like bullies."

He turned back.

"I don't care where they're from."

"They use power because they think nobody can stop them."

His voice remained calm.

"And I'm not going to sit back while innocent people are being killed."

Erskine glanced at Steve's thin frame.

"And what exactly could you offer the army... in your current condition?"

Steve looked down at his own trembling hands.

"I honestly don't know."

A small smile crossed his face.

"But if I can stand between a bully and someone who can't defend themselves..."

He looked Erskine in the eyes.

"...then at least I'll die fighting for something I believe in."

Silence.

For several seconds, Erskine simply stared at him.

Finally...

A warm smile spread across the doctor's face.

"Mr. Rogers..."

He reached into his coat and handed Steve a card.

"Report to this address tomorrow morning."

Steve accepted it cautiously.

"I'll make sure your enlistment is taken care of."

Steve looked at the card, then back at Erskine.

"You mean..."

Erskine smiled knowingly.

"I believe you've waited long enough."

For the first time in years...

Steve Rogers allowed himself to believe that tomorrow might finally be the day he could serve his country.

## Chapter Two: A Good Man
### Strategic Scientific Reserve Training Facility
**The Following Morning**

The gates swung open as military trucks rolled into the compound.

Young men stepped out carrying duffel bags over their shoulders, each one convinced they had what it took to fight for their country.

Steve Rogers climbed down last.

His bag nearly slipped from his shoulder as another recruit bumped into him.

"Watch it, twig."

Steve simply steadied himself and kept walking.

Rows of recruits assembled in formation beneath the morning sun.

A tall officer marched before them with his hands clasped behind his back.

Colonel Chester Phillips.

His eyes swept across the line until they landed on Steve.

He frowned.

Without saying a word, he turned toward Dr. Abraham Erskine.

Quietly enough that only Erskine could hear, he muttered,

"This is your candidate?"

Erskine answered calmly.

"Yes."

Phillips looked Steve up and down.

"I've seen better-looking corpses."

Erskine smiled.

"I wasn't looking for muscles."

"No..."

Phillips replied.

"I noticed."

He stepped forward.

"Ladies!"

His booming voice silenced the yard.

"You are here because your country needs soldiers."

His eyes scanned every face.

"Some of you think muscles win wars."

He paused.

"They don't."

He walked slowly between the ranks.

"Discipline."

Another step.

"Determination."

Another.

"Courage."

He stopped beside Steve.

"...and common sense."

His eyes lingered for a moment.

"We'll find out who has them."

### Training Begins

The whistle shrieked.

The recruits exploded into motion.

Walls.

Barbed wire.

Mud pits.

Rope climbs.

Every obstacle demanded strength and endurance.

Steve was the last over every wall.

The last through every trench.

The last to reach the finish line.

His breathing became ragged.

His legs trembled.

The other recruits laughed whenever they passed him.

"Move it, Rogers!"

"You call that running?"

Steve ignored every insult.

Each time he stumbled...

He stood.

Each time he slipped...

He climbed again.

By sunset, he collapsed onto the dirt, completely exhausted.

Phillips watched from a distance.

"He'll never make it."

Erskine folded his arms.

"He already is."

### Days Later

Nothing changed.

Steve remained the weakest recruit.

Couldn't outrun anyone.

Couldn't outlift anyone.

Couldn't outfight anyone.

Yet every morning...

He was the first one standing in formation.

Every night...

He refused medical leave.

Every bruise became another reason to keep going.

### The Flag

The recruits gathered near a towering flagpole.

An American flag waved high above them.

Colonel Phillips pointed upward.

"Whoever gets that flag down..."

He held up a set of truck keys.

"...gets a ride back to camp."

Before he finished speaking—

Dozens of recruits rushed the pole.

They shoved.

Pulled.

Climbed over each other's shoulders.

One nearly reached the top before sliding back down.

Another tried boosting two others.

Nothing worked.

Steve remained where he was.

Watching.

Thinking.

His eyes drifted toward the base of the pole.

A small retaining pin held it upright.

He walked over.

Kneeled.

Removed the pin.

The entire pole slowly tilted toward the ground.

The recruits froze in disbelief.

Steve calmly untied the flag.

Folded it carefully.

Walked over to Colonel Phillips.

"Sir."

He handed him the folded flag.

Silence.

Phillips stared at him.

"...Smart."

Steve smiled politely.

"Thank you, sir."

As Steve walked away, Phillips muttered under his breath,

"I hate it when they're smarter than me."

Erskine chuckled quietly.

"I thought you wanted common sense."

### The Grenade

Several days later.

The recruits stood in a semicircle around Colonel Phillips.

He held a training grenade.

"Battlefield doesn't care how strong you are."

He pulled the pin.

Without warning—

He threw it directly into the middle of the group.

"GRENADE!"

Panic erupted.

The recruits scattered in every direction.

Some dove behind crates.

Others sprinted for cover.

Steve didn't move away.

He ran toward it.

Without hesitation, he threw himself over the grenade, wrapping his arms around it.

"GET BACK!"

He shouted at the others.

"EVERYBODY GET BACK!"

Silence.

Nothing happened.

Steve slowly opened one eye.

The grenade was fake.

The yard fell silent.

Colonel Phillips walked toward him.

"You absolute fool."

Steve looked confused.

"I thought it was real."

"I know."

Phillips reached down and helped him to his feet.

For the first time...

There was no mockery in his voice.

Only respect.

### That Evening

The camp had gone quiet.

Most recruits slept after another exhausting day.

Steve sat alone outside his barracks, staring at the stars.

Dr. Erskine approached carrying two steaming cups of coffee.

"Mind if I join you?"

Steve smiled.

"I'd like that."

They sat together in silence.

Finally, Steve spoke.

"I still don't understand."

Erskine looked toward him.

"Understand what?"

"Why me?"

Steve laughed softly.

"I can't run."

"I can't fight."

"I'm probably the worst soldier in camp."

Erskine took a thoughtful sip of coffee.

"When I was younger..."

he began,

"...I knew another man who desperately wanted power."

His voice became quieter.

"He believed strength alone made someone superior."

Steve listened carefully.

"He was wrong."

Erskine turned toward Steve.

"The serum amplifies everything that already exists."

He paused.

"It makes the strong stronger."

He looked directly into Steve's eyes.

"But it also makes the cruel more cruel."

Steve remained silent.

"Power has never created character."

Erskine continued.

"It reveals it."

He smiled warmly.

"That is why I searched for a good man..."

"...not a perfect soldier."

Steve lowered his eyes.

"I don't know if I'm a good man."

Erskine smiled.

"You jumped on a grenade."

"It turned out to be fake."

"You didn't know that."

Steve thought for a moment.

"I just didn't want anyone else to die."

Erskine nodded.

"I know."

He stood.

"Tomorrow morning."

Steve looked up.

"Be ready."

"For what?"

Erskine smiled.

"The future."

## Chapter Three: The First Super Soldier

### Strategic Scientific Reserve Laboratory

The laboratory buzzed with controlled urgency.

Scientists hurried between consoles, calibrating massive electrical coils that surrounded a reinforced steel chamber at the center of the room. Officers came to vitness this miracle, Colonel Phillips, along with them Agent Margeret Carter (Peggy).

Among them stood a distinguished engineer in his early fifties, his silver-streaked hair neatly combed beneath a pair of protective goggles.

**Edmund Stark.**

Founder of Stark Industries and the Strategic Scientific Reserve's leading engineering consultant.

His inventions had modernized military aircraft, communications systems, and experimental energy equipment for the Allied forces. While others designed weapons, Edmund believed technology existed to protect lives.

He adjusted one of the Vita-Ray generators before turning toward Dr. Abraham Erskine.

"The chamber is stable."

Erskine smiled.

"I wouldn't trust anyone else's work."

Edmund gave a modest nod.

"I only built the machine, Doctor."

He looked toward Steve Rogers.

"You built the man."

Steve stood beside the chamber, dressed in a simple military uniform.

Erskine approached him.

"Nervous?"

"A little."

"Good."

Erskine handed him a small vial.

"This serum will not make you a hero."

Steve studied the liquid.

"It will simply allow the hero already inside you to do what he could never do before."

Steve nodded.

"I understand."

He stepped inside.

The steel doors sealed shut.

Outside, Edmund Stark gave the final approval.

"Power levels stable."

Scientists prepared the injection sequence.

"Beginning serum administration."

One by one, syringes delivered the experimental compound into Steve's body.

His muscles immediately tightened.

His breathing became strained.

Edmund watched every instrument carefully.

"Vital signs fluctuating..."

Erskine remained calm.

"Prepare Vita Rays."

The generators roared to life.

Columns of brilliant white energy flooded the chamber.

Steve cried out in agony as the transformation began.

Electrical arcs danced across the steel walls.

The laboratory trembled beneath the immense surge of power.

Peggy instinctively stepped forward.

"Doctor—"

"Wait."

Erskine never took his eyes off Steve.

Minutes later...

The energy slowly subsided.

Steam hissed from the chamber.

The heavy doors opened.

Steve Rogers stepped forward.

His frail body was gone.

Broad shoulders.

Powerful physique.

Perfect physical conditioning.

Yet the kindness in his eyes remained unchanged.

A smile spread across Erskine's face.

"It worked."

Edmund Stark quietly looked over the readings.

"Extraordinary..."

Before anyone could celebrate—

A gunshot echoed through the laboratory.

Erskine staggered backward.

Peggy gasped.

"Doctor!"

A Hydra operative lowered his pistol, grabbed the remaining vial of serum from the workstation, and sprinted toward the exit.

"Stop him!" Phillips shouted.

Steve looked toward Erskine.

The dying scientist gave a faint nod.

"Go..."

Steve ran.

### The Chase

Steve burst through the laboratory doors.

The world suddenly felt different.

Lighter.

Sharper.

He pushed off the ground—

—and covered nearly twenty feet in a single stride.

His eyes widened.

He wasn't simply running.

He was flying across the pavement.

The assassin fired several shots behind him.

Steve instinctively vaulted over a military truck, landing effortlessly on the opposite side.

Even he couldn't believe what his body was capable of.

The chase led toward the harbor.

The Hydra operative reached a waiting mini-submarine.

Just as he grabbed the hatch—

Steve launched himself from the pier.

The two crashed onto the dock.

Steve pinned him with overwhelming strength.

"Tell me where Hydra is."

The operative smiled.

"You cannot stop what has already begun."

He bit down on a concealed cyanide capsule.

Within seconds...

He was dead.

The vial slipped from his hand.

It shattered against the dock.

The last remaining serum disappeared into the ocean.

### The Last Formula

The laboratory had fallen silent.

Scientists searched frantically through Erskine's research.

Cabinets.

Journals.

Blueprints.

Nothing.

Edmund Stark carefully examined every notebook.

Hours passed before he slowly removed his glasses.

"There is no completed formula."

Colonel Phillips frowned.

"What do you mean?"

"He never wrote the final process down."

Edmund closed the last notebook.

"Dr. Erskine believed the serum was too dangerous to exist without someone capable of understanding its consequences."

"So he carried the complete formula in his mind."

Peggy lowered her eyes.

"And now it's gone."

Edmund looked toward Steve.

"No."

He spoke quietly.

"It isn't."

Everyone turned.

"The doctor's greatest achievement isn't written in these pages."

He looked directly at Steve.

"It is standing right there."

Steve's gaze drifted toward Erskine's covered body.

"I wish he could've seen it."

Edmund rested a reassuring hand on his shoulder.

"He did."

"He chose the right man."

### Three Months of Preparation

The following morning.

Steve stood once again on the training grounds.

Only this time...

Everything felt different.

The world no longer challenged his body.

It challenged his discipline.

Colonel Phillips folded his arms.

"Congratulations."

"You're stronger than every man in this camp."

He stepped closer.

"That doesn't make you a soldier."

"We're starting from the beginning."

The weeks that followed became a relentless routine.

The serum had transformed Steve's body overnight.

Now, the military had to transform his mind.

Before sunrise...

Steve underwent a specialized physical training regimen designed by the Strategic Scientific Reserve.

Obstacle courses were rebuilt to challenge his enhanced speed.

Steel climbing structures replaced wooden ones.

Weighted sprints became heavier each week.

His reflexes were tested with moving targets.

Balance exercises evolved into advanced gymnastic drills, teaching him how to control the extraordinary agility his new body possessed.

Every movement had a purpose.

Power without control was dangerous.

By afternoon...

Experienced military instructors introduced Steve to a variety of combat disciplines.

Classical boxing taught him distance, timing, and footwork.

Military wrestling emphasized leverage and control over brute force.

Close-quarters combat focused on ending confrontations quickly and efficiently.

Knife fighting taught precision under pressure.

Silent takedowns and infiltration techniques prepared him for operations behind enemy lines.

Rather than attempting to overpower Steve, each instructor corrected his technique.

Again.

Again.

Again.

Every mistake became another lesson.

Steve quickly realized that strength alone only made poor technique more dangerous.

Colonel Phillips personally oversaw Steve's military education.

Every evening became another classroom.

Battlefield maps covered the tables.

Hydra supply routes.

Enemy fortifications.

Ambush tactics.

Reconnaissance.

Squad command.

Decision-making under pressure.

"You don't win wars by hitting harder," Phillips reminded him.

"You win because you make better decisions before the first shot is fired."

Steve listened carefully.

He wasn't just learning to fight.

He was learning how to lead.

Peggy Carter focused on another aspect of Steve's education.

Weapons.

Marksmanship.

Field intelligence.

Enemy identification.

Radio communications.

Code recognition.

Mission planning.

She taught Steve how to gather information before engaging an enemy, reminding him that understanding the battlefield was often more valuable than firing the first shot.

"The best fight..."

Peggy said while handing Steve a pair of binoculars,

"...is the one you already understand before it begins."

Steve nodded.

"And the worst?"

She smiled.

"The one you rush into."

As the weeks passed, Steve's movements became effortless.

His punches were no longer wild.

His footing became precise.

His balance almost flawless.

Every leap landed exactly where he intended.

Every movement flowed into the next.

The serum had given him extraordinary potential.

Three months of relentless training had finally taught him how to use it.

By the end of his training...

Steve Rogers was no longer simply the world's first Super Soldier.

He had become the complete soldier Dr. Erskine believed he could be.

Several weeks later...

Edmund Stark led Steve into the Strategic Scientific Reserve's armory.

Rows of experimental weapons lined the walls.

Prototype rifles.

Modified pistols.

Combat knives.

Protective armor.

Steve examined each one carefully.

Edmund picked up a rifle.

"Accurate."

He reached for another.

"Lighter."

Then a combat knife.

"Reliable."

Steve listened attentively.

His eyes drifted toward the far corner of the workshop.

Partially covered by a heavy canvas rested a circular object unlike anything else in the room.

Curious, Steve stepped closer.

"What about that one?"

Edmund followed his gaze.

For a brief moment...

His expression changed.

He walked over and pulled the canvas back only enough to reveal part of the shield.

Its metallic surface shimmered unlike ordinary steel.

"This..."

He said quietly.

"...is a prototype."

Steve reached toward it but stopped himself.

"What makes it different?"

Edmund gently rested a hand on the shield.

"It isn't steel."

"It's made from the rarest and strongest metal known to man."

"Vibranium."

"There isn't enough of it on Earth to build another."

Steve studied it in silence.

"It doesn't belong in a museum."

Edmund smiled faintly.

"No."

"It belongs with someone who understands what it represents."

He covered it once more.

"And I am afraid you're not ready yet."

There was no disappointment in Steve's face.

Only understanding.

"I understand."

Edmund nodded approvingly.

Instead, he walked to another workbench and lifted a sturdy triangular shield.

"Take this."

"It's lighter, dependable, and built to withstand battlefield conditions."

Steve accepted it without hesitation.

"Appreciate this."

Edmund smiled.

"You'll know when you've earned the other one."

Weeks turned into months.

Steve mastered firearms.

Close-quarters combat.

Knife defense.

Shield techniques.

Urban warfare.

Field medicine.

Squad leadership.

By the end of the third month...

He was no longer simply a man transformed by science.

He had become the soldier Erskine believed he could be.

One rainy afternoon—

The doors to headquarters burst open.

A communications officer hurried inside carrying encrypted reports.

"Colonel Phillips."

Phillips took the documents.

His expression darkened.

"What happened?"

"The 107th Infantry."

"They reached the suspected Hydra installation three days ago."

"We've lost all communication."

"No survivors have returned."

Steve instinctively looked toward the report.

His heart stopped.

Among the list of missing soldiers...

**Sergeant James Buchanan Barnes.**

Steve immediately stepped forward.

"I'm going."

Phillips didn't even look up.

"No."

"Sir—"

"That's an order."

"They're sending more men to die."

"We don't even know if anyone's alive."

Steve's voice hardened.

"Bucky is."

Phillips met his eyes.

"You've trained for three months."

"But you're still not ready to fight Hydra."

Steve clenched his fists.

"Then when will I be?"

"When I say you are."

Silence filled the room.

Steve saluted.

"...Yes, sir."

He turned and walked away.

That evening.

Steve sat alone outside the barracks, watching the American Hero play on the stage far away.

The rain fell softly against the roof above him.

He stared at Bucky's name written on the mission report.

Footsteps approached.

Peggy Carter sat beside him.

A moment later...

Edmund Stark joined them.

For a while...

None of them spoke.

Finally, Peggy broke the silence.

"You're going anyway."

Steve didn't answer.

He didn't have to.

Edmund gave a small smile.

"I thought so."

Steve looked between them.

"I can't leave them there."

Peggy nodded.

"Neither can we."

Edmund unfolded a blueprint of a nearby military airfield.

Edmund, "I can get you there, but you have to make sure you don't get caught."

Steve, "Okay."

At night, around nine o'clock, when everyone felt asleep, the performers where resting, Steve sneaked into the room and took the stage costume.

He wore the costume and hid his face. He did this to avoid being spotted by the authorities and to avoid being caught and then ran to the aircraft where Stark is waiting.

## Chapter Four: The First Mission

Night had fallen over the forests surrounding the Hydra installation.

High above the clouds, a modified military transport aircraft cut silently through the darkness.

At the controls sat Edmund Stark.

His eyes never left the instruments.

Behind him, Steve wears his military gear over the stage costume he stole, removed the mask and wore a helmet.

The bright stars and stripes looked almost ridiculous beneath combat gear.

Edmund glanced back with a smile.

"You know..."

"...history is going to have questions about that outfit."

Steve looked down.

"It was the only disguise I could find."

"I know."

Edmund reached into his coat and handed Steve a folded map.

"Hydra's prison complex is here."

He pointed toward the eastern side of the compound.

"The launch facility is on the opposite end."

Steve memorized every route.

"The extraction point?"

Edmund smiled.

"I'll worry about finding you."

Steve, "You go back to the base, I will return with the soldiers."

Edmund nodded.

The cargo door slowly opened.

Cold air rushed into the aircraft.

Far below...

A sea of pine trees stretched toward the mountain fortress.

Steve looked down.

"No parachute?"

"It'll make you spotted by enemies."

Steve simply nodded.

"Fair enough."

Without another word...

He jumped.

Steve crashed through branches before landing heavily in the snow-covered forest.

The ground beneath his feet cracked.

He stood.

Barely a scratch.

He smiled to himself.

"Good to know."

Ahead...

Hydra's fortress illuminated the mountainside.

Steve disappeared into the trees.

Two guards patrolled the outer fence.

Neither saw the shadow moving above them.

Steve dropped silently between them.

One precise strike to the neck.

Another to the abdomen.

Both collapsed before they could raise an alarm.

Steve caught one rifle before it hit the ground.

No noise.

No wasted movement.

He slipped inside.

The corridors echoed with marching boots.

Steve moved from cover to cover.

A guard rounded the corner.

Steve grabbed him.

Turned.

Elbow.

Disarm.

Unconscious.

Another.

Shield strike.

Sweep.

Wall.

Out cold.

The triangular shield quickly became more than protection.

Every edge.

Every angle.

Every movement flowed naturally.

Hydra soldiers began arriving in greater numbers.

One activated an unfamiliar rifle.

A brilliant blue beam erupted from its barrel.

Steve barely avoided it.

The blast tore through a steel support column as though it were paper.

His eyes widened.

"What kind of weapon..."

More soldiers arrived.

Blue energy illuminated the hallway.

Steve charged.

His enhanced speed blurred past incoming fire.

He drove one soldier through a reinforced door.

Another crashed across an entire room.

A third attempted to strike him with a rifle.

Steve caught it.

Bent the barrel with his bare hands.

Then used the weapon itself to knock two others unconscious.

The energy blasts became increasingly destructive.

One struck his triangular shield directly.

The impact threw Steve backward.

The shield split along one edge.

A jagged crack spread across its surface.

Steve stared at it briefly.

Then kept fighting.

Deep within the fortress...

Heavy prison doors lined a dimly lit corridor.

Steve forced one open.

Inside...

American soldiers looked up in disbelief.

"...Steve?"

Bucky Barnes stepped forward.

"I knew you'd come."
