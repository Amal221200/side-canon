# Marvel Specials – Iron Man

## Prelude

The Stark name had helped shape the modern world.

During the Second World War...

Edmund Stark transformed engineering into a weapon against tyranny.

His inventions protected soldiers.

Saved lives.

And stood beside legends who changed history.

After the war...

His son, Howard Stark, inherited the company.

Where Edmund had built for survival...

Howard built for progress.

Communications.

Energy.

Transportation.

Manufacturing.

Under Howard's leadership, Stark Industries became one of the largest technological companies in the world.

Then...

Everything changed.

A rainy evening.

A winding mountain road.

The wreckage of a black sedan.

The headlines spread across the country the following morning.

**HOWARD STARK AND HIS WIFE KILLED IN AUTOMOBILE ACCIDENT**

Their only son...

Anthony Edward Stark...

Was still a teenager.

Too young to inherit an empire.

Too young to lead.

For the next several years...

The responsibility of Stark Industries fell to Howard's trusted business partner.

Obadiah Stane.

He protected the company.

Expanded it.

Prepared it for the day Howard's son would take control.

And eventually...

That day arrived.

Years later.

Towering above Manhattan...

Stark Industries had become synonymous with innovation.

Satellites.

Medical technology.

Robotics.

Artificial intelligence.

Military engineering.

There wasn't a corner of modern life untouched by Stark technology.

At the center of it all...

Tony Stark.

A genius unlike anyone of his generation.

He could solve engineering problems that entire teams considered impossible.

He could design revolutionary technology overnight.

He could build machines decades ahead of their time.

Unfortunately...

He rarely cared what happened after they left his workshop.

## Chapter One: The Cost of Innovation

A luxurious casino overlooked the Las Vegas skyline.

Cheers erupted as chips scattered across a poker table.

Tony Stark leaned comfortably in his chair.

A glass of expensive whiskey rested in one hand.

The other casually pushed another stack of chips toward the center.

"If I lose..."

He smiled.

"I'll invent something tomorrow."

Laughter erupted around the table.

Across the room...

Television screens displayed breaking news.

**TERRORIST CELL SUSPECTED OF USING ADVANCED WEAPONS**

A reporter held fragments of destroyed military equipment before the camera.

The Stark Industries logo remained clearly visible.

One guest glanced toward Tony.

"You ever worry about things like that?"

Tony barely looked at the screen.

"We sell to governments."

"What happens afterward..."

He shrugged.

"...isn't my department."

The conversation ended as quickly as it began.

Back in Malibu...

Tony entered his mansion, far away from the city.

"Welcome home, sir."

A calm British voice echoed throughout the penthouse.

"Your meeting with the Department of Defense begins in eight hours."

Tony smiled.

"Still trying to make me responsible, Jarvis?"

"I continue to believe it is worth the effort."

Tony chuckled.

"So did the original."

The artificial intelligence wasn't just another computer.

Years earlier...

Tony had designed it in memory of the Stark family's longtime butler.

Edwin Jarvis.

A man who had quietly helped raise him after his parents' death.

"In fairness, sir..."

Jarvis replied,

"...the original had considerably more patience."

Tony laughed.

"I miss him too."

The following morning.

Pepper Potts entered Tony's office carrying three folders, two coffee cups, and tomorrow's schedule.

"You missed four board meetings."

Tony looked up from a holographic engine design.

"Were they important?"

"They became important when the CEO didn't attend."

Tony smiled innocently.

"I knew you'd handle it."

Pepper sighed.

"One of these days..."

"I'm going to stop doing that."

"You've been saying that for five years."

"And one day you'll believe me."

Tony grinned.

"I hope not."

A knock interrupted them.

Colonel James Rhodes stepped inside.

Military uniform.

Perfect posture.

Expression already exhausted.

"Tell me you remembered today's weapons demonstration."

Tony looked toward Pepper.

"Did I remember today's weapons demonstration?"

Pepper handed him another folder.

"No."

"I scheduled it."

Rhodey shook his head.

"How we're still friends..."

"I'll never understand."

Tony walked past him, placing a hand on his shoulder.

"Because somebody has to keep me out of trouble."

Rhodey looked directly at him.

"My concern is that one day..."

"...I won't be able to."

Tony only smiled.

He had heard those words many times before.

He never imagined how soon they would prove true.

Far above the city...

The Stark Industries logo reflected the morning sun from his new Stark Tower at the heart of the city.

To the world...

Tony Stark was the future.

He just hadn't realized...

The future he was building was already coming back to find him.

### Afghanistan

The desert stretched endlessly beneath a cloudless sky.

A convoy of military vehicles stood assembled near a remote testing range.

Engineers hurried between equipment.

Soldiers secured the perimeter.

Journalists prepared their cameras.

At the center of it all...

Tony Stark stood beside Colonel James Rhodes.

Confident.

Relaxed.

Completely in his element.

A sleek missile rested on its launch platform.

Tony admired it with unmistakable pride.

"Gentlemen..."

He spread his arms.

"...allow me to introduce the Jericho."

Military officers exchanged curious glances.

Tony smiled.

"This isn't just another missile."

"It doesn't level a battlefield."

"It chooses one."

He pointed toward the distant mountain range.

"Jarvis."

A calm voice echoed from the portable control station.

"Systems online, sir."

"Trajectory confirmed."

Tony looked toward the assembled officers.

"You might want to cover your ears."

The launch sequence began.

The missile roared into the sky.

For several breathtaking seconds...

Nothing happened.

Then—

The warhead split into dozens of smaller projectiles.

Each one precisely adjusted its course.

A heartbeat later...

The mountains erupted.

Multiple peaks disappeared beneath a wall of fire.

The shockwave reached the observation platform several seconds afterward.

Silence followed.

Then applause.

Military officers exchanged impressed smiles.

Rhodey looked toward Tony.

"You always have to make an entrance."

Tony smirked.

"If you're going to build the future..."

"...you might as well make it memorable."

Several hours later.

The convoy prepared to return.

Military intelligence officers approached Colonel Rhodes.

"Sir."

"We've identified a nearby terrorist encampment."

"A patrol is preparing to investigate."

Rhodey nodded.

"I'll send additional support."

Tony overheard the conversation.

"Terrorists?"

Rhodey immediately looked at him.

"No."

Tony smiled.

"I've never seen my work in the field."

"And you're not starting today."

Tony laughed.

"I'm just observing."

"Tony."

"I'm serious."

"It's dangerous."

Tony casually walked toward one of the armored vehicles.

"So that's why you brought soldiers."

He climbed onto the side of the vehicle.

Rhodey sighed.

"I really don't have time for this."

Tony opened the hatch of a nearby tank.

Rhodey is irritated.

"You are impossible."

Tony grinned.

"I've been called worse."

He disappeared inside.

Rhodey muttered under his breath.

"I know."

The convoy rolled into the desert.

Nearly an hour passed.

The terrain narrowed into a rocky valley.

Everything seemed peaceful.

Then...

The first explosion erupted beneath the lead vehicle.

The convoy stopped instantly.

"Ambush!"

Gunfire echoed from every ridge.

Rocket-propelled grenades streaked through the air.

Stark Industries weapons.

The soldiers returned fire immediately.

Tony climbed halfway out of the tank.

"What the hell is happening?"

Officer's voice crackled over the radio.

"Stay inside!"

Another explosion threw the tank sideways.

Tony crashed against the interior hatch.

The world spun.

Outside...

Chaos.

One missile struck an armored personnel carrier.

Its twisted remains bore a familiar logo.

**Stark Industries.**

Tony froze.

"No..."

More rockets.

More gunfire.

The convoy had been completely surrounded.

A nearby Humvee exploded.

The blast threw Tony from the tank.

He slammed violently against the desert floor.

His vision blurred.

A piece of metal struck his chest.

The pain became unbearable.

The sounds of battle slowly faded.

The last thing Tony saw...

Was a group of armed men approaching him.

One carried a rifle manufactured by Stark Industries.

Everything went dark.

Tony's eyes slowly opened.

Darkness surrounded him.

Pain pulsed through his chest.

He instinctively reached toward the wound.

His fingers touched cold metal.

Embedded in his chest...

A strange circular device glowed faintly beneath a transparent covering.

"What..."

He tried pulling it free.

"Don't."

A calm voice interrupted.

Tony turned.

An older man hurried toward him.

"If you remove it..."

"...you'll die."

Tony stared at him.

"What is this?"

The man carefully helped him sit upright.

"When they brought you here..."

"...your chest was filled with shrapnel."

He pointed toward the device.

"I built an electromagnet."

"It prevents the fragments from entering your heart."

Tony looked down.

"So this thing..."

"...is keeping me alive?"

The man nodded.

"For now."

Tony studied him.

"Who are you?"

The man offered a tired smile.

"My name is Ho Yinsen."

"I am a doctor."

Tony quietly looked around the cave.

"So..."

"We're prisoners."

Yinsen gave a slow nod.

"We are."

Before Tony could ask another question—

The cave entrance burst open.

Armed guards stormed inside.

Their leader barked an order.

The men forced Tony and Yinsen to their feet.

Outside...

The full scale of the camp became visible.

The flag of the Ten Rings. 

Villagers worked under armed supervision.

Children carried heavy crates.

Prisoners dug trenches.

Anyone who resisted was beaten.

Tony watched silently.

Then...

He noticed something that made his blood run cold.

Crates stacked beside a warehouse.

Each one marked with the unmistakable Stark Industries logo.

Jericho missile components.

Assault rifles.

Ammunition.

His weapons.

His inventions.

His name.

For the first time in years...

Tony Stark had no clever remark.

The guards shoved him forward.

They entered a larger cavern.

Unlike the prison cells...

This one had been converted into a machine workshop.

Industrial tools.

Metal presses.

Welding equipment.

Engineering benches.

The terrorist leader slowly approached.

He placed a blueprint onto the workbench.

A Stark Industries weapon.

He looked directly at Tony.

"You are a very valuable man."

A translator stepped forward.

"He says..."

"...from today..."

"...you work for him."

Tony remained silent.

The translator continued.

"You will build these weapons."

He smiled coldly.

"And if you refuse..."

He gestured toward the guards surrounding them.

"...your life belongs to us."

The workshop doors slammed shut.

The locks echoed through the cavern.

For the first time in his life...

Tony Stark understood what it felt like...

To be trapped by something he had created.

## Chapter Two: The Heart of Iron

The workshop remained silent.

Only the distant sound of machinery echoed through the cavern.

Tony sat against the wall, studying the electromagnet embedded in his chest.

Every few seconds...

It pulsed softly.

A reminder that his own heart could betray him at any moment.

Yinsen carefully sorted through a pile of medical supplies.

Tony finally broke the silence.

"I still don't understand."

Yinsen looked up.

"What?"

Tony gestured toward the Stark Industries crates outside.

"We sold those weapons directly to the military."

"They were never supposed to end up here."

Yinsen quietly smiled.

"I once believed the same."

Tony frowned.

"What do you mean?"

"I tried to warn you."

Tony looked surprised.

"You met me?"

"I tried."

Yinsen nodded.

"I requested meetings."

"I wrote letters."

"I contacted your company."

"I wanted to tell you someone inside Stark Industries was diverting your weapons."

Tony immediately looked up.

"And?"

Yinsen's expression remained calm.

"I was never able to reach you."

Tony lowered his eyes.

"You were busy."

He paused.

"And when I finally did see you..."

"...I realized there was no imposter."

"You truly believed none of this was your responsibility."

The words lingered in the air.

Tony couldn't argue.

Because they were true.

Several moments passed before Tony spoke again.

"So..."

"What do we do now?"

Yinsen gave a tired smile.

"We build weapons."

He looked around the workshop.

"They sell them."

"They become stronger."

"We build more."

He shrugged.

"And eventually..."

"...we die."

Tony looked toward the cave entrance.

"There has to be another way."

Yinsen slowly shook his head.

"You still have something to lose."

Tony laughed quietly.

"No."

"I really don't."

"No family."

"No one waiting for me."

"No reason to believe anyone's coming."

Yinsen reached into his pocket.

From inside...

He unfolded a worn photograph.

His wife.

His children.

"My family is still alive."

He smiled sadly.

"At least..."

"I hope they are."

He stared at the picture for several moments before carefully folding it away again.

"I've already accepted..."

"...I'll never see them again."

Tony remained silent.

For the first time in years...

He wished he had a photograph worth carrying.

That night...

Tony paced slowly through the workshop.

Then stopped.

"I know what we're going to do."

Yinsen looked up.

"You have a plan?"

Tony nodded.

"I don't know who's stealing my company's weapons."

"But I know one thing."

"If I get out of here..."

"...I'm going to find them."

He looked directly at Yinsen.

"And I need your help."

Yinsen smiled faintly.

"I was wondering when the engineer would finally appear."

The following morning...

The terrorist leader entered the workshop.

Tony handed him a handwritten list.

The translator studied it.

"Computer."

"Industrial batteries."

"Palladium."

"Copper."

"Steel."

"Fuel."

"Machine tools."

The terrorist frowned.

"Why?"

Tony answered confidently.

"You asked me to build the greatest weapon I've ever made."

He looked around the workshop.

"I can't do that with scraps."

The translator relayed every word.

The leader considered it.

Then nodded.

Within hours...

Everything Tony had requested began arriving.

The terrorists believed they were preparing to build the next Jericho missile.

Tony had something very different in mind.

Days passed.

Tony worked almost without sleep.

Metal sparks filled the cavern.

Yinsen carefully assisted wherever he could.

One evening...

Tony held two circular metal rings connected by fine copper wiring.

He carefully lowered a small piece of refined Palladium into the center.

The machine slowly came to life.

A brilliant blue light filled the workshop.

Yinsen stared in disbelief.

"What is it?"

Tony smiled.

"The future."

He carefully disconnected the crude electromagnet from his chest.

For one terrifying second...

The shrapnel shifted.

Tony placed the glowing device into the opening.

The Arc Reactor activated.

Its magnetic field immediately stabilized the fragments surrounding his heart.

Tony slowly breathed again.

Yinsen looked at the glowing reactor.

"It saved your life."

Tony nodded.

"And now..."

"...it's going to power something much bigger."

The weeks that followed became a blur of steel and fire.

Hydraulic joints.

Servo motors.

Reinforced plating.

Primitive control systems.

Every component was built by hand.

Every mistake meant starting over.

Yinsen watched the growing machine take shape.

"You aren't building a missile."

Tony smiled.

"No."

He looked at the massive steel frame.

"I'm building my way home."

The terrorists grew impatient.

One afternoon...

Several armed men stormed into the workshop.

The unfinished armor stood hidden beneath heavy tarps.

The leader slammed his rifle against the workbench.

The translator stepped forward.

"He wants to know where his weapon is."

Tony didn't flinch.

"You asked for the greatest weapon I've ever created."

He pointed toward the covered structure.

"Great things take time."

The terrorist grabbed Tony by his shirt.

The translator spoke again.

"He says he'll kill both of you if you're lying."

Tony calmly looked him in the eyes.

"If you want your weapon..."

"...then you'll keep us alive."

The terrorist hesitated.

Then released him.

The guards left.

The workshop doors slammed shut once more.

Yinsen exhaled.

"I was certain that wouldn't work."

Tony smiled.

"I wasn't."

Finally...

The last steel plate locked into place.

The armor towered over both men.

Crude.

Heavy.

Unelegant.

Yet unmistakably powerful.

Tony slowly stepped back.

"Mark One."

Yinsen looked up at the machine.

"It doesn't look like a weapon."

Tony smiled.

"It isn't."

He quietly walked to the primitive computer the terrorists had provided.

Despite its age...

It was enough.

He connected it to the armor's design files.

Encrypted every schematic.

Then initiated a secure transmission.

Somewhere far away...

Deep within JARVIS' servers...

The files began uploading.

Yinsen looked toward him.

"You think they'll find it?"

Tony shut down the terminal.

"I don't know."

He looked at the armor one last time.

"But if I don't make it..."

"...someone has to know what really happened here."

The Arc Reactor glowed steadily beneath his chest.

The armor stood ready.

For the first time since entering the cave...

Tony Stark wasn't waiting to survive.

He was preparing to fight.

## Chapter Three: Iron is Forged in Fire

The workshop had never been quieter.

The Mark I armor stood in the center of the cavern.

Rough.

Bulky.

Uneven.

Its steel plates still bore fresh welding marks.

It looked less like a machine...

...and more like a walking tank.

Tony stared at it for several moments.

"Well..."

He smiled faintly.

"Let's hope this actually works."

Yinsen returned the smile.

"I've had less confidence in worse surgeries."

Tony stepped onto the armor's lower frame.

One piece at a time...

The steel shell came together.

Leg assemblies.

Hip joints.

Chest plating.

Shoulder locks.

Heavy gauntlets.

Every section weighed more than a grown man.

Yinsen tightened each locking mechanism by hand.

"Left arm secure."

Another click.

"Right arm secure."

Hydraulic pistons hissed as Tony flexed his fingers.

The armor responded.

Slowly.

But it responded.

Tony took his first step.

The entire workshop shook.

He nearly lost his balance.

"Caution..."

Yinsen smiled.

"You've become considerably heavier."

Tony laughed.

"I noticed."

Finally...

Yinsen lifted the helmet.

The heavy steel shell lowered over Tony's head.

The world became a narrow slit of vision.

He could hear the Arc Reactor humming beneath the armor.

Yinsen walked to the computer.

His fingers hovered above the keyboard.

"Once I begin..."

"...there is no stopping it."

Tony nodded.

"Do it."

Yinsen pressed the key.

**SYSTEM INITIALIZATION**

**30 SECONDS**

The armor remained motionless.

Outside...

The terrorists watched the workshop through a surveillance monitor.

One frowned.

"What are they doing?"

The image showed the enormous steel figure standing silently.

The alarm immediately sounded.

Within seconds...

Armed men sprinted toward the workshop.

**20 SECONDS**

The footsteps grew louder.

Yinsen looked toward the reinforced door.

His smile disappeared.

"They're coming."

Tony strained against the motionless armor.

"It isn't ready."

The computer continued counting.

**18...**

**17...**

Yinsen quietly looked toward Tony.

Then reached for a nearby rifle.

Tony immediately realized what he was about to do.

"No."

Yinsen calmly checked the weapon.

"I'll buy you time."

"You don't have to do this."

Yinsen smiled.

"I know."

Tony struggled against the inactive armor.

"Yinsen!"

The doctor walked to the heavy workshop door.

Before opening it...

He turned back one final time.

"You once told me..."

"...you had nothing waiting for you."

Tony remained silent.

"You do now."

Yinsen unlocked the door.

"I've already lived my life."

"You still have yours."

The door closed behind him.

Then...

He locked it from the outside.

**10 SECONDS**

Gunfire erupted outside.

Yinsen fired only enough to keep the terrorists from advancing.

Not to win.

Only to delay.

The terrorists returned overwhelming fire.

The heavy workshop door shook violently.

Tony pounded against the armor's controls.

"Come on..."

"Come on..."

Another explosion.

The hinges began to bend.

**5...**

**4...**

The terrorists planted explosives.

The steel door bulged inward.

**3...**

**2...**

The blast erupted.

The entire entrance collapsed.

Dust filled the workshop.

The terrorists raised their rifles.

**1...**

**SYSTEM ONLINE**

The Arc Reactor surged.

Power flooded every system.

Tony slowly lifted his head.

Inside the helmet...

His eyes opened.

"My turn."

The first terrorist fired.

The bullets struck Tony's chest harmlessly.

He stepped forward.

A mounted flamethrower erupted from his gauntlet.

A torrent of fire swept through the workshop entrance.

The attackers scattered in panic.

Tony walked directly through the flames.

Another soldier charged.

Tony caught him effortlessly.

The armored fist hurled him across the cavern.

Three more rushed together.

Tony activated the mounted machine gun.

Short controlled bursts.

The room fell silent.

Every movement shook the cave.

The Mark I wasn't elegant.

It was unstoppable.

Tony smashed through walls.

Steel beams bent beneath his weight.

Ten Rings terrorists fled before him.

Outside...

The camp erupted into chaos.

Tony emerged from the workshop like a walking furnace.

The terrorists opened fire from every direction.

The armor absorbed the bullets.

Each impact dented the steel.

Warning lights flashed inside the helmet.

Armor integrity dropping.

Tony kept moving.

He fired another stream of flame across nearby fuel stores.

The resulting explosion consumed half the camp.

Several armored vehicles blocked his path.

Tony raised his left arm.

The improvised missile launcher deployed.

"Let's see if this works."

The missile streaked across the camp.

A deafening explosion destroyed the convoy.

Shockwaves rolled across the valley.

Then...

Tony saw him.

Yinsen.

Lying quietly against a rock.

Tony immediately knelt beside him.

"No."

He removed his helmet.

"Come on."

"We're leaving."

He reached for Yinsen.

"My family..."

Yinsen whispered.

"...is waiting."

Tony, "Yes, I will get you out of here."

Yinsen, "You don't understand, my family is waiting somewhere above, they are waiting for me."

Tony stopped.

He understood.

Yinsen had never been speaking about surviving this cave.

He had been speaking about going home...

To those he had already lost.

Tears filled Tony's eyes.

"It was an honor working with you."

Yinsen smiled weakly.

"You changed your destiny."

He looked toward the glowing Arc Reactor.

"Don't waste..."

"...the life you were given."

His hand slowly slipped away.

The cave became silent.

Tony lowered his head.

"Thank you..."

He quietly stood.

Put on the helmet.

And walked away.

Reinforcements flooded the valley.

Tony ignited the remaining fuel depots with another blast of fire.

The mountain echoed with explosions.

The Mark I reached the edge of a cliff.

He looked toward the endless desert.

"There goes nothing."

The thrusters ignited.

For one glorious moment...

Iron Man flew.

Awkwardly.

Violently.

Completely out of control.

The heavy armor struggled to remain airborne.

Still...

It carried him miles away from the camp.

Then—

Power dropped.

Warning alarms screamed.

The damaged thrusters failed.

Tony plummeted into the desert.

The Mark I crashed across the sand.

Steel plates tore away.

Hydraulic joints shattered.

The armor finally broke apart around him.

Tony crawled from the wreckage.

Bruised.

Exhausted.

Alive.

He looked toward the horizon.

Then began walking.

One step.

Then another.

The desert offered no shortcuts.

No comfort.

Only distance.

Days passed.

The sun burned overhead.

The nights froze.

Still...

Tony kept walking.

Until the distant sound of helicopter blades broke the silence.

Military rescue teams descended onto the sand.

Soldiers rushed toward him.

One familiar face pushed through the crowd.

Rhodey.

He stopped for a brief moment.

Then wrapped Tony in a tight embrace.

"You idiot."

Tony laughed weakly.

"I missed you too."

Rhodey stepped back, looking him over.

"Next time..."

"...listen when I tell you to stay in the convoy."

Tony smiled.

"For the record..."

He looked back toward the endless desert.

"...I think you were right."

For the first time in years...

Colonel James Rhodes heard something he'd never expected.

Tony Stark admitting he had been wrong.

## Chapter Four: A Different Future

The private jet touched down just before sunset.

As the cabin door opened...

The familiar skyline of New York greeted Tony for the first time in months.

He stood there quietly.

Breathing.

Simply grateful to be home.

At the bottom of the stairs...

Pepper Potts waited.

The moment Tony stepped onto the runway...

She walked straight toward him.

Without warning...

She wrapped her arms tightly around him.

Tony blinked.

"...You missed me?"

Pepper pulled away just enough to glare at him.

"You fool."

Tony smiled.

"I'll take that as a yes."

Happy Hogan carried Tony's luggage toward the waiting limousine.

"So..."

Tony asked.

"What's next?"

Pepper immediately handed him a tablet.

"You have a press conference."

Tony sighed.

"Already?"

"Obadiah Stane postponed it until you returned."

She looked directly at him.

"He's expecting you."

Tony nodded.

"Fair enough."

He climbed into the car.

Then looked toward Happy.

"Happy."

"Yes, boss?"

"Can we stop for a cheeseburger?"

Pepper stared at him.

"...You're serious?"

Tony looked genuinely confused, "I haven't had one in months."

Pepper, "The press conference starts in fifteen minutes."

Tony shrugged.

"They'll wait."

Happy smiled, "I know a place."

Pepper sighed, "Of course you do."

### Stark Industries

Reporters packed every seat inside the conference hall.

Camera flashes illuminated the room.

Executives whispered among themselves.

Obadiah Stane stood near the podium.

The moment Tony entered...

The room erupted into applause.

Still holding a half-eaten cheeseburger.

Stane laughed with relief, "There he is."

He embraced Tony tightly, "It's good to have you back."

Tony smiled politely, "Nice seeing you too, Obie."

Moments later...

Tony stepped behind the podium.

The room became silent.

He looked across hundreds of reporters.

Then slowly placed the cheeseburger on the podium.

"For years..."

He began quietly.

"I believed my job ended the moment my inventions left the factory."

He paused.

"I told myself we sold only to governments."

"I convinced myself everything that happened afterward..."

"...was someone else's responsibility."

His eyes drifted across the room.

"Then I saw my own weapons."

"Not in military warehouses."

"Not protecting innocent people."

"I saw them in the hands of terrorists."

"I saw villages destroyed."

"I saw children terrified of the machines carrying my family's name."

Silence filled the hall.

Tony took a slow breath.

"I can't pretend I didn't see that."

"So..."

He looked directly into the cameras.

"As of today..."

"Stark Industries will immediately cease all weapons manufacturing."

Gasps spread through the audience.

Reporters began shouting questions.

"What?"

"Mr. Stark!"

"What happens to your military contracts?"

"Is this temporary?"

Tony continued.

"Our company will no longer profit from destruction."

"Not directly."

"Not indirectly."

"That chapter ends today."

The room exploded into chaos.

Obadiah Stane stood frozen.

Outside...

Stane hurried after Tony.

"What are you thinking?"

Tony stopped beside the limousine.

Stane lowered his voice.

"Weapons manufacturing built this company."

"It pays for everything."

Tony calmly looked at him.

"Then maybe..."

"...it's time this company stood for something else."

He entered the limousine.

Leaving Obadiah speechless.

### Malibu

The Pacific waves crashed gently against the cliffs below.

Tony had barely left his workshop since returning home.

The mansion felt quieter than ever.

Pepper knocked softly, "You have a visitor."

Tony didn't look up, "Send him in."

Colonel James Rhodes entered.

He looked around the workshop.

Blueprints covered every table.

Half-disassembled electronics lay scattered everywhere.

Tony simply nodded.

"Hi."

Rhodey folded his arms.

"Hi?"

## Chapter Five: Learning to Fly

The laboratory lights never turned off.

"Sir."

"You have now worked continuously for one hundred and eighteen hours."

"So?"

"Too heavy."

"Too slow."

"It deserves something better."

"Too heavy."

"Gold?"

"The reactor remains stable."

Silence.

Attempt number two.

"Reduce output."

Five percent.

Twelve.

Weight distribution.

Center of gravity.

Momentum.

Perfectly still.

Several feet above the floor.

"I think we've got it."

JARVIS replied,

"Congratulations, sir."

"You have successfully remained airborne for twelve consecutive seconds."

"Don't undersell me."

The final components arrived.

Titanium alloy plating.

Gold-titanium thermal panels.

Refined servo assemblies.

Tony carefully assembled every section.

Unlike the Mark One...

Elegant.

Powerful.

Its metallic red and gold finish reflected the workshop lights.

Mechanical arms surrounded him.

One by one...

Leg armor.

Gauntlets.

Chest assembly.

Shoulders.

Back plating.

Finally...

The faceplate sealed shut.

Darkness.

Then—

The helmet came alive.

Heads-Up Display online.

Transparent holographic graphics surrounded his vision.

Power output.

Altitude.

Structural integrity.

Environmental analysis.

Everything appeared instantly.

"Good evening, sir."

JARVIS' familiar voice echoed inside the helmet.

Tony smiled.

"That's considerably better."

"Arc Reactor operating at one hundred percent."

"Primary propulsion systems online."

Temperature.

Potential collision risks.

Not against him.

"Weapon systems?"

JARVIS answered.

"Good."

"One step at a time."

The massive workshop doors slowly opened.

Sunlight poured inside.

Beyond them...

The Pacific Ocean stretched toward the horizon.

"JARVIS."

"Ready?"

"Let's see if mankind was ever supposed to fly like this."

Clouds rushed past.

Out of pure joy.

Higher.

Warning alarms.

"Sir."

"I no longer recommend further ascent."

Silence.

"Oh no."

Clouds tore past at terrifying speed.

The suit tumbled uncontrollably.

Altitude dropped faster every second.

"JARVIS!"

"Attempt faster!"

Ten thousand feet.

Five thousand.

One thousand.

Completely silent.

"We finally built a real suit."

JARVIS replied,

"We're getting closer."

"No."

Back assembly.

Power output.

Repulsor systems.

Operational.

Operational.
