## Chapter 1: Life of Illyana Rasputin

The story opens with a peaceful morning on a countryside farm just outside Westchester, New York. Although the Rasputin family has Russian roots, they have lived in America for generations, carrying only their traditions, language and values from their homeland.

Their home is modest but warm, surrounded by acres of farmland, forests and open fields. The family believes in hard work, honesty and staying together.

The family consists of:

- **Piotr Rasputin (14)** – The eldest son. Quiet, humble, intelligent and surprisingly mature for his age. Despite his large physique, he has a gentle personality and spends most of his free time drawing landscapes, animals and portraits. Unknown to most people, Piotr is already a mutant capable of transforming into organic steel. He carefully hides his abilities from the outside world and dreams of attending Charles Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters after finishing middle school, believing it will finally allow him to live without fear of hiding who he is.

- **Illyana Rasputin (8)** – Piotr's younger sister. Loud, energetic, fearless and unbelievably competitive. She excels at every sport in school and is easily one of the most popular children in her class. She constantly tries to prove she can do everything better than her older brother, partly because everyone admires Piotr. She acts as though she doesn't care about him, often teasing or arguing with him, but secretly looks up to him more than anyone.

- **Nikolai Rasputin** – Their hardworking father who manages the farm. A firm but loving man who believes every problem can be solved with patience and hard work.

- **Alexandra Rasputin** – Their caring mother who holds the family together. She worries constantly about her children but always hides it behind a smile.

- **Mikhail Rasputin** – Their grandfather. A retired farmer in his late seventies who still insists on helping around the farm despite everyone telling him to rest. He loves telling stories about Russia, family traditions and life lessons, while secretly spoiling Illyana whenever nobody is looking.

---

### Everyday Life

The morning begins peacefully.

Grandfather Mikhail is already outside feeding the chickens before sunrise.

Piotr sits on the porch quietly sketching the sunrise over the fields in his notebook.

Illyana suddenly runs past him.

> "Race you to the barn!"

Without waiting for an answer, she sprints away.

Piotr smiles.

> "That's not how races work."

She turns around while still running.

> "Scared you'll lose?"

Piotr sighs before closing his sketchbook.

> "You're impossible."

The two race across the fields.

Illyana is faster.

Piotr could easily catch her using his enhanced strength, but deliberately slows himself down.

She reaches the barn first and raises both arms victoriously.

> "I WIN!"

Grandfather Mikhail laughs loudly.

> "Of course you won. Your brother let you."

Illyana immediately protests.

> "He did not!"

Piotr simply smiles.

---

Later, Piotr quietly helps his father move an impossibly heavy tractor wheel behind the barn.

When nobody is watching, his skin briefly transforms into shining organic steel.

He effortlessly lifts the wheel into place.

Before anyone else notices, he returns to normal.

Only Illyana catches a glimpse.

She crosses her arms.

> "You cheated."

Piotr looks around nervously.

> "Keep it between us."

She grins.

> "Only if you let me win the next race."

---

### Dinner Conversation

That evening the whole family gathers around the dinner table.

Grandfather tells exaggerated stories about wrestling bears back in Russia.

Illyana immediately interrupts.

> "You're making that up."

Grandfather places a hand dramatically on his chest.

> "You wound me."

Everyone laughs.

The conversation eventually turns toward Piotr's future.

His father asks,

> "Have you thought about what comes after school?"

Piotr hesitates.

Then answers honestly.

> "I want to apply to Xavier's School."

The room becomes quiet.

His parents already know about his mutation.

His father nods slowly.

> "You shouldn't have to hide forever."

His mother smiles proudly.

> "We'll support whatever path you choose."

Illyana rolls her eyes dramatically.

> "Your weird mutant school sounds boring."

Piotr chuckles.

> "Maybe you'll come one day."

She immediately points a finger at him.

> "Never."

Grandfather laughs.

> "Give her a few years."

Nobody realizes how true those words will become.

---

### The Awakening

Later that evening everyone begins preparing dinner together.

Piotr chops vegetables.

His mother stirs soup.

His father sets the table.

Grandfather complains that nobody lets him help anymore.

Illyana laughs while stealing pieces of bread from the counter.

Then...

She suddenly freezes.

Her smile disappears.

She grabs her head.

A sharp pain shoots through her body.

She falls to her knees.

Everyone immediately rushes toward her.

"Illyana!"

She screams.

The lights throughout the house begin flickering.

Kitchen utensils shake violently.

Windows rattle.

A strange golden energy begins surrounding her body.

Her eyes suddenly glow with brilliant arcane light.

Nobody understands what they're witnessing.

Ancient glowing symbols briefly appear beneath her feet.

Then—

A circular stepping disc silently opens beneath her.

Before anyone can reach her—

She falls through it.

The portal instantly closes.

Silence.

---

### Thirty Minutes

Nobody speaks.

Piotr stares at the empty floor.

His mother collapses into tears.

His father desperately searches every room in the house despite knowing she cannot possibly be there.

Grandfather repeatedly whispers prayers in Russian.

The police are called.

Emergency services arrive.

Nobody believes what the family describes.

How do you explain that your daughter disappeared into a glowing circle in the kitchen?

Piotr blames himself.

He keeps replaying the moment in his head.

If he had moved faster...

Maybe he could have caught her.

Thirty minutes pass.

It feels like an eternity.

---

### The Return

Without warning—

Another stepping disc opens in the middle of the kitchen.

Something falls through it.

The family rushes forward.

"Illyana!"

She slowly pushes herself off the floor.

Everyone stops.

She...

isn't eight years old anymore.

She now appears nearly the same age as Piotr.

Her once cheerful face has become hardened.

Her body is noticeably stronger and more athletic, as though she has spent years training.

Her clothes are unfamiliar.

Covered in dirt.

Torn.

Marked by countless battles.

There are old scars across her arms.

Her breathing is heavy.

Her eyes no longer belong to a child.

Piotr slowly approaches.

"...Yana?"

She instinctively reaches toward her waist—

As though expecting a sword that is no longer there.

Her entire body immediately shifts into a defensive fighting stance.

Ready for combat.

Only after several seconds does she realize...

There are no demons.

No monsters.

No battlefield.

She is home.

She looks at Piotr.

Recognition slowly fills her eyes.

Her lips tremble.

"...Piotr?"

Before anyone can say another word—

She collapses into his arms.

The entire family stands frozen.

Only thirty minutes have passed for them.

For Illyana...

Several years have passed.

Their little girl is finally home.

But she is no longer the same person who disappeared.

## Chapter 2: Life of Elizabeth Braddock

While the Rasputins lived a quiet life in the countryside of America, thousands of miles away, another family lived a life of privilege in the English countryside.

The Braddocks were one of Britain's most respected and influential families. Their wealth had been built over generations, but unlike many aristocratic families, they valued freedom more than tradition.

Their estate overlooked rolling green hills, private gardens and a small lake where the children spent much of their free time.

The Braddock family consisted of:

- **Jamie Braddock (22)** – The eldest brother. Charismatic, fearless and adventurous. One of Britain's rising professional racing drivers, constantly travelling around the world. Betsy admired him more than anyone.

- **Elizabeth "Betsy" Braddock (15)** – Intelligent, athletic and independent. Although naturally gifted in academics, she had little interest in following a traditional career. Inspired by Jamie, she dreamed of travelling the world, living an adventurous life beyond classrooms and offices. She enjoyed drawing, video games, anime, gymnastics and spending time outdoors.

- **Brian Braddock (15)** – Betsy's twin brother. Calm, thoughtful and highly academic. Unlike Betsy, Brian loved science, history and engineering, hoping to pursue higher education one day. Despite their different personalities, the twins shared an unbreakable bond.

- **Sir James Braddock** – Their father. A successful businessman who believed education opened every door. While he appreciated Betsy's adventurous spirit, he quietly hoped she would eventually choose university.

- **Lady Elizabeth Braddock** – Their mother. Warm, supportive and encouraging. She believed both twins should follow whichever path made them happiest.

---

### Twin Differences

One afternoon, the twins sat together in the family library.

Brian was buried beneath stacks of books preparing for an upcoming science competition.

Across the room, Betsy balanced effortlessly across the backrest of a sofa while sketching ideas into her notebook.

Brian looked up.

> "How do you study upside down?"

Betsy shrugged.

> "Because sitting normally is boring."

Brian sighed.

> "Everything is boring according to you."

She grinned.

> "Exactly."

---

Later that afternoon, Jamie returned home after finishing preparations for an international racing event.

Before he could even step out of his car—

Betsy sprinted outside.

"You won again?"

Jamie laughed.

"I haven't even raced yet."

She looked disappointed.

"Oh..."

He handed her a racing pass.

"You'll watch me win this weekend instead."

Her eyes immediately lit up.

---

### Family Dinner

That evening the Braddocks gathered for dinner.

Jamie excitedly explained the upcoming race being held in America.

Brian immediately became interested in visiting universities while they were there.

Their father smiled.

"There'll be plenty of time for sightseeing."

Jamie looked toward Betsy.

"And you'll finally get to see a proper pit lane."

She nearly jumped out of her chair.

"I've waited years for this!"

Brian smirked.

"You know they're not going to let you drive."

She kicked him gently beneath the table.

"I know."

"...Yet."

Everyone laughed.

Their mother watched the twins with a smile.

Two children.

Born on the same day.

Yet completely different dreams.

---

### Across the Atlantic

Several days later, the Braddock family's private jet crossed the Atlantic Ocean.

Jamie had already travelled ahead with his racing team.

The rest of the family planned to meet him in America before the weekend.

Inside the cabin...

Brian read another engineering book.

Their father reviewed business documents.

Their mother admired the clouds outside.

Betsy sat by the window wearing headphones while sketching racing cars in her notebook.

Occasionally she looked outside.

America.

She had always wanted to visit.

---

### Something Goes Wrong

Without warning—

The aircraft violently shook.

The lights flickered.

A warning alarm echoed throughout the cabin.

The pilots immediately began speaking over the intercom.

"We're experiencing an unexpected systems failure."

The aircraft lurched again.

Harder this time.

Brian grabbed his seat.

Their mother reached for both twins.

Their father looked toward the cockpit.

Outside the windows...

One engine erupted into flames.

The aircraft rapidly began losing altitude.

Panic filled the cabin.

Masks deployed.

Loose luggage flew across the interior.

The pilots desperately fought for control.

The plane continued falling.

---

### Awakening

Betsy closed her eyes.

She thought...

This was the end.

Something inside her awakened.

An overwhelming psychic pulse exploded from her mind.

Thousands of kilometres away—

At Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters...

Professor Charles Xavier suddenly stopped speaking mid-conversation.

His expression changed instantly.

Jean Grey froze.

She had felt it too.

A psychic scream.

Powerful.

Terrified.

Unlike anything either of them had ever experienced.

Jean rushed into Cerebro's monitoring room.

Hank McCoy immediately followed.

Storm and Professor Xavier entered moments later.

Jean suddenly gasped.

"Charles..."

Professor Xavier immediately felt it as well.

"A new mutant."

Hank activated Cerebro.

The psychic signature was unlike anything they had recorded before.

Cerebro slowly narrowed the search.

Location acquired.

Northern Canada.

Without hesitation, Charles gave the order.

"Prepare the Blackbird."

Scott Summers.

Jean Grey.

Ororo Munroe.

The three departed immediately.

### The Crash Site

The Blackbird crossed the Canadian border at supersonic speed, following Cerebro's coordinates deep into the Canadian Rockies.

Towering snow-capped mountains stretched endlessly across the horizon.

Dense pine forests covered the valleys below, broken only by steep cliffs, frozen streams and rocky terrain.

Smoke rose from a mountainside several kilometres ahead.

The jet slowly descended.

The private aircraft had clipped the side of a mountain before tumbling down the slope, tearing through hundreds of pine trees before finally coming to rest in a narrow valley below.

Debris was scattered across the mountainside for nearly a kilometre.

The remoteness of the region meant emergency services had not yet arrived.

Only the wind echoed through the valley.

The silence was haunting.

Scott cautiously searched the wreckage.

Storm helped clear debris.

Jean used her telepathy to search for survivors.

Nothing.

One by one...

Bodies were discovered.

The pilot.

Crew members.

Passengers.

No survivors.

Until...

Jean suddenly stopped.

"...Wait."

She felt something.

Weak.

But alive.

The three rushed toward the centre of the wreckage.

There...

They found a teenage girl lying unconscious.

Not a single visible injury.

Around her...

The aircraft had been completely crushed.

Twisted steel.

Broken glass.

Burned debris.

Everything had been violently pushed away from her body, forming a perfectly circular space around her, as though an invisible force had protected her during the impact.

Jean slowly knelt beside her.

"She's alive."

Scott looked around in disbelief.

"How is that even possible?"

Jean gently reached toward the girl's mind.

The moment their minds briefly connected—

She was overwhelmed by flashes.

The aircraft.

The screaming.

The explosion.

The fear.

Then...

Nothing.

Jean immediately pulled away.

Her breathing became heavy.

Professor Xavier's voice came through the communicator.

"Jean?"

She looked down at the unconscious girl.

"...She's a telepath."

Professor Xavier remained silent for a moment.

Then calmly replied,

"Bring her home."

As the X-Men carefully lifted the unconscious survivor into the Blackbird...

None of them realized they had just rescued the future Psylocke.

## Chapter 3: A New Beginning

The X-Mansion's medical laboratory remained quiet.

The only sounds were the rhythmic beeping of medical monitors and the soft hum of Hank McCoy's equipment.

Betsy lay unconscious on the medical bed.

Not a single visible injury could be found on her body.

Hank carefully reviewed the latest scans before turning toward Professor Xavier.

Jean, Scott and Ororo waited silently.

Hank finally spoke.

"Physically..."

He looked back at the monitor.

"...she's remarkably healthy."

Scott frowned.

"After surviving a plane crash?"

Hank nodded.

"Her telekinetic abilities appear to have manifested instinctively. They created some form of psychic barrier around her before impact."

He paused.

"That barrier most likely saved her life."

Scott glanced toward Betsy.

"So she'll recover?"

Hank hesitated.

"Her body will."

The room grew quieter.

"The mind is another matter."

Jean lowered her eyes.

"The awakening happened at the exact moment she experienced extreme trauma."

Hank nodded.

"It's impossible to predict the psychological consequences."

Scott asked,

"What kind of consequences?"

Hank folded his arms.

"Amnesia."

He continued.

"Post-traumatic stress."

He looked toward Betsy.

"Difficulty controlling her powers."

"Emotional instability."

"There are countless possibilities."

Professor Xavier calmly looked at the unconscious girl.

"Only time will tell."

---

Outside the medical wing...

Jamie Braddock sat alone.

Still wearing the same clothes from the race.

His helmet rested beside him.

He hadn't slept.

Every passing minute felt longer than the last.

The door quietly opened.

Professor Xavier wheeled himself into the hallway.

Jamie immediately stood.

"Professor..."

Charles offered a gentle smile.

"You must be Jamie Braddock."

Jamie nodded.

"I came as soon as I heard."

Charles extended his hand.

"I'm sorry we had to meet under such circumstances."

Jamie looked past him toward the medical room.

"How is Betsy?"

Charles answered honestly.

"It's too soon to know."

"She's still unconscious."

"But she's stable."

"And she's receiving the best care we can provide."

Jamie closed his eyes for a moment.

A deep breath escaped him.

"Thank God..."

For the first time since the accident...

There was a little relief.

Charles remained silent for a moment before continuing.

"There is something else you should know."

Jamie's expression became serious.

"During the crash..."

"...Elizabeth's mutant abilities awakened."

Jamie didn't look surprised.

Charles noticed.

"You know about mutants."

Jamie nodded slowly.

"I've followed your work for years."

Charles smiled faintly.

"Then you understand what happened."

Jamie looked toward Betsy's room.

"Those powers..."

"They're why she survived."

Charles nodded.

"They protected her instinctively."

Jamie stood quietly for several moments.

He wasn't afraid.

He wasn't angry.

He was simply grateful.

"When can I see her?"

Charles looked through the small window into the medical room.

"As soon as she wakes."

Jamie's eyes never left his sister.

"I'll wait."

Charles smiled gently.

"I had a feeling you would."

The two men stood together in silence.

Watching over the only surviving member of the Braddock family.

---

Several days passed.

The unconscious girl remained peacefully asleep.

Every day Hank checked her physical condition.

Every day Jean quietly sat beside her, occasionally reading a book while monitoring her psychic activity.

The nightmares gradually became less frequent.

Then...

One morning...

Betsy's fingers twitched.

Jean immediately noticed.

She gently put her book down.

The girl's eyes slowly opened.

Bright white lights greeted her.

Confused...

She instinctively tried to sit up.

Jean immediately placed a gentle hand on her shoulder.

"Easy..."

"You're safe."

Betsy looked around the unfamiliar room.

"...Where am I?"

"You're at Xavier's School."

"My name is Jean."

"You were involved in an accident."

Jean spoke softly.

"Can you tell me your name?"

Silence.

Betsy searched her mind.

Nothing.

"I..."

She closed her eyes, trying harder.

Nothing.

"I'm sorry..."

"I don't know."

Jean quietly noted the response.

"That's alright."

"Do you remember your parents?"

Betsy slowly shook her head.

"Your brothers?"

Another shake.

"Do you remember the accident?"

Nothing.

"Your home?"

Nothing.

Every answer was the same.

Nothing.

She smiled warmly.

"You don't need to force yourself."

"Memories don't always return immediately."

"For now..."

"Just focus on getting better."

Betsy looked at her nervously.

"Will I remember again?"

Jean answered honestly.

"I don't know."

"But whatever happens..."

"You won't face it alone."

For the first time since waking...

Betsy felt a little calmer.

---

The following afternoon...

Jean walked beside Betsy through one of the mansion's gardens.

The fresh air was comforting.

Birds sang from nearby trees.

For a brief moment...

Everything felt peaceful.

Near the entrance stood Professor Xavier.

Beside him...

Three people waited.

Jamie Braddock.

His uncle.

His aunt.

The moment Jamie saw Betsy...

He smiled.

He slowly walked forward before lowering himself to one knee so they were at eye level.

"Hey, Angel."

"Do you remember me?"

Betsy instinctively stepped behind Jean.

She peeked around her shoulder.

Then quietly shook her head.

Jamie's smile didn't disappear.

"That's alright."

"We'll catch up another time."

He slowly stood and took a respectful step back.

He wasn't disappointed.

He wasn't angry.

He simply accepted where Betsy was.

Storm gently approached.

"Come with me."

"I'll show you the gardens."

Betsy looked toward Jean.

Jean nodded reassuringly.

"You'll be alright."

Storm and Betsy quietly walked away.

---

Inside Professor Xavier's office...

The atmosphere became considerably heavier.

Charles folded his hands.

"I wanted to discuss Elizabeth's future."

Jean placed her notes on the table.

"Her memory loss appears extensive."

"It isn't selective."

"It affects nearly every aspect of her identity."

Her aunt quietly asked,

"So... what happens if we take her home?"

Jean answered carefully.

"At this stage..."

"Returning her to an unfamiliar environment while expecting her to remember could place enormous psychological stress on her."

"It could worsen her trauma."

"It may even trigger uncontrolled psychic episodes."

Silence filled the room.

Her uncle looked down.

"So you're saying..."

"Our home isn't really her home anymore."

Jean lowered her eyes.

"At least not to her."

Charles gently continued.

"I understand how difficult this is."

"No family wants to leave their child with strangers."

"But Elizabeth isn't only recovering from a tragedy."

"She has awakened extraordinary abilities."

"They require guidance."

"And more importantly..."

"So does she."

Her aunt wiped away a tear.

"We don't want her to think we abandoned her."

Charles answered immediately.

"She never will."

"We'll make sure she knows she still has a family."

Jamie's voice finally broke the silence.

"I'll stay."

Everyone looked toward him.

"I'll visit her."

"As often as I can."

He looked at his uncle and aunt.

"You both have enough to deal with back home."

"The estate."

"The funeral."

"Everything else."

"I'll make sure she never feels alone."

His aunt reached over and held his hand.

"You've always looked after your brother and sister."

She smiled sadly.

"And now you're trying to look after all of us."

Jamie simply nodded.

Charles finally spoke.

"If you allow it..."

"Elizabeth can remain here."

"She'll continue her recovery."

"Receive an education."

"And learn to control her gifts."

Her uncle looked toward his wife.

Then toward Jamie.

Finally...

Toward Professor Xavier.

After a long silence...

He slowly nodded.

"If Elizabeth truly feels safe here..."

"Then this is where she should stay."

Charles smiled warmly.

"You have my word."

"We'll take good care of her."

Her aunt asked one final question.

"And her education?"

Charles smiled.

"She'll be enrolled with our second-year high school students."

"When she's ready..."

"She'll begin again."

Outside...

Storm showed Betsy the grounds.

Children laughed in the distance.

For the first time since waking...

The mansion didn't feel like a hospital.

It felt...

Like the beginning of a new life.

Bookshelves lined one wall.

A large window overlooked the gardens.

"You'll begin classes tomorrow."

Betsy looked slightly nervous.

"Already?"

Jean nodded.

"You'll be sharing this room with another student."

"Her name is Illyana."

"If you need anything..."

"My office is always open."

"Thank you... Miss Grey."

Anything.

Neither knew the other carried scars just as deep.

Uncontrolled.

Unconscious.

"We've got another class coming in."

Students began collecting their bags.

The gym slowly emptied.

Then...

Betsy looked toward the gymnastics floor.

Toward the balance beam.

Toward the vault.

Something stirred inside her.

Not a memory.

Not a thought.

An instinct.

Almost without realizing it...

She began walking.

Ann noticed.

"Betsy?"

The students turned.

Betsy walked to the starting line.

She looked at the apparatus ahead of her.

Confused.

Almost as if her body already knew what to do...

...while her mind didn't.

Illyana quietly watched from across the room.

Curiosity flickered across her face.

*What's she doing?*

Betsy inhaled.

Then moved.

She sprinted.

Jumped.

Her body exploded into motion.

A perfectly controlled roundoff.

Back handspring.

Double layout.

She landed.

Didn't stop.

She transitioned onto the balance beam.

Every step impossibly precise.

Every spin effortless.

Every flip connected seamlessly to the next.

She vaulted.

Twisted.

Rotated through the air with breathtaking control.

Years of professional training...

Hidden somewhere inside her.

Despite months of inactivity...

Despite the rust...

Her body remembered.

She finished with one final twisting dismount.

Landing perfectly.

Silence.

No one spoke.

Even Ann stared in disbelief.

"...My..."

"...God."

Betsy slowly straightened herself.

She looked just as surprised as everyone else.

"I..."

"I didn't..."

A single clap echoed through the gym.

Everyone looked toward Warren.

He smiled.

"That..."

"...was incredible."

Another clap followed.

Then another.

Soon...

The entire class applauded.

Jubilee's eyes were wide.

"Where was *that* yesterday?"

Kitty laughed in amazement.

"I thought she said she couldn't control her powers."

"That wasn't powers."

"That was talent."

Across the room...

Illyana remained silent.

She didn't clap.

She simply watched Betsy.

Really watched her.

The precision.

The balance.

The discipline.

For the first time...

Illyana looked genuinely impressed.

Then—

Betsy's smile disappeared.

The applause became distant.

Blurred.

A ringing filled her ears.

The gymnasium spun.

Images flashed across her mind.

A little girl practicing.

A coach.

National competitions.

Endless hours inside a gym.

Then...

The plane.

The screaming.

The crash.

Past and present collided together.

Her knees buckled.

She collapsed onto the mat.

"Betsy!"

Ann was already running.

The applause stopped instantly.

Students rushed forward.

Ann knelt beside her.

"Betsy!"

There was no response.

She checked her pulse.

Still breathing.

"Someone get a stretcher!"

"I'm on it!"

Warren immediately sprinted toward the medical wing.

Bobby followed close behind.

The remaining students stood frozen.

No one knew what to do.

No one spoke.

Among them...

Illyana remained completely still.

Her eyes never left Betsy.

She remembered the previous night.

The frightened girl who had accidentally wandered into her memories.

Now...

Watching Betsy lying unconscious on the gym floor...

For the first time...

Illyana wondered if Betsy was carrying wounds every bit as deep as her own.

## Chapter 8: Remembering

Darkness.

Silence.

A distant rhythmic beep echoed through the room.

Betsy slowly opened her eyes.

The ceiling above her was unfamiliar.

White lights.

Medical equipment.

The infirmary.

She blinked several times before turning her head.

Jean Grey sat quietly beside the bed, reading a medical chart.

Behind her, Jamie Braddock stood near a monitor, anxiously watching the readings.

Betsy's lips barely moved.

"...Jamie."

The room froze.

Jamie slowly turned around.

His eyes widened.

"Betsy?"

She looked at him.

For the first time...

She truly recognized him.

"...Jamie."

He rushed to her bedside.

"Oh my God..."

He laughed through tears.

"You remember me."

"You actually remember me."

Betsy's eyes filled with tears.

"I remember..."

She began breathing faster.

"I remember Mom..."

"I remember Dad..."

"...Brian..."

The flood of memories overwhelmed her.

She broke down crying.

Jamie immediately wrapped his arms around her.

"Hey..."

"Hey..."

"I know."

"I know."

"It's alright."

"You've been through so much."

"You don't have to carry it alone anymore."

He gently held her hand.

"I'm not going anywhere."

"I'm here."

Jean quietly smiled.

Without disturbing the moment, she stood.

"I'll be right back."

She quietly left the room.

---

Jean hurried through the mansion until she found Hank inside his laboratory.

"Hank!"

He immediately looked up.

"What happened?"

Jean smiled.

"She remembers."

Hank blinked.

"What?"

"Her memories."

"They're back."

He stared at her for a moment before slowly smiling.

"So..."

"The gymnastics routine wasn't just muscle memory."

Jean nodded.

"It triggered something."

Hank leaned back in thought.

"Remarkable..."

"The brain sometimes stores procedural memory separately from autobiographical memory."

"So reconnecting with one..."

"...helped restore the other."

Jean smiled.

"I thought you'd enjoy the science."

"Oh, I do."

Hank laughed.

"I very much do."

---

A few hours later...

Jamie and Betsy entered Professor Xavier's office.

Charles greeted them with his usual warm smile.

"I'm very pleased to see you awake, Elizabeth."

Betsy smiled.

"Thank you, Professor."

Charles folded his hands.

"Jean tells me you've experienced quite a breakthrough."

"You managed to recover your memories..."

"...under rather unusual circumstances."

Jamie chuckled.

"I know."

He looked proudly at his sister.

"Turns out she's an incredible gymnast."

Charles smiled.

"I never doubted that."

A brief silence followed.

"But..."

"That isn't why I asked you both here."

Jamie became attentive.

Charles continued.

"Elizabeth came here to understand her mutation."

"Now that she has recovered much of her memory..."

"I believe the decision should belong to the two of you."

He looked toward Jamie.

"Would you like Betsy to remain at Xavier's School?"

Jamie didn't answer immediately.

Instead...

He looked toward his sister.

"What do you want?"

Betsy didn't hesitate.

"I want to stay."

Jamie listened quietly.

"I've already learned things here that I never could have understood on my own."

She smiled softly.

"I've made friends."

Her thoughts briefly drifted to Warren.

"And..."

She looked down at her hands.

"I want to learn how to control my powers."

"So I don't hurt anyone again."

Silence filled the office.

Jamie looked at his sister for several long seconds.

Then he smiled.

"I trust you."

He turned toward Charles.

"And I trust you."

"If this is where she wants to be..."

"...then she has my blessing."

Charles smiled warmly.

"I'm honored by your confidence."

Jamie extended his hand.

Charles accepted it.

"Welcome to Xavier's School, Miss Braddock."

Betsy smiled.

For the first time since arriving...

It finally felt like home.

---

Outside the office...

A figure stood silently around the corner.

Illyana.

She hadn't intended to eavesdrop.

But once she'd heard Betsy's voice...

She hadn't walked away.

She heard everything.

The plane crash.

The lost memories.

The fear of hurting others.

The decision to stay.

The hallway fell silent once the office door closed.

Illyana slowly looked down at her own hand.

A faint purple glow shimmered across her fingertips.

Arcane energy.

Just enough to remind her it was there.

She stared at it for a long moment.

Then quietly closed her hand into a fist.

The magic disappeared.

Without saying a word...

She turned...

And walked away.

### Danger room

The following morning...

The Danger Room was quiet once again.

Standing inside were only four people.

Scott Summers.

Jean Grey.

Dr. Hank McCoy.

And Betsy Braddock.

The same pedestal.

The same apple.

The same distance.

Nothing had changed.

Except Betsy.

Hank carefully adjusted the monitoring helmet.

"Baseline activity is normal."

He looked toward Jean.

"Whenever you're ready."

Jean smiled.

"No pressure."

"Just breathe."

Betsy nodded.

She closed her eyes.

A slow breath in.

A slow breath out.

Then she opened them again.

She raised one hand.

The room remained perfectly silent.

Hank watched the readings.

"Stable..."

The apple trembled.

Unlike before...

There was no pain.

No screaming.

No memories forcing their way to the surface.

The apple slowly lifted from the pedestal.

Jean couldn't hide her smile.

"That's it..."

"Don't rush."

Betsy concentrated.

The apple floated steadily through the air.

Every movement was smooth.

Controlled.

Confident.

Hank's eyes moved rapidly across the monitors.

"No abnormal neural activity."

"Everything is stable."

The apple drifted closer.

Closer.

Until...

It gently settled into Betsy's open hand.

Silence.

Betsy stared at the apple.

Almost unable to believe it.

"...I did it."

Jean laughed.

"You did."

The helmet came off.

The moment it did...

Betsy couldn't contain herself anymore.

She ran straight toward Jean.

Jean barely had time to react before Betsy threw her arms around her.

"I did it!"

Jean hugged her back, laughing.

"I know."

"I knew you could."

Across the room...

Hank looked down at his tablet with quiet satisfaction.

"The mental block has significantly diminished."

Scott folded his arms.

He looked at Betsy for a moment before speaking.

"Well done."

Betsy immediately stood a little straighter.

"But..."

Scott continued,

"...you still have a long way to go."

Her smile never disappeared.

She nodded.

But for now, it was enough.

Outside the observation window...

Professor Xavier quietly watched without interrupting.

A gentle smile spread across his face.

Not because Elizabeth Braddock had moved an apple.

But because...

For the first time since arriving at Xavier's School...

She believed she could move mountains.
